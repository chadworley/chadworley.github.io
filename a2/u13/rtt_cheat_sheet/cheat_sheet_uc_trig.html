Unit-Circle Trigonometry Cheat Sheet

Definitions

\[\begin{align*} \sin(\theta) &= y & \cos(\theta) &= x & \tan(\theta) &= \frac{y}{x}=\frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)}=\text{slope} \end{align*}\]

Pythagorean Identities

\[\begin{align*} \sin^2(\theta)+\cos^2(\theta)&=1 & |\sin(\theta)|&=\sqrt{1-\cos^2(\theta)} & |\cos(\theta)|&=\sqrt{1-\sin^2(\theta)}\\\\ \tan^2(\theta)+1&=\frac{1}{\cos^2(\theta)} & |\tan(\theta)|&=\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos^2(\theta)}{\cos^2(\theta)}} & |\cos(\theta)|&=\sqrt{\frac{1}{\tan^2(\theta)+1}}\\\\ \tan^2(\theta)+1&=\frac{1}{1-\sin^2(\theta)} & |\tan(\theta)|&=\sqrt{\frac{\sin^2(\theta)}{1-\sin^2(\theta)}} & |\sin(\theta)|&=\sqrt{\frac{\tan^2(\theta)}{\tan^2(\theta)+1}}\\\\ \end{align*}\]

Polar Coordinates

\[\begin{align*} x &= r\cdot\cos(\theta) & r &= \sqrt{x^2+y^2} \\ y &=r\cdot\sin(\theta) & \theta&=\arctan(y,x) \end{align*}\]

Special angles

  • Draw an isosceles right triangle with a hypotenuse of length 1 and leg length of \(x\). Solve \(x^2+x^2=1^2\) to prove length ratios of \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}:\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}:1\) for the \(45^{\circ}-45^{\circ}-90^{\circ}\) triangle.
  • Draw an equilateral triangle, and cut it in half to produce a right triangle with a hypotenuse of length 1, a leg of length 1/2, and another leg of length \(x\). Solve \(x^2+\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2=1^2\) to prove length ratios of \(\frac{1}{2}:\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}:1\) for the \(30^{\circ}-60^{\circ}-90^{\circ}\) triangle..
  • See the right-triangle cheat sheet for diagrams.
  • Use symmetry of the unit circle to determine all coordinates shown below.

So, for example:

\[\begin{align*} \sin\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)&=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & \cos\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)&=\frac{-1}{2} & \tan\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)&=\frac{\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)}{\left(\frac{-1}{2}\right)}=-\sqrt{3} \end{align*}\]
