  1. Question

    Given:

    \[\tan(\theta)~=~-0.672\] \[\theta\text{ is in quadrant II}\]

    Find \(\cos(\theta)\). The tolerance is \(\pm 0.001\).


    Solution

    Remember, \(\tan(\theta)\) represents the slope of the segment connecting \((0,0)\) to \((\cos(\theta),\sin(\theta))\). Draw a sketch. We know the point of interest is in quadrant II. Draw the unit circle centered at the origin and the line \(y=-0.672x\).


    You can draw a right triangle. The standard way to do this is by using a segment along the \(x\)-axis and a vertical segment from the \(x\)-axis to the point. In order for the slope to equal \(\frac{y}{x}\), we can enforce that \(|y|=|-0.672x|\). Notice I am labeling the lengths with positive numbers; I will take care of signs (whether \(x\) or \(y\) is positive or negative), after using Pythagorean Theorem, by considering the quadrant.


    Use Pythagorean Theorem.

    \[x^2+(0.672x)^2~=~1^2\]

    Distribute the power of 2 across the two factors in the parentheses. Also, \(1^2=1\).

    \[x^2+0.451584x^2~=~1\]

    Combine like terms. \(1x^2+0.451584x^2\equiv (1+0.451584)x^2 \equiv 1.451584x^2\)

    \[1.451584x^2~=~1\]

    Divide both sides by 1.451584.

    \[x^2~=~\frac{1}{1.451584}\]

    Square root both sides.

    \[|x|~=~ \sqrt{\frac{1}{1.451584}}\]

    We know the quadrant is quadrant II, so \(x\) is negative.

    \[x~=~ -0.8300016\]

    So,

    \[\cos(\theta)~=~-0.8300016\]
