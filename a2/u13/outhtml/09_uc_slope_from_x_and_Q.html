  1. Question

    A unit circle (a circle with radius of 1) is centered at the origin of a \(xy\)-plane. On the circle’s edge, a point has a \(x\) coordinate of -0.6942138, and the point is in quadrant II.

    Another line connects the origin to the point described above. What is the slope of this line that connects the point to the origin? Please be accurate within \(\pm 0.001\).


    Solution

    The equation of a unit circle centered at the origin is based on the distance formula.

    \[x^2+y^2=1\]

    We wish to solve for \(y\), so subtract \(x^2\) from both sides.

    \[y^2=1-x^2\]

    Now, to isolate \(y\), we can square-root both sides. We have to accommodate both the positive and negative square roots as possibilities.

    \[y=\pm \sqrt{1-x^2}\]

    Plug in the given value.

    \[y=\pm \sqrt{1-(-0.6942138)^2}\]

    \[y\approx\pm \sqrt{1-0.4819329}\]

    \[y\approx\pm \sqrt{0.5180671}\]

    \[y\approx\pm 0.7197688\]

    We were told the point is in quadrant II. Draw a sketch.


    Notice, in quadrant II, the \(x\) coordinate is negative, and the \(y\) coordinate is positive. So, we know that \(y=0.7197688\).


    The slope of the line can be found with the “rise over run” formula.


    \[\text{slope} ~=~ \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}\]

    \[\text{slope} ~=~ \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\]

    \[\text{slope} ~=~ \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\]

    \[\text{slope} ~=~ \frac{0.7197688-0}{-0.6942138-0}\]

    \[\text{slope} ~=~ \frac{0.7197688}{-0.6942138}\]

    \[\text{slope} ~\approx~ -1.0368114\]
