  1. Question

    A unit circle is drawn centered at the origin. A ray emanates from the origin at an angle of \(\theta\) radians and intersects the unit circle at point \((0.815, 0.579)\), as shown below.


    Evaluate the following trigonometric functions:

    • \(\sin(\theta)=\)
    • \(\cos(\theta)=\)
    • \(\tan(\theta)=\)

    Also, find the angle measure, \(\theta\), in radians.

    • \(\theta=\)


    Solution

    Notice, you can draw a right triangle with a hypotenuse of length 1.


    Use SOHCAHTOA to find the answers.

    \[\sin(\theta) ~=~ \frac{0.579}{1} ~=~ 0.579\]

    \[\cos(\theta) ~=~ \frac{0.815}{1} ~=~ 0.815\] \[\tan(\theta) ~=~ \frac{0.579}{0.815} ~\approx~ 0.71\]

    To find the angle measure, we can use any of the inverse trig functions.

    \[\arcsin(0.579)~\approx~0.618\] \[\arccos(0.815)~\approx~0.618\] \[\arctan(0.71)~\approx~0.617\]

    So, \(\theta\approx0.618\).

    Notice, on a unit circle, \(\sin(\theta)=y\) and \(\cos(\theta)=x\) and \(\tan(\theta)=\frac{y}{x}\). Also, notice that \(\tan(\theta)\) gives the SLOPE of the ray.
