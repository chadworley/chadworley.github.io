  1. Question

    A right triangle has an angle of \(\theta\) radians. The adjacent leg has a length of 34 meters, and the opposite leg has a length of 27 meters. What is the angle measure \(\theta\) in radians?


    Please round your answer to the nearest hundredth of a radian.


    Solution

    First, identify the correct trigonometric function. Remember, SOHCAHTOA.

    \[\sin(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\cos(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{adjacent}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\tan(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{adjacent}}\]

    In this case, we are given the adjacent and the opposite, and we are asked for the angle measure. The trigonometric ratio that relates these two sides is tangent. Use \(\theta\) to represent the requested angle measure.

    \[\tan(\theta) = \frac{27}{34}\]

    To evaluate \(\theta\), we need to use the inverse of tangent. This inverse function has a few names: arctan, atan, or \(\tan^{-1}\) are the most commonly used.

    \[\theta = \arctan\left(\frac{27}{34}\right)\] Depending on your calculator, you might type something like round(arctan(27/34),2)

    \[\theta \approx 0.67\]
