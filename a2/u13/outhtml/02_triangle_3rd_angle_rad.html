02_triangle_3rd_angle_rad


  1. Question

    A triangle is drawn. Two of the interior angle measures are known to be \(\frac{\pi}{12}\) and \(\frac{\pi}{36}\). Find the third interior angle measure in radians.

    • /


    Solution

    The interior angle measures sum to \(\pi\) radians (because \(\pi\) radians equals 180°). The unknown angle measure can be found by subtracting the other two angles from \(\pi\).

    \[\pi-\frac{\pi}{12}-\frac{\pi}{36}\]

    Find the least common denominator. The least common multiple of 12 and 36 is 36.

    \[\frac{36\pi}{36}-\frac{3\pi}{36}-\frac{1\pi}{36}\]

    With common denominators, we can simply subtract the numerators.

    \[\frac{36\pi-3\pi-1\pi}{36}\]

    Subtract.

    \[\frac{32\pi}{36}\]

    Simplify (if necessary)

    \[\frac{8\pi}{9}\]
