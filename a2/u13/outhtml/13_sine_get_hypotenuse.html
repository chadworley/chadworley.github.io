  1. Question

    A right triangle has an angle of 0.67 radians. The opposite leg has a length of 44 meters. How long, in meters, is the hypotenuse?


    Please round your answer to the nearest meter.


    Solution

    First, identify the correct trigonometric function. Remember, SOHCAHTOA.

    \[\sin(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\cos(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{adjacent}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\tan(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{adjacent}}\]

    In this case, we are given the angle and the opposite, and we are asked for the hypotenuse. The trigonometric ratio that relates these two sides is sine. Let \(x\) represent the requested length.

    \[\sin(0.67) = \frac{44}{x}\] Multiply both sides by \(x\) and divide both sides by \(\sin(0.67)\) to solve for \(x\).

    \[x = \frac{44}{\sin(0.67)}\] To evaluate \(x\), type the following into a modern calculator: round(44/sin(0.67)). Make sure to be in radian mode, not degree mode.

    \[x \approx 71\]
