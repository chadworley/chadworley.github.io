  1. Question

    A unit circle is drawn centered at the origin. A ray emanates from the origin and intersects the unit circle at point \((x,y)\). An arc along the unit circle from \((0,1)\) to point \((x,y)\) has a length of 0.618, as shown below.


    Find the coordinates of the point.

    • \(x=\)
    • \(y=\)

    Find the slope of the ray that connects the origin to the point.

    • slope =


    Solution

    Notice, you can draw a right triangle with a hypotenuse of length 1. Also, remember that the angle measure (in radians) is equivalent to the arc length on the unit circle.


    Use SOHCAHTOA to find the answers.

    Find \(x\).

    \[\cos(0.618) ~=~ \frac{x}{1}\]

    \[0.815 ~=~ x\]

    Find \(y\).

    \[\sin(0.618) ~=~ \frac{y}{1}\]

    \[0.579 ~=~ y\]

    Find the slope. Notice, since the ray goes through \((0,0)\), the slope, which is rise over run, is simply \(y/x\). Surprisingly, maybe, this is the same thing as \(\tan(\theta)\).

    \[\tan(0.618) ~=~ \text{slope} ~=~ \frac{y}{x} ~=~ \frac{\sin(0.618)}{\cos(0.618)}\]

    \[0.71 ~=~ \text{slope}\]
