  1. Question

    A right triangle has an angle measure \(\theta\). The opposite leg’s length is 27 meters, and the hypotenuse’s length is 44 meters. What is the angle measure \(\theta\) in radians?


    Please round your answer to the hundredth of a radian.


    Solution

    First, identify the correct trigonometric function. Remember, SOHCAHTOA.

    \[\sin(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\cos(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{adjacent}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\tan(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{adjacent}}\]

    In this case, we are given the opposite and the hypotenuse, and we are asked for the angle measure. The trigonometric ratio that relates these two sides is sine. Use \(\theta\) to represent the requested angle measure.

    \[\sin(\theta) = \frac{27}{44}\]

    To evaluate \(\theta\), we need to use the inverse of sine. This inverse function has a few names: arcsin, asin, or \(\sin^{-1}\) are the most commonly used.

    \[\theta = \arcsin\left(\frac{27}{44}\right)\]

    \[\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{27}{44}\right)\]

    Depending on your calculator, you might type something like round(arcsin(27/44),2)

    \[\theta \approx 0.66\]
