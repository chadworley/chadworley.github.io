  1. Question

    Consider the two matrices below.

    \[A=\begin{bmatrix} -8 & -5 & -7\\ -5 & -3 & -5\\ 1 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\]

    \[B=\begin{bmatrix} -5 & 7 & -4\\ 5 & -7 & 5\\ 2 & -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\]

    Matrices \(A\) and \(B\) are inverses if and only if \(AB=BA=I\), where \(I\) is the appropriate identity matrix. Are \(A\) and \(B\) multiplicative inverses?


    1. Yes, the matrices are inverses.
    2. No, the matrices are NOT inverses.

    Solution

    Use SageMath.

    A = matrix([[-8,-5,-7],
            [-5,-3,-5],
            [1,1,0]])
B = matrix([[-5,7,-4],
            [5,-7,5],
            [2,-3,1]])
pretty_print(A*B)
pretty_print(B*A)

    \[AB = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\] \[BA = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\]

    Notice, the products are identity matrices. Remember, an identity matrix has 1s on the main diagonal and 0s everywhere else.

    You could also use Desmos Matrix Calculator.
