  1. Question

    A \(2\times 2\) matrix is often interpreted as two 2D points. We could think of each row (or each column) as representing a point in the \((x,y)\) plane.

    \[A~=~\begin{bmatrix}x_1 & y_1 \\ x_2 & y_2\end{bmatrix}\]

    The determinant is defined as follows:

    \[\det(A)~=~x_1 y_2 - x_2y_1\]

    Let’s practice evaluating the determinant.

    \[B=\begin{bmatrix}4 & 1 \\ 3 & 8\end{bmatrix}\]

    Evaluate \(\det(B)\).


    Solution

    \[\det(B) ~=~ 4\cdot8 - 3\cdot1\] \[\det(B) ~=~ 29\]

    Notice, this also is the area of the parallelogram shown below:


    \[G=29\]
