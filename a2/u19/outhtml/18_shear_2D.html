  1. Question

    A video-game developer is hoping to apply a vertical shear to a set of points by using matrix multiplication.


    The starting points are stored as rows in a 5 \(\times\) 2 matrix \(S\).

    \[S=\begin{bmatrix} -1.04 & 4.714\\ 3.603 & -4.886\\ 2.371 & 5.39\\ 5.156 & -1.663\\ -4.171 & -1.64 \end{bmatrix}\]

    The vertical shear matrix can be found by using \(k=0.494\) in the formula matrix.

    \[T~=~\begin{bmatrix} 1 & k\\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\]

    The final points (in matrix \(F\)) are found by multiplying \(S\cdot T\).

    \[F=S\cdot T\]

    That multiplication can be done using the diagram below.


    What is the new y coordinate when point \((2.371, 5.39)\) undergoes the vertical shear transformation with \(k=0.494\)?


    Solution

    The vertical shear transformation matrix:

    \[T=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0.494\\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\]

    We are looking for the new y coordinate after point \((2.371, 5.39)\) undergoes the shear transformation. Use column 2 of \(T\):

    \[(2.371)(0.494)~+~(5.39)(1)\]

    The new y coordinate is 6.561274.
