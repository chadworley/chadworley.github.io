  1. Question

    Let \(A\) and \(B\) be 3x4 matrices.

    \[A = \left[ \begin{matrix} 3 & -6 & 6 & 1\\ -5 & 2 & -7 & 0\\ 6 & -5 & -2 & 2\\ \end{matrix} \right]\]

    \[B = \left[ \begin{matrix} 9 & 9 & -3 & 4\\ 9 & -7 & 5 & 6\\ -7 & 1 & -8 & 2\\ \end{matrix} \right]\]

    Let \(C\) be the sum of \(A\) plus \(B\).

    \[C = A + B\]

    Determine the values of matrix \(C\):



    Solution

    Simply add corresponding elements.

    \[C ~=~ \left[ \begin{matrix} 12 & 3 & 3 & 5\\ 4 & -5 & -2 & 6\\ -1 & -4 & -10 & 4\\ \end{matrix} \right]\]

    You might be wondering why matrix multiplication is not as simple. What you are looking for is called the Hadamard product.
