  1. Question

    The 2D rotation matrix is defined below for a counter-clockwise rotation by an angle \(\theta\) when it is the second operand in matrix multiplication. (Note: wikipedia’s article shows the more standard left operator. I’m opting for the right-operator definition for various mysterious reasons…)

    \[R = \begin{bmatrix}\cos(\theta) & \sin(\theta) \\ -\sin(\theta) & \cos(\theta) \end{bmatrix} \]

    Let \(\theta=1.40\) radians. Determine \(R\) (approximate decimal values).

    \(R =\)


    Solution

    \[R = \begin{bmatrix} 0.17 & 0.99\\ -0.99 & 0.17 \end{bmatrix}\]
