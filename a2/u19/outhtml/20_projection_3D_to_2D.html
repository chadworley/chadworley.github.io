  1. Question

    A video-game developer is hoping to apply an oblique projection to show a set of 3D points as a 2D image. The starting points are stored as rows in a 5 \(\times\) 3 matrix \(S\).

    \[S=\begin{bmatrix} 1.1083 & 0.6921 & 1.1369\\ 1.6243 & -0.3065 & -0.5174\\ 0.2532 & 0.7108 & -1.5591\\ -0.2628 & 1.7093 & 0.0952\\ -0.6808 & -0.7014 & 0.2111 \end{bmatrix}\]

    The oblique projection matrix \(P\) has two key parameters: \(\theta\) and \(r\). For this problem, use \(\theta=0.9987\) and \(r=0.5106\).

    \[P~=~\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ r\cdot\cos(\theta) & r\cdot\sin(\theta) \end{bmatrix}\]

    The final points (in matrix \(F\)) are found by multiplying \(S\cdot P\).

    \[F=S\cdot P\]


    What is the new \(x\) coordinate when point \((1.6243, -0.3065, -0.5174)\) is projected using the oblique projection with \(\theta=0.9987\) and \(r=0.5106\)?


    Solution

    Find the projection matrix by pluggin in \(\theta=0.9987\) and \(r=0.5106\).

    \[P=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ 0.276436675712063 & 0.429296080020845 \end{bmatrix}\]

    We use row 2 of the points matrix and column 1 of the projection matrix.

    \[(1.6243)(1)~+~(-0.3065)(0)~+~(-0.5174)(0.2764367)\]

    Thus, \(x=1.4812717\).
