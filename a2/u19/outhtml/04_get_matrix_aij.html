  1. Question

    A matrix is a rectangular array of numbers. We typically represent matrices by placing numbers (the elements) in a regular rectangular formation inside large square brackets. For example, let matrix \(A\) be defined below:

    \[A~=~\begin{bmatrix} 76 & 77 & 47 & 52 & 83\\ 33 & 40 & 71 & 94 & 87\\ 50 & 70 & 79 & 65 & 55\\ 90 & 32 & 49 & 17 & 82 \end{bmatrix}\]

    Notice, matrix \(A\) has 4 rows and 5 columns. We say \(A\) is a 4-by-5 matrix. The matrix is composed of 20 elements. Each element has an “address”: its row number and its column number (in that order!).

    There are many notations for expressing the element at row \(i\) and column \(j\). Some options include \(a_{ij}\) or \(a_{i,\,j}\) or \(A[i,j]\).

    What element is in row 1 and column 4?

    \[a_{1,4}~=~ ?\]


    Solution

    The element 52 is in row 1 and column 4.
