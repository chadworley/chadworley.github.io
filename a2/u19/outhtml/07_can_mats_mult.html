  1. Question

    If the dimensions of matrix \(A\) are \(m\times n\) and the dimensions of matrix \(B\) are \(p\times q\), then the matrix-multiplication \(AB\) is only possible if \(n=p\). If the multiplication is possible, then the product is defined with dimensions \(m\times q\).

    \[A=\begin{bmatrix} -6 & -5\\ 4 & -6\\ -2 & 5\\ 3 & -6 \end{bmatrix}\] \[B=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 7 & -9\\ 2 & 1 & -5\\ 0 & -6 & 8\\ 7 & 0 & -6\\ 8 & 7 & 1\\ -1 & 2 & -1\\ -2 & 4 & -2 \end{bmatrix}\]

    • \(\mathrm{dim}(A)=\) \(\times\)
    • \(\mathrm{dim}(B)=\) \(\times\)
    • Is the product \(AB\) defined?


    Solution

    • The dimensions of \(A\) are \(4\times2\).
    • The dimensions of \(B\) are \(7\times3\).
    • The number of columns of \(A\) is NOT equal to the number of rows of \(B\), so we can NOT find the product \(AB\).

  2. Question

    If the dimensions of matrix \(A\) are \(m\times n\) and the dimensions of matrix \(B\) are \(p\times q\), then the matrix-multiplication \(AB\) is only possible if \(n=p\). If the multiplication is possible, then the product is defined with dimensions \(m\times q\).

    \[A=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -9 & 8 & 0 & -3\\ 8 & -9 & -4 & 6 & 1\\ -4 & -6 & -5 & 4 & -4 \end{bmatrix}\] \[B=\begin{bmatrix} -4 & 2 & -4 & -9 & -2 & -3 & -3\\ -7 & -6 & 6 & 9 & -7 & 8 & 6\\ 0 & 8 & -7 & -9 & -3 & 0 & 5\\ -9 & 0 & 5 & 7 & -5 & -6 & 8\\ 6 & -1 & -9 & -4 & -4 & 9 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\]

    Solution

    The dimensions of the product \(C=AB\) would be \(3\times7\).
