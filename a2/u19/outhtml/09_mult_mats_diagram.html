  1. Question

    Let matrices \(A\) and \(B\) be defined below.

    \[A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 & 5 & 5\\ 4 & 5 & 3 & 5\\ 3 & 2 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\] \[B = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 1 & 4 & 1 & 3\\ 3 & 5 & 0 & 4 & 4\\ 5 & 0 & 4 & 5 & 6\\ 5 & 3 & 3 & 6 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\]

    A third matrix, \(C\), is equal to the product \(AB\). A student set up a diagram to help find the product; they are missing a single element in \(C\), element \(c_{3,3}\).


    What is \(c_{3,3}\)?


    Solution

    To find \(c_{3,3}\), you use row 3 from matrix \(A\) and column 3 from matrix \(B\).


    You multiply corresponding elements and sum those products.

    \[c_{3,3}~=~( 3 \cdot 4 )+( 2 \cdot 0 )+( 0 \cdot 4 )+( 2 \cdot 3 )\]

    \[c_{3,3}~=~12+0+0+6\]

    \[c_{3,3}~=~18\]

    Thus,

    \[\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 & 5 & 5\\ 4 & 5 & 3 & 5\\ 3 & 2 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 1 & 4 & 1 & 3\\ 3 & 5 & 0 & 4 & 4\\ 5 & 0 & 4 & 5 & 6\\ 5 & 3 & 3 & 6 & 1 \end{bmatrix} ~=~ \begin{bmatrix} 70 & 37 & 43 & 73 & 57\\ 71 & 44 & 43 & 69 & 55\\ 28 & 19 & 18 & 23 & 19 \end{bmatrix}\]
