  1. Question

    A video-game developer is hoping to rotate a set of points around the origin by 0.829 radians counterclockwise by using matrix multiplication.


    The starting points are stored as rows in a 10 \(\times\) 2 matrix \(S\).

    \[S=\begin{bmatrix} 3.56 & -7.291\\ -3.741 & -7.683\\ -5.12 & 0.412\\ 2.746 & -8.27\\ -6.069 & 4.263\\ 4.425 & 7.813\\ 1.355 & -5.172\\ 1.122 & 2.236\\ -6 & -4.388\\ -5.49 & 3.613 \end{bmatrix}\]

    The 2D-rotation matrix can be found by using \(\theta=0.829\) in the formula below.

    \[R~=~\begin{bmatrix}\cos(\theta)&\sin(\theta)\\ -\sin(\theta) & \cos(\theta)\end{bmatrix}\]

    The final points (in matrix \(F\)) are found by multiplying \(S\cdot R\).

    \[F=S\cdot R\]

    That multiplication can be done using the diagram below.


    As denoted by the question mark in the diagram above, when the point \((-6,-4.388)\) is rotated by 0.829 radians, what is the new \(x\) coordinate?


    Solution

    First, you can get decimal approximations for the rotation-matrix elements by using \(\theta=0.829\).

    \[R=\begin{bmatrix} 0.675613353881537 & 0.737256126496716\\ -0.737256126496716 & 0.675613353881537 \end{bmatrix}\]

    The initial point \((-6,-4.388)\) is in row 9 of \(S\). Because we want to find the new \(x\) coordinate, use column 1 of \(R\).

    \[(-6)(0.6756134)~+~(-4.388)(-0.7372561)\]

    So, the new \(x\) coordinate is -0.8186002.
