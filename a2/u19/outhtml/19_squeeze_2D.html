  1. Question

    A video-game developer is hoping to apply a squeeze map to a set of points by using matrix multiplication. Interestingly, during a squeeze map, the area remains constant.


    The starting points are stored as rows in a 5 \(\times\) 2 matrix \(S\).

    \[S=\begin{bmatrix} 4.683 & 5.35\\ 5.832 & -4.735\\ 2.365 & -3.74\\ -2.396 & -5.348\\ -4.102 & 3.017 \end{bmatrix}\]

    The squeeze matrix can be found by using \(k=0.625\) in the formula below.

    \[T~=~\begin{bmatrix} k & 0\\ 0 & 1/k \end{bmatrix}\]

    The final points (in matrix \(F\)) are found by multiplying \(S\cdot T\).

    \[F=S\cdot T\]

    That multiplication can be done using the diagram below.


    What is the new y coordinate when point \((-4.102, 3.017)\) undergoes the squeeze transformation with \(k=0.625\)?


    Solution

    The squeeze transformation matrix:

    \[T=\begin{bmatrix} 0.625 & 0\\ 0 & 1.6 \end{bmatrix}\]

    We are looking for the new y coordinate after point \((-4.102, 3.017)\) undergoes the squeeze mapping. Use column 2 of \(T\):

    \[(-4.102)(0)~+~(3.017)(1.6)\]

    The new y coordinate is 4.8272.
