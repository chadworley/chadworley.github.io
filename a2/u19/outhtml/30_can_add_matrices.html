  1. Question

    If the dimensions of matrix \(A\) are \(m\times n\) and the dimensions of matrix \(B\) are \(p\times q\), then matrix addition \(A+B\) is only possible if \(m=p\) and \(q=n\). If addition is possible, then the sum also has dimensions \(m\times n\).

    \[A=\begin{bmatrix} -9 & -1 & 8\\ -2 & -6 & -3\\ 5 & -6 & -1\\ -8 & 9 & -7\\ -5 & -8 & -4\\ 3 & 1 & -2 \end{bmatrix}\] \[B=\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 3 & 5\\ 8 & -7 & 0\\ 2 & 6 & 0\\ -1 & -2 & 5\\ -4 & -2 & -8\\ 3 & -9 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\]

    • \(\mathrm{dim}(A)=\) \(\times\)
    • \(\mathrm{dim}(B)=\) \(\times\)
    • Is the sum \(A+B\) defined?


    Solution

    • The dimensions of \(A\) are \(6\times3\).
    • The dimensions of \(B\) are \(6\times3\).
    • The dimensions match, so the sum is defined.
