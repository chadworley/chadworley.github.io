Question

A video-game developer is hoping to rotate a set of points around the origin by 0.565 radians counterclockwise by using matrix multiplication.


The starting points are stored as rows in a 10 \(\times\) 2 matrix \(S\).

\[S=\begin{bmatrix} 7.762 & 0.042\\ 1.371 & 9.617\\ 3.105 & -0.071\\ 8.54 & -0.558\\ -6.045 & 2.118\\ -6.601 & -1.668\\ -4.001 & 5.758\\ 1.227 & -2.613\\ 4.578 & 3.869\\ -0.272 & -4.292 \end{bmatrix}\]

The 2D-rotation matrix can be found by using \(\theta=0.565\) in the formula below.

\[R~=~\begin{bmatrix}\cos(\theta)&\sin(\theta)\\ -\sin(\theta) & \cos(\theta)\end{bmatrix}\]

The final points (in matrix \(F\)) are found by multiplying \(S\cdot R\).

\[F=S\cdot R\]

That multiplication can be done using the diagram below.


As denoted by the question mark in the diagram above, when the point \((4.578,3.869)\) is rotated by 0.565 radians, what is the new \(x\) coordinate? The tolerance is \(\pm 0.001\).

Solution

First, you can get decimal approximations for the rotation-matrix elements by using \(\theta=0.565\).

\[R=\begin{bmatrix} 0.844588600423353 & 0.535415816011183\\ -0.535415816011183 & 0.844588600423353 \end{bmatrix}\]

The initial point \((4.578,3.869)\) is in row 9 of \(S\). Because we want to find the new \(x\) coordinate, use column 1 of \(R\).

\[(4.578)(0.8445886)~+~(3.869)(-0.5354158)\]

So, the new \(x\) coordinate is 1.7950028.
