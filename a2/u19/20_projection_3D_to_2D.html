Question

A video-game developer is hoping to apply an oblique projection to show a set of 3D points as a 2D image. The starting points are stored as rows in a 5 \(\times\) 3 matrix \(S\).

\[S=\begin{bmatrix} 1.6404 & -0.1299 & 0.5405\\ 0.3312 & -1.6392 & 0.4511\\ -0.2409 & -1.0351 & -1.3677\\ 1.0683 & 0.4742 & -1.2782\\ -0.6997 & 0.5825 & 0.4136 \end{bmatrix}\]

The oblique projection matrix \(P\) has two key parameters: \(\theta\) and \(r\). For this problem, use \(\theta=0.6924\) and \(r=0.2986\).

\[P~=~\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ r\cdot\cos(\theta) & r\cdot\sin(\theta) \end{bmatrix}\]

The final points (in matrix \(F\)) are found by multiplying \(S\cdot P\).

\[F=S\cdot P\]


What is the new \(x\) coordinate when point \((1.0683, 0.4742, -1.2782)\) is projected using the oblique projection with \(\theta=0.6924\) and \(r=0.2986\)?

Solution

Find the projection matrix by pluggin in \(\theta=0.6924\) and \(r=0.2986\).

\[P=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ 0.229837229263215 & 0.190622160423725 \end{bmatrix}\]

We use row 4 of the points matrix and column 1 of the projection matrix.

\[(1.0683)(1)~+~(0.4742)(0)~+~(-1.2782)(0.2298372)\]

Thus, \(x=0.7745221\).
