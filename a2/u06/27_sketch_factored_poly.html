Question

A polynomial, \(p(x)\), is given in factored form. \[p(x)~=~-(x+5)^{2}\cdot (x+1)\cdot (x-8)\]

Which of the following graphs matches \(y=p(x)\)?


Solution

First, consider the end behavior. If you expanded the polynomial into standard form, the highest-degree term would be \(-x^{4}\).

\[p(x)~=~-x^{4}+\ldots\]

The highest degree term has a negative coefficient and an even degree, so the end behavior is “down,down”. Or, more formally, \(\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty} p(x) ~=~ -\infty\) and \(\lim\limits_{x\to\infty} p(x) ~=~ -\infty\).

Next, we consider the roots. From the factored form, \(p(x)~=~-(x+5)^{2}\cdot (x+1)\cdot (x-8)\), it is easy to see (see zero-product property) that \(x=-5\) or \(x=-1\) or \(x=8\) makes the polynomial output 0.

\[p(-5)~=~-(-5+5)^{2}\cdot (-5+1)\cdot (-5-8)~=~0\] \[p(-1)~=~-(-1+5)^{2}\cdot (-1+1)\cdot (-1-8)~=~0\] \[p(8)~=~-(8+5)^{2}\cdot (8+1)\cdot (8-8)~=~0\]

We predict that at \(x=-5\) there is a double root (multiplicity of 2) because the corresponding factor is squared. Graphically, we expect the curve to “bounce” off the \(x\) axis at \(x=-5\). At the other roots, we expect the curve to cut through the \(x\) axis.

Anyway, based on this analysis, we can produce a sign graph of the polynomial.

\(x\) interval or value sign analysis of \(p(x)~=~-(x+5)^{2}\cdot (x+1)\cdot (x-8)\) sign
\((-\infty,-5)\) \(-(\mathrm{neg})^{2}\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\) \(-1\)
\(-5\) \(-(\mathrm{zero})^{2}\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\) \(0\)
\((-5,-1)\) \(-(\mathrm{pos})^{2}\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\) \(-1\)
\(-1\) \(-(\mathrm{pos})^{2}\cdot (\mathrm{zero})\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\) \(0\)
\((-1,8)\) \(-(\mathrm{pos})^{2}\cdot (\mathrm{pos})\cdot (\mathrm{neg})\) \(1\)
\(8\) \(-(\mathrm{pos})^{2}\cdot (\mathrm{pos})\cdot (\mathrm{zero})\) \(0\)
\((8,\infty)\) \(-(\mathrm{pos})^{2}\cdot (\mathrm{pos})\cdot (\mathrm{pos})\) \(-1\)


And only one option matches.
