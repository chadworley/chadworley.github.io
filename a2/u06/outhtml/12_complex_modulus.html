  1. Question

    Let \(z_1\) be a complex number with integer components. It is graphed on a complex plane below, along with a segment connecting it to the origin.


    The modulus of a complex number is analogous to finding distance on a 2D Cartesian plane from the origin to a point. If complex number \(z=a+bi\) then its modulus, \(|z|\), is calculated with a variation on the distance formula: \[|z|=\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\] Calculate \(|z_1|\). The tolerance is \(\pm 0.1\).


    Solution

    The horizontal (real) component is -6. The vertical (imaginary) component is -3. So, \[z_1 = -6-3i\]

    Use the formula. \[|z_1| = \sqrt{(-6)^2+(-3)^2}\] \[|z_1| = \sqrt{36+9}\] \[|z_1| = \sqrt{45} \approx 6.7082039 \]
