  1. Question

    A composite number was created by multiplying a sample (with repetition) of primes between 2 and 13.

    • The composite: 660

    Please factor the composite number into its prime factorization.

    • The prime factorization:

    \(660~=~2\)\(\cdot 3\)\(\cdot 5\)\(\cdot 7\)\(\cdot 11\)\(\cdot 13\)



    Solution

    You can make a factor tree.

    plot of chunk unnamed-chunk-3

    We can express 660 as a product of primes.

    \[660~=~ 2\cdot2\cdot3\cdot5\cdot11\]

    Repeated multiplication can be expressed with powers.

    \[660~=~ 2^{2}\cdot3^{1}\cdot5^{1}\cdot11^{1}\]

    For this problem, we want all the exponents for primes between 2 and 13 to be expressed explicitly (so we include some exponents of 0).

    \[660~=~ 2^{2}\cdot3^{1}\cdot5^{1}\cdot7^{0}\cdot11^{1}\cdot13^{0}\]
