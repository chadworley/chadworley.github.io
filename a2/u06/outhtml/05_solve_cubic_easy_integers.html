  1. Question

    Factor the cubic polynomial \(x^{3} + 5 x^{2} - 36 x - 180\) to find solutions to the cubic equation: \[x^{3} + 5 x^{2} - 36 x - 180=0\]

    Hint: all solutions are integers between -6 and 6.

    You might find the rational root theorem helpful. I’d recommend using synthetic division to check your guesses until you find one linear factor, then you’ll just need to factor the quadratic factor into two linear factors.


    1. -6
    2. -5
    3. -4
    4. -3
    5. -2
    6. -1
    7. 0
    8. 1
    9. 2
    10. 3
    11. 4
    12. 5
    13. 6

    Solution

    Possible successful route 1

    • Guess that \((x+6)\) is a factor:
      1 5 -36 -180
      -6 -6 6 180
      1 -1 -30 0
      Notice, the remainder is 0, so the guess was good! So, we rephrase the original equation into a semi-factored form.\[(x+6)(x^2-x-30)=0\]Then factor the quadratic expression to get the fully factored form of the equation.\[(x+6)(x+5)(x-6)=0\]Thus the solutions are: {-6, -5, 6}

    Possible successful route 2

    • Guess that \((x+5)\) is a factor:
      1 5 -36 -180
      -5 -5 0 180
      1 0 -36 0
      Notice, the remainder is 0, so the guess was good! So, we rephrase the original equation into a semi-factored form.\[(x+5)(x^2-36)=0\]Then factor the quadratic expression to get the fully factored form of the equation.\[(x+6)(x+5)(x-6)=0\]Thus the solutions are: {-6, -5, 6}
