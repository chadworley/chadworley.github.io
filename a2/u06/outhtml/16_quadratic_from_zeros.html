  1. Question

    A quadratic polynomial, \(f(x)\), is generated by randomly selecting two parameters, \(B\) and \(C\), in the equation below.

    \[f(x)~=~x^2+Bx+C\]

    Notice the leading coefficient (of the quadratic term) is 1. However the linear coefficient and the constant are randomly determined.

    When \(y=f(x)\) is graphed below, you notice it has zeros (roots, \(x\)-intercepts) at coordinates \((1,0)\) and \((5,0)\).


    Factored form

    The polynomial \(f(x)\) can be expressed in factored form. Which option correctly expresses \(f(x)\) in factored form?

    1. \(f(x) = (x-{1})\cdot (x+{5})\)
    2. \(f(x) = (x+{1})\cdot (x-{5})\)
    3. \(f(x) = (x-{1})\cdot (x-{5})\)
    4. \(f(x) = (x+{1})\cdot (x+{5})\)

    Standard (expanded) form

    The polynomial \(f(x)\) can be expressed in standard form. Which option correctly expresses \(f(x)\) in standard form?

    1. \(f(x) = x^{2}-{6}x+{5}\)
    2. \(f(x) = x^{2}+{6}x+{5}\)
    3. \(f(x) = x^{2}-{4}x-{5}\)
    4. \(f(x) = x^{2}+{4}x-{5}\)

    Vertex form

    The polynomial \(f(x)\) can be expressed in vertex form:

    \[f(x)~=~ (x-h)^2+k\]

    where coordinates \((h,k)\) are at the vertex of the parabola (as marked on the graph). Which option correctly expresses \(f(x)\) in vertex form?

    1. \(f(x)~=~(x+2)^2-9\)
    2. \(f(x)~=~(x-3)^2-4\)
    3. \(f(x)~=~(x-2)^2-9\)
    4. \(f(x)~=~(x+3)^2-4\)



    Solution

    If the zeros are at \((p,0)\) and \((q,0)\), then the factored form will be: \(f(x)~=~(x-p)\cdot(x-q)\). In our case, \(p=1\) and \(q=5\), so in factored form:

    \[f(x) = (x-{1})\cdot (x-{5})\]

    To get the standard (expanded) form, we can multiply the two linear binomials (using the FOIL method or box method).

    * \(x\) \(-1\)
    \(x\) \(x^2\) \(-x\)
    \(-5\) \({-5}x\) \(5\)

    \[f(x)~=~x^{2}-{5}x-x+{5}\]

    Combine like terms.

    \[f(x)~=~x^{2}-{6}x+{5}\]

    To get the vertex form, first average \(p\) and \(q\) to get \(h\).

    \[h~=~ \frac{{1}+{5}}{2} ~=~ 3\]

    Then, plug \(x=3\) into the factored form or the standard form to get \(k\).

    \[k~=~({3}-{1})\cdot({3}-{5}) ~=~ -4\] \[k~=~(3)^2+(-6)(3)+(5) ~=~ -4\]

    So,

    \[f(x)~=~(x-3)^2-4\]
