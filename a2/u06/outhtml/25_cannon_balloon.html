  1. Question

    On an alien planet, the acceleration due to gravity is -13.8 \(\mathrm{m/s^2}\). At \(t=0\), a projectile is launched straight up with a speed of 41.7 \(\mathrm{m/s}\). The effect of air resistance is minimal, so the projectile’s height, \(p\), is a quadratic function of time.

    \[p(t)~=~-6.9t^2+41.7t\]

    After \(t=1.6\) seconds, a balloon is released from the same location as the projectile was launched. The balloon rises at a constant speed of 9.5 \(\mathrm{m/s}\), thus the balloon’s height, \(b\), is a linear function of time.

    \[b(t)~=~9.5\cdot(t-1.6)\]


    At what time (in seconds after the projectile is launched) does the balloon get popped by the projectile?


    Solution

    To find the intersection of \(y=p(t)\) and \(y=b(t)\), we find \(t\) such that \(p(t)=b(t)\) and \(t>0\).

    \[\begin{align*} p(t) ~&=~ b(t)\\ -6.9t^2+41.7t ~&=~ 9.5\cdot(t-1.6) \end{align*}\]

    Distribute the right-hand side of the equation.

    \[-6.9t^2+41.7t ~=~ 9.5t-15.2\]

    Use addition (or subtraction) to bring all terms to the right-hand side.

    \[0 ~=~ 6.9t^2-41.7t + 9.5t-15.2\]

    Combine like terms.

    \[0 ~=~ 6.9t^2-32.2t-15.2\]

    Use the quadratic formula.

    \[t = \frac{32.2 \pm \sqrt{(-32.2)^2-4(6.9)(-15.2)}}{2\cdot(6.9)}\]

    Our two possible times are \(t=-0.4320496\) and \(t=5.0987163\). We are only interested in the positive solution.

    \[t ~=~ 5.099\, \mathrm{s}\]
