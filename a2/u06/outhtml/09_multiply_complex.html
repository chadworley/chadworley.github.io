  1. Question

    Let \(z_1\) and \(z_2\) be two complex numbers. \[z_1 = -6-3i\] \[z_2 = -9-8i\] Find the product. \[z_1 \cdot z_2 = ~?\]

    Type your answer precisely, with no spaces, using digits, “-”, “+”, and “i”. If the real term is nonzero, put it first. If either component is zero, do not include that term. If both components are zero, type “0” without the quotes. Instead of writing “1i” or “-1i” just write “i” or “-i”.


    Solution

    To multiply complex numbers, follow a procedure similar to FOIL, but remember \(i^2=-1\).

    \[(-6-3i)(-9-8i)\]

    \[\Big((-6)+(-3i)\Big)\Big((-9)+(-8i)\Big)\]

    \[(-6)(-9)~~+~~(-6)(-8i)~~+~~(-3i)(-9)~~+~~(-3i)(-8i)\] \[54~~+~~(48)i~~+~~(27)i ~~+~~(24)i^2\]

    You could also use a box to multiply.

    * \(-6\) \(-3i\)
    \(-9\) \(54\) \(27i\)
    \(-8i\) \(48i\) \(24i^2\)

    \[54~~+~~(48)i~~+~~(27)i ~~+~~(24)i^2\]

    Remember that \(i^2=-1\).

    \[54~~+~~(48)i~~+~~(27)i~~+~~(-24)\]

    Let’s group similar terms. We put the real terms to the left and the imaginary terms to the right.

    \[54~~+~~(-24)~~+~~(48)i~~+~~(27)i\] Combine the real terms and combine the imaginary terms.

    \[30+75i\]
