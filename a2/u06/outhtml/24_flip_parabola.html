  1. Question

    A downward-facing parabola \(f\) is defined below.

    \[f(x)~=~-x^{2}-{4}x-{9}\]

    An upwards-facing parabola, \(g\), is constructed such that \(g\) and \(f\) are mirror images with a shared vertex, as shown in the graph below.


    The second parabola, \(g\), can be written in standard form with two parameters (\(B\) and \(C\)).

    \[g(x)~=~x^2+Bx+C\]

    Find \(B\) and \(C\).

    • \(B~=~\)
    • \(C~=~\)


    Solution

    1. Find the vertex by completing the square. (First factor out the negative to get a quadratic polynomial with a +1 leading coefficient.)

    \[f(x)~=~-\left(x^{2}+{4}x+{9}\right)\]

    \[\left(\frac{4}{2}\right)^2~=~4\]

    \[f(x)~=~-\left(x^{2}+{4}x+{4}+{9}-{4}\right)\]

    \[f(x)~=~-\left( (x+{2})^2+{5} \right)\]

    \[f(x)~=~-(x+{2})^2-{5}\]

    1. The second parabola has the same vertex \((-2,-5)\), but it faces upwards. The general vertex form is \(A(x-h)^2+k\). If \(A\) is positive, the parabola faces upward, and if \(A\) is negative, the parabola faces downward. If \(|A|>1\), then the parabola gets stretched vertically. If \(|A|<1\), then the parabola gets squished vertically. Anyway, in this case, we just need to switch the sign of \(A\).

    \[g(x)~=~(x+{2})^2-{5}\]

    1. Expand to produce standard form.

    \[g(x)~=~x^{2}+{4}x+{4}-{5}\]

    \[g(x)~=~x^{2}+{4}x-{1}\]
