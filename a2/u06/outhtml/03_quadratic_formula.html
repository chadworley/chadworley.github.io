  1. Question

    The quadratic formula states:

    • If \(ax^2+bx+c=0\) then: \[x = \frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\]

    Use the quadratic formula to solve the quadratic equation: \[6x^2-19x+10=0\]


    1. -5/2
    2. -5/3
    3. -5/7
    4. -2/3
    5. 2/3
    6. 5/7
    7. 5/3
    8. 5/2

    Solution

    Plug numbers into the quadratic formula.

    \[x = \frac{-(-19)\pm\sqrt{(-19)^2-4(6)(10)}}{2(6)} \]

    Simplify.

    \[x = \frac{19 \pm \sqrt{121}}{12}\]

    \[x = \frac{19 \pm 11}{12}\]

    \[x = \frac{8}{12}~~~~~~~~\text{OR}~~~~~~~~\frac{30}{12}\]

    \[x = \frac{2}{3} ~~~~~~\text{OR}~~~~~~~ x=\frac{5}{2} \]

    Factoring

    I should mention if you are handy with factoring, you might create an equivalent (factored) equation:

    \[(3x-2)(2x-5) = 0\]
