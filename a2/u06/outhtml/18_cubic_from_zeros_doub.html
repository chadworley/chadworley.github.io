  1. Question

    A cubic polynomial, \(f(x)\), is generated by randomly selecting the three parameters, \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\), in the equation below.

    \[f(x)~=~x^3+Bx^2+Cx+D\]

    You know the graph \(y=f(x)\) has two distinct real roots at \(x=-3\) and \(x=3\), as shown below. Also, since the curve “bounces” at \(x=-3\), we say that root has multiplicity of 2.


    Find \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\).

    • \(B=\)
    • \(C=\)
    • \(D=\)


    Solution

    First, write the cubic polynomial in factored form (based on the three roots).

    \[f(x)~=~(x+{3})(x+{3})(x-{3})\]

    Partially expand the expression by writing \((x+{3})(x+{3})\) in standard form. To do this, we can use the FOIL method or a box.

    * \(x\) \(3\)
    \(x\) \(x^2\) \(3x\)
    \(3\) \(3x\) \(9\)

    \[f(x)~=~ \left(x^{2}+{3}x+{3}x+{9}\right)(x-{3})\]

    Combine like terms.

    \[f(x)~=~ \left(x^{2}+{6}x+{9}\right)(x-{3})\]

    Now, multiply the quadratic by the linear.

    * \(x^2\) \(6x\) \(9\)
    \(x\) \(x^3\) \(6x^2\) \(9x\)
    \(-3\) \(-3x^2\) \(-18x\) \(-27\)

    \[f(x)~=~x^{3}-{3}x^{2}+{6}x^{2}-{18}x+{9}x-{27}\]

    Combine like terms.

    \[f(x)~=~x^{3}+{3}x^{2}-{9}x-{27}\]

    Thus,

    • \(B=3\)
    • \(C=-9\)
    • \(D=-27\)
