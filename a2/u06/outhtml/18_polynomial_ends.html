  1. Question

    \[f(x) = -7x^5-4x^4-10x^3-9x^2+5x+9\]

    Your answers:

    • Left end behavior: \(\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}f(x)=\)
    • Right end behavior: \(\lim\limits_{x\to\infty}f(x)=\)


    Solution

    As \(x\) heads toward \(-\infty\) or \(\infty\), the highest-degree term dominates. So, the end behavior is dictated by the \(-7x^5\) term. There are two characteristics of two parameters to consider:

    • The sign of the leading coefficient. In this case, -7 is negative.
    • The parity of the exponent. In this case, 5 is odd.

    So, \[\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}f(x) = -7(-\infty)^{5} = (-7)(-\infty) = \infty\] \[\lim\limits_{x\to\infty}f(x) = -7(\infty)^{5} = (-7)(\infty) = -\infty\]
