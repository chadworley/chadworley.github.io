  1. Question

    A cubic polynomial, \(f(x)\), is generated by randomly selecting the three real parameters, \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\), in the equation below.

    \[f(x)~=~x^3+Bx^2+Cx+D\]

    You know the graph \(y=f(x)\) has one real root at \(x=3\). You are told that another (complex) root is \(x=-3+i\).


    Find \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\).

    • \(B=\)
    • \(C=\)
    • \(D=\)


    Solution

    We know that complex roots come as pairs of complex conjugates. We are told \(x=-3+i\) is a root, so we also know \(x=-3-i\) is another root.

    Write the cubic polynomial in factored form (based on the three roots).

    \[f(x)~=~(x-{3})\cdot\left(x-(-3+i)\right)\cdot\left(x-(-3-i)\right)\]

    I would expand \(\left(x-(-3+i)\right)\cdot\left(x-(-3-i)\right)\) by first distributing across the internal parentheses to get \(\left(x+3-i\right)\cdot\left(x+3+i\right)\) and then using the box method to multiply.

    * \(x\) \(3\) \(-1i\)
    \(x\) \(x^2\) \(3x\) \(-1ix\)
    \(3\) \(3x\) \(9\) \(-3i\)
    \(1i\) \(1ix\) \(3i\) \(1\)

    Combine like terms to get:

    \[f(x)~=~(x-{3})\cdot\left(x^{2}+{6}x+{10}\right)\]

    Now, we multiply again.

    * \(x^2\) \(6x\) \(10\)
    \(x\) \(x^3\) \(6x^2\) \(10x\)
    \(-3\) \(-3x^2\) \(-18x\) \(-30\)

    Combine like terms to get: \[f(x)~~=~~ x^{3}+{3}x^{2}-{8}x-{30}\]

    Thus,

    • \(B=3\)
    • \(C=-8\)
    • \(D=-30\)
