  1. Question

    Consider a certificate of deposit (CD) with 2.64% APY and a maturity term of 4 years. An investor invests $250000 in this CD. When the CD matures (after 4 years), how much money can be withdrawn?


    Solution

    Each year the value increases by 2.64%, and this compounding occurs 4 times.

    Find the scale factor. \[1+0.0264~=~ 1.0264\]

    Multiply the principal ($250000) by the scale factor 4 times. Remember, repeated multiplication can be represented with an exponent.

    \[250000\cdot 1.0264^{4}~=~ 277463.96\]

    So, after 4 years, this CD is worth $277463.96. You need to remember to withdraw in a short grace period, otherwise the money will typically roll into a new CD, and you’ll need to wait another 4 years to withdraw without penalties.
