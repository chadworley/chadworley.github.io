    In Desmos, we can produce sequences defined recursively. In basic Chaos Theory, it is common to discuss the logistic map.

    Let’s produce a sequence using the logistic map with \(r=3.72643\) and \(f(1)=0.39\). In Desmos, write the following expressions: \[f(n)~=~ 3.72643\cdot f(n-1) \cdot \Big(1-f(n-1)\Big)\] \[f(1)~=~ 0.39\]

    You can then click on the Create Table button that shows up to the left of your first equation. By default, it will show the first 5 elements of the sequence in the table (along with the indices in the first column):

    \(x\) \(f(x)\)
    1 0.39
    2 0.886517697
    3 0.37489402421913
    4 0.873283261566687
    5 0.412365277949644

    If you click on the last row’s \(x\) value and repeatedly hit the Enter key on your keyboard, you can extend the sequence as far as you’d like.
