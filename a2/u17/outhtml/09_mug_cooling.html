  1. Question

    At \(t=0\) minutes, a cup of coffee is 78 Kelvin hotter than the surrounding air. Each minute, this amount decreases by 4.2%. After 29 minutes, how many Kelvin above surrounding-air temperature is the cup of coffee?


    Solution

    Find the scale factor.

    \[1-0.042~=~0.958\]

    Multiply the initial amount by the scale factor 29 times.

    \[78\cdot 0.958^{29}~=~22.4747239\]

    So, after 29 minutes, the coffee is only 22 Kelvin hotter than the surrounding air.
