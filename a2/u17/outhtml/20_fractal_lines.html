  1. Question


    The fractal above is composed of many line segments. The largest segment is 1 unit long. There are then 3 segments, each with a length of \(k = 0.29\). To continue the pattern, there are 9 segments with length \(k^2=0.0841\). Then, 27 segments with length \(k^3\) and 81 with length \(k^4\) etcâ€¦

    How much total length is needed to construct this fractal (with infinite depths of recursion)? Find the sum of all the lengths.

    In other words, if \(k=0.29\), evaluate the following:

    \[\sum_{i=0}^\infty(3k)^{i} ~=~ 1 + 3k + 9k^2 + 27k^3 + \ldots ~=~ ~? \]

    The tolerance is \(\pm 0.01\).


    Solution

    This summation is a geometric series. In general, if \(|r|<1\) then

    \[a + ar + ar^2 +ar^3 + ar^4 +\ldots + ar^{\infty} ~=~ \frac{a}{1-r} \]

    In our case, \(a=1\) and \(r=0.87\). Notice, to calculate the common ratio \(r\), we multiply the branching number (3) by the scaling factor (\(k=0.29\)).

    \[\frac{1}{1-0.87} = 7.6923077\]

    So, the total length of all the segments is 7.6923077 units.
