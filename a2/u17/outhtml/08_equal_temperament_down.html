08_equal_temperament_down


  1. Question

    Most modern music is written in a twelve-tone equal temperament system. Thus, when playing an descending chromatic scale, every new note’s fundamental frequency is approximately \(5.6125687\)% lower than the previous note’s.

    Typically, the A above middle C (noted as \(A_4\) in scientific pitch notation) is tuned to a frequency of 440 Hz, but throughout history this was not so standardized.

    Let’s imagine an orchestra tunes \(A_4\) to 436 Hz. What will the frequency be of the \(C_4\) that is 9 notes lower than \(A_4\)?


    Solution

    Find the scale factor.

    \[1-0.0561257~=~0.9438743\]

    The exact scale factor for twelve-tone equal temperament is \(2^{-1/12}\) (when descending by semitones).

    You multiply the given frequency by the scale factor 9 times.

    \[436\cdot 0.9438743^{9} ~=~ 259.2471511\]
