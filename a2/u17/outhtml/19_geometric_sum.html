  1. Question

    A geometric sequence has a first term 3 and a common ratio 1.2. Find the sum of the first 23 terms.

    The tolerance is \(\pm 0.01\).

    (We call the sum of a geometric sequence a geometric series.)


    Solution

    It might be helpful to reference geometric series. Identify the parameters.

    \[a = 3\]

    \[r = 1.2\]

    Define the explicit formula to find the \(n\)th term.

    \[a_n = (3)(1.2)^{(n-1)}\]

    The sum of the first 23 terms could be donated with \(S_{23}\) or with the upper-case Sigma summation operator.

    \[S_{23} = \sum_{i=1}^{23}(3)(1.2)^{(i-1)} \]

    A constant factor (multiplier) can be brought in front of a summation operator.

    \[S_{23} = 3\sum_{i=1}^{23}(1.2)^{(i-1)} \]

    Just to be clear, we are attempting to find the following sum:

    \[S_{23} = (3)\left(1+1.2+1.2^2+1.2^3+\ldots+1.2^{19}+1.2^{20}+1.2^{21}+1.2^{22}\right) \]

    If you are cool, you might multiply both sides by 1.2, and distribute to all the terms in the summation.

    \[1.2\cdot S_{23} = (3)\left(1.2+1.2^2+1.2^3+1.2^4+\ldots+1.2^{20}+1.2^{21}+1.2^{22}+1.2^{23}\right) \]

    Subtract the previous two equations and simplify by eliminating the opposite terms.

    \[S_{23} - 1.2\cdot S_{23} = (3)(1-1.2^{23})\]

    Factor out \(S_{23}\) from the terms on the left side of the equation.

    \[S_{23}(1-1.2)= (3)(1-1.2^{23})\]

    Divide both sides by \((1-1.2)\).

    \[S_{23}= \frac{(3)(1-1.2^{23})}{(1-1.2)}\]

    This process suggests the geometric series formula.

    \[S_n = \frac{a(1-r^n)}{1-r} \]

    \[S_{23}= 978.71059\]
