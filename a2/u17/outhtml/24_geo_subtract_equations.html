  1. Question

    Imagine you hope to determine the sum, \(S\), of the geometric series shown below.

    \[S~=~4243.20+3530.34+2937.24+2443.79\]

    But, since it is a geometric series, and you remember the proof of the partial geometric series formula, you take an interesting approach. You multiply the equation by the common ratio, which is \(r=0.832\).

    \[rS~=~3530.34+2937.24+2443.79+2033.23\]

    Your next step is two subtract the second equation from the first equation. What is the value of \(S-rS\)?

    \[S-rS ~=~ ?\]


    Solution

    To find the value of \(S-rS\), you should recognize that most terms show up in both equations. Because we are subtracting equations, many terms cancel out.

    \[\begin{array} ~ & S & = & 4243.20 & + & 3530.34 & + & 2937.24 & + & 2443.79 \\ \ominus & rS & = & & & 3530.34 & + & 2937.24 & + & 2443.79 & + & 2033.23\\ \hline & S-rS & = & 4243.2 & + & 0 & + & 0 & + & 0 & - & 2033.23 \end{array}\]

    Leave out the zeros.

    \[S-rS ~=~ 4243.2-2033.23\]

    Calculate the difference.

    \[S-rS ~=~ 2209.97\]
