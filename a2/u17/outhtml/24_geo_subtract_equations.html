  1. Question

    Imagine you hope to determine the sum, \(S\), of the geometric series shown below.

    \[S~=~925.60+659.03+469.23+334.09+237.87+169.36\]

    But, since it is a geometric series, and you remember the proof of the partial geometric series formula, you take an interesting approach. You multiply the equation by the common ratio, which is \(r=0.712\).

    \[rS~=~659.03+469.23+334.09+237.87+169.36+120.59\]

    Your next step is two subtract the second equation from the first equation. What is the value of \(S-rS\)?

    \[S-rS ~=~ ?\]


    Solution

    To find the value of \(S-rS\), you should recognize that most terms show up in both equations. Because we are subtracting equations, many terms cancel out.

    \[\begin{array} ~ & S & = & 925.60 & + & 659.03 & + & 469.23 & + & 334.09 & + & 237.87 & + & 169.36 \\ \ominus & rS & = & & & 659.03 & + & 469.23 & + & 334.09 & + & 237.87 & + & 169.36 & + & 120.59\\ \hline & S-rS & = & 925.6 & + & 0 & + & 0 & + & 0 & + & 0 & + & 0 & - & 120.59 \end{array}\]

    Leave out the zeros.

    \[S-rS ~=~ 925.6-120.59\]

    Calculate the difference.

    \[S-rS ~=~ 805.01\]

    And that is the answer to this problem (805.01). Of course, to find the sum, you’d factor the left side,

    \[S\cdot(1-r) ~=~ 805.01\]

    and divide both sides by \(1-r\).

    \[S ~=~ \frac{805.01}{1-r} ~=~ \frac{805.01}{1-0.712}\]
