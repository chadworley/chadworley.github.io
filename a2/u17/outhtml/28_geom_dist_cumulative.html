  1. Question

    A geometric distribution describes the probability of getting a certain number of failures in a row before succeeding when repeatedly attempting a trial with fixed chance of success.

    Imagine a trial that has, for each attempt, a \(22\%\) chance of success and a \(78\%\) chance of failure. (On each trial you either succeed or fail.) You will repeatedly attempt until you succeed.

    What is the probability of your first success occurring before your sixth attempt?


    Solution

    We (hopefully) already learned how to calculate the probability mass function of the geometric distribution. We can use \(p=0.22\) and \(q=0.78\) to represent the simple probabilities of each attempt.

    \[P(\text{first success on first attempt})~=~pq^0~=~0.22(0.78)^{0}~=~0.22\] \[P(\text{first success on second attempt})~=~pq^1~=~0.22(0.78)^{1}~=~0.1716\] \[P(\text{first success on third attempt})~=~pq^2~=~0.22(0.78)^{2}~=~0.133848\] \[P(\text{first success on fourth attempt})~=~pq^3~=~0.22(0.78)^{3}~=~0.10440144\] \[P(\text{first success on fifth attempt})~=~pq^4~=~0.22(0.78)^{4}~=~0.0814331232\]

    Each of the probabilities shown above corresponds to a mutually exclusive event. For example, if the first success was on the third attempt, then the first success was not on the fourth attempt.

    We need to find the cumulative probability. Because of mutual exclusivity, we can simply add the probabilities shown above.

    \[P(\text{first success in fewer than 6 attempts})~=~0.22(0.78)^{0}+0.22(0.78)^{1}+0.22(0.78)^{2}+0.22(0.78)^{3}+0.22(0.78)^{4}\]

    Notice this is a geometric series. The first term is \(a=0.22\). The common ratio is \(r=0.78\). The number of terms is \(n=5\). We wish to find the sum (\(s\)) of the series. The following steps are meant to remind you of the steps in the proof of the partial geometric series formula, but shown with specific numbers.

    \[s~=~0.22(0.78)^{0}+0.22(0.78)^{1}+0.22(0.78)^{2}+0.22(0.78)^{3}+0.22(0.78)^{4}\]

    Multiply both sides by the common ratio.

    \[0.78s~=~0.22(0.78)^{1}+0.22(0.78)^{2}+0.22(0.78)^{3}+0.22(0.78)^{4}+0.22(0.78)^{5}\]

    Subtract the equations.

    \[s-0.78s~=~0.22-0.22(0.78)^{5}\]

    Solve for the sum by factoring and dividing.

    \[s~=~\frac{0.22-0.22(0.78)^{5}}{1-0.78}\] \[s~=~0.7112826\]

    You could generalize your results to give a formula for the cumulative geometric distribution.

    \[P(\text{first success before }n+1\text{ attempts})~=~\sum_{i=0}^{n-1}pq^i ~=~ \frac{p-pq^n}{1-q}\]

    I should note that you could greatly simplify the formula because we know that \(p+q=1\), but my primary goal is to make a connection to the partial geometric series formula.

    \[s~=~\frac{a-ar^n}{1-r}\]
