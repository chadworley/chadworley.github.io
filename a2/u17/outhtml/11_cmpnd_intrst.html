  1. Question

    An investment earns compound interest with periodic compounding.

    • Principal = \(P=470\) USD
    • Nominal annual interest rate = \(r~=~0.09 ~=~9\%\)
    • Compounding frequency = \(n=3\) per year
    • Time = \(t=30\) years

    Calculate the amount, in USD, after 30 years, using the formula below:

    \[A = P\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}\] For the final amount, you can round to the nearest dollar. (Tolerance is \(\pm 1\))


    Solution

    Notice the definition of \(r\) is a bit tricky. Really, 3 times per year the money grows by multiplication by \(\left(1+\frac{0.09}{3}\right)\) which equals 1.03. Over 30 years, this multiplication occurs 90 times.

    \[A ~=~ 470 \cdot (1.03)^{90} ~=~ 6721.22\]

    Each year, the money actually grows by multiplying by \(\left(1+\frac{0.09}{3}\right)^{3}\), which equals 1.092727, which is more than the \(1.09\) you might expect from \(r\). In the US, credit cards will often state an APR, but compound monthly (or even daily!), so the effective interest rate is higher (if you allow the debt to linger).
