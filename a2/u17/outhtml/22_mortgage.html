  1. Question

    A mortgage is a loan. Every month interest accrues, but then a payment covers the interest plus a bit of the principal. The bank calculates how much you need to pay each month to pay off the loan in a specific amount of time.

    Imagine a loan with a principal of $489000 and an interest rate of 7.44%. Based your chosen timeline, the bank determines you should pay $3400 per month. We can simulate the loan (in Desmos) using a recursively defined function.

    \[f(x)~~=~~\left(1+\frac{0.0744}{12}\right)\cdot f(x-1)~-~3400\]

    \[f(0)~=~4.89\cdot 10^{5}\]

    where \(f(x)\) represents the remaining debt after \(x\) months of payments.

    Find how much debt remains after 53 months.

    \[f(53)~=~?\]


    Solution

    Use Desmos to iterate the recursive definition.

    You might be interested to extend the simulation to \(x=360\) to check that the mortgage is indeed paid off in 30 years.
