06_exponential_population


  1. Question

    When organisms reproduce during abundance without predators, disease, or other culling factors, exponential population growth is expected. Common organisms regularly cultured in laboratories, like Escherichia coli and Saccharomyces cerevisiae, can exhibit this type of exponential population growth.

    Under some conditions, a culture of E. coli is expected to increase its population size by 1.62% every minute. If at \(t=0\) minutes there are 1000 cells, how many cells do you predict there will be after 120 minutes?


    Solution

    Find the scale factor.

    \[1+0.0162 ~=~ 1.0162\]

    Multiply the initial population size by the scale factor 120 times.

    \[1000 \cdot 1.0162^{120} ~=~ 6878.6445627\]

    If we use \(r=0.0162\), we can write a formula to find the population at time \(t\) (\(P_t\)) from the initial population at time 0 (\(P_0\)), and the growth rate \(r\).

    \[P_t~=~P_0\cdot (1+r)^{t}\]
