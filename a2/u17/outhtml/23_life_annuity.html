  1. Question

    For various reasons, a dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow. (See inflation and time value of money.)

    The present value of money is often modeled as exponentially dependent on the amount of time between now and when the money is received.

    A life annuity is a series of payments made at equal intervals until the death of the recipient. We can calculate the present value of an annuity by adding together the present value of every payment.

    A human is considering an annuity that pays $1400 every year (as a single payment at the end of the year). The recipient will live another 26 years. This human’s present values of those payments follow a geometric progression.

    present value future payment
    $1297.80 now is equally enjoyable as $1400 in 1 year
    $1203.06 now is equally enjoyable as $1400 in 2 years
    $1115.24 now is equally enjoyable as $1400 in 3 years
    $1033.82 now is equally enjoyable as $1400 in 4 years
    $958.36 now is equally enjoyable as $1400 in 5 years
    \(\vdots\) \(\vdots\) \(\vdots\)
    $195.07 now is equally enjoyable as $1400 in 26 years

    To calculate how much this annuity is worth to this human, please calculate the sum of the present values.


    Solution

    Remember, the sum (\(S\)) of a partial geometric series is determined by its initial value (\(a\)), its common ratio (\(r\)), and the number of terms (\(n\)).

    \[S~=~a+ar+ar^2+\cdots+ar^{n-3}+ar^{n-2}+ar^{n-1}\]

    In the given problem, it is easy to determine \(a=1297.8\) and \(n=26\). However, we need to calculate the common ratio.

    \[\frac{1203.06}{1297.8}~=~0.9269995\] \[\frac{1115.24}{1203.06}~=~0.9270028\] \[\frac{1033.82}{1115.24}~=~0.9269933\] \[\frac{958.36}{1033.82}~=~0.9270086\]

    It appears the common ratio is approximately \(r=0.927\). We now have all the parameters that determine the sum. We’ve learned two methods to calculate the sum:

    1. Use the partial geometric series formula. \[S~=~\frac{a-ar^n}{1-r}~=~\frac{1297.8-1297.8(0.927)^{26}}{1-0.927}\]
    2. Use the summation operator to ask Desmos to calculate the sum of the series.

    \[S~=~\sum_{i=0}^{n-1}ar^{i}~=~\sum_{i=0}^{26-1}1297.8(0.927)^{i}\]

    Either way, you should get that the present value of the life annuity is $15300.9.
