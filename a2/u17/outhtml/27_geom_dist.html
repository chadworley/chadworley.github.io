  1. Question

    A geometric distribution describes the probability of getting a certain number of failures in a row before succeeding when repeatedly attempting a trial with fixed chance of success.

    Imagine a trial that has, for each attempt, a \(11\%\) chance of success and a \(89\%\) chance of failure. (On each trial you either succeed or fail.) You will repeatedly attempt until you succeed.

    What is the probability of your first success occurring on your fifth attempt?


    Solution

    In order for your first success to come on the fifth attempt, you need to fail 4 times and then succeed.

    So, you need to fail on the first attempt, fail on the second attempt, fail on the third attempt, fail on the fourth attempt, and then succeed on the fifth attempt.

    Each attempt is independent. This means the compound event’s probability is the product of the basic events’ probabilities.

    \[P(\text{fail first trial})~=~0.89\] \[P(\text{fail second trial})~=~0.89\] \[P(\text{fail third trial})~=~0.89\] \[P(\text{fail fourth trial})~=~0.89\] \[P(\text{succeed fifth attempt})~=~0.11\]

    \[P(\text{fail on first 4 attempts and succeed on fifth attempt})~=~(0.89)^{4}(0.11)^1\]

    \[P(\text{fail on first 4 attempts and succeed on fifth attempt})~=~0.0690165\]

    So the probability of having the first success be on the fifth attempt is 6.9016465%, which is 0.0690165 as a decimal probability.
