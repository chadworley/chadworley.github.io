  1. Question

    When a linear binomial is cubed, the result is a cubic polynomial. Express the polynomial below in standard form.

    \[r(x)~=~\left({-9}x-{4}\right)^3\]

    • \(r(x)~=\) \(x^3\) \(x^2\) \(x\)


    Solution

    Just like with integers, to cube a polynomial, you can first find the square and then multiply again. So, in our example, we can rewrite \(r(x)\).

    \[r(x) ~=~ ({-9}x-{4})\cdot({-9}x-{4})\cdot({-9}x-{4})\]

    \[r(x) ~=~ ({-9}x-{4})^2\cdot({-9}x-{4})\]

    You can first expand \((-9x+-4)^2\).

    * \(-9x\) \(-4\)
    \(-9x\) \(81x^2\) \(36x\)
    \(-4\) \(36x\) \(16\)

    \[r(x) ~=~ \left({81}x^{2}+{36}x+{36}x+{16}\right)\cdot({-9}x-{4})\]

    Combine like terms.

    \[r(x)~=~ \left({81}x^{2}+{72}x+{16}\right)\cdot({-9}x-{4})\]

    Now multiply the quadratic by the linear.

    * \(-9x\) \(-4\)
    \(81x^2\) \(-729x^3\) \(-324x^2\)
    \(72x\) \(-648x^2\) \(-288x\)
    \(16\) \(-144x\) \(-64\)

    \[r(x)~~=~~ {-729}x^{3}-{324}x^{2}-{648}x^{2}-{288}x-{144}x-{64}\]

    Combine like terms.

    \[r(x)~~=~~ {-729}x^{3}-{972}x^{2}-{432}x-{64}\]

    Quicker strategy:

    You might find the binomial theorem helpful. In this case, it promises that:

    \[(ax+b)^3 ~~\equiv~~ \left(a^3\right)x^3 ~+~ \left(3a^2b\right)x^2 ~+~ \left(3ab^2\right)x ~+~ \left(b^3\right) \]

    Notice: \[(-9)^3 ~=~ -729\] \[3\cdot(-9)^2\cdot(-4) ~=~ -972\] \[3\cdot(-9)\cdot(-4)^2 ~=~ -432\] \[(-4)^3 ~=~ -64\]
