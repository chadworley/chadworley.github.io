  1. Question

    Below is a polynomial of a single variable, \(x\), written in standard form.

    \[P(x)={-6}x^{4}+x^{3}-{3}x-{9}\]

    A polynomial is composed by adding (and subtracting) together multiple terms. Each term has the form \(ax^b\), where \(a\) is the coefficient and \(b\) is the degree, which must be a nonnegative integer. Some special cases are simplified. If \(a=1\) or \(a=-1\), then the 1 will not be explicitly shown. If \(a=0\), then the whole term will not be shown. Similarly, \(x^1\) simplifies to \(x\) and \(x^0\) simplifies to 1. (Technically \(0^0\) is undefined, but letâ€™s ignore that for now.)

    Please identify the coefficients of the terms.

    Term Degree Coefficient
    quartic 4
    cubic 3
    quadratic 2
    linear 1
    constant 0




    Solution

    It might help to see the polynomial in unsimplified form.

    \[(-6)x^{4}+(1)x^{3}+(0)x^{2}+(-3)x^{1}+(-9)x^{0}\]

    Term Degree Coefficient
    quartic 4 -6
    cubic 3 1
    quadratic 2 0
    linear 1 -3
    constant 0 -9
