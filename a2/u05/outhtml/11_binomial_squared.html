  1. Question

    A polynomial, \(r(x)\), is shown as the product of two binomials. Use the FOIL method to expand the expression.

    \[r(x) ~=~ \left({-3}x^{4}-{4}x^{2}\right)\cdot \left({-3}x^{4}-{4}x^{2}\right)\]

    Which option below shows \(r(x)\) in standard form (expanded, with highest degree to lowest degree)?


    1. \({9}x^{8}+{16}x^{4}\)
    2. \({9}x^{8}+{24}x^{6}+{16}x^{4}\)
    3. \({9}x^{4}+{16}x^{2}\)
    4. \({9}x^{8}+{12}x^{6}+{16}x^{4}\)
    5. \({-9}x^{16}+{16}x^{4}\)

    Solution

    * \(-3x^{4}\) \(-4x^{2}\)
    \(-3x^{4}\) \(9x^{8}\) \(12x^{6}\)
    \(-4x^{2}\) \(12x^{6}\) \(16x^{4}\)

    \(r(x) = {9}x^{8}+{12}x^{6}+{12}x^{6}+{16}x^{4}\)

    Combine the like terms.

    \(r(x) = {9}x^{8}+{24}x^{6}+{16}x^{4}\)

    Notice that this is a special case of generating a perfect-square trinomial, like those used to complete the square.

    \[r(x) ~=~ \left({-3}x^{4}-{4}x^{2}\right)^2 ~=~ {9}x^{8}+{24}x^{6}+{16}x^{4}\]
