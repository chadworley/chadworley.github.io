  1. Question

    When a linear binomial is raised to the fourth power, the result is a quartic polynomial. Express the polynomial below in standard form.

    \[r(x)~=~\left({-2}x-{5}\right)^4\]

    • \(r(x)~=\) \(x^4\) \(x^3\) \(x^2\) \(x\)


    Solution

    You could first find that \(({-2}x-{5})^2~=~{4}x^{2}+{20}x+{25}\) and then find \(\left({4}x^{2}+{20}x+{25}\right)^2\). But, we want to get comfortable using the binomial theorem.

    When raising a linear binomial to the fourth, the binomial theorem promises that:

    \[(ax+b)^4 ~~\equiv~~ \left(a^4\right)x^4 ~+~ \left(4a^3b\right)x^3 ~+~ \left(6a^2b^2\right)x^2 ~+~ \left(4ab^3\right)x ~+~ \left(b^4\right) \]

    Notice: \[(-2)^4 ~=~ 16\] \[4\cdot(-2)^3\cdot(-5) ~=~ 160\] \[6\cdot(-2)^2\cdot(-5)^2 ~=~ 600\] \[4\cdot(-2)\cdot(-5)^3 ~=~ 1000\] \[(-5)^4 ~=~ 625\]

    Thus,

    \[r(x)~=~ {16}x^{4}+{160}x^{3}+{600}x^{2}+{1000}x+{625}\]
