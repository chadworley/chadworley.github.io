  1. Question

    Consider the two polynomial functions below:

    \[p(x) ~=~ {-2}x^{4}+x^{3}-{8}x^{2}-{9}x-{1}\] \[q(x) ~=~ {9}x^{6}+{4}x^{5}-{9}x^{4}+{9}x^{2}+{1}\]

    Let polynomial \(r(x)\) equal the sum of polynomials \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\):

    \[r(x) = p(x)+q(x)\]

    The resulting polynomial can be expressed using \(A\), \(B\), \(C\), \(D\), \(E\), \(F\), and \(G\) to represent the coefficients.

    \[r(x) ~=~ {A}x^{6}+{B}x^{5}+{C}x^{4}+{D}x^{3}+{E}x^{2}+{F}x+{G}\]

    Find the value of each coefficient. Notice they might be negative or 0.

    • The sextic coefficient, \(A=\)
    • The quintic coefficient, \(B=\)
    • The quartic coefficient, \(C=\)
    • The cubic coefficient, \(D=\)
    • The quadratic coefficient, \(E=\)
    • The linear coefficient, \(F=\)
    • The constant coefficient, \(G=\)


    Solution

    You might find it helpful to put \(p\) and \(q\) in unsimplified form.

    \[p(x)=(0)x^{6}+(0)x^{5}+(-2)x^{4}+(1)x^{3}+(-8)x^{2}+(-9)x^{1}+(-1)x^{0}\] \[q(x)=(9)x^{6}+(4)x^{5}+(-9)x^{4}+(0)x^{3}+(9)x^{2}+(0)x^{1}+(1)x^{0}\]

    Add the coefficients of the like terms. \[r(x)=(9)x^{6}+(4)x^{5}+(-11)x^{4}+(1)x^{3}+(1)x^{2}+(-9)x^{1}+(0)x^{0}\]

    Thus, we get our answers:

    • The sextic coefficient, \(A=9\)
    • The quintic coefficient, \(B=4\)
    • The quartic coefficient, \(C=-11\)
    • The cubic coefficient, \(D=1\)
    • The quadratic coefficient, \(E=1\)
    • The linear coefficient, \(F=-9\)
    • The constant coefficient, \(G=0\)

    Most people would simplify the result.

    \[r(x)={9}x^{6}+{4}x^{5}-{11}x^{4}+x^{3}+x^{2}-{9}x\]
