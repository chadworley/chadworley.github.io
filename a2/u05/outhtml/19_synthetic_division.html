  1. Question

    Let polynomial \(p(x)=- 7 x^{3} + 5 x^{2} + 2 x\).

    Use synthetic division to divide \(p(x)\) by \(x + 2\).



    Solution

    Begin by filling the top row with the coefficients of the polynomial. Remember to use 0 whenever a term is missing.

    Then, since the denominator is \(x + 2\), fill the leftmost element with \(-2\).

    -7 5 2 0
    -2

    Drop the leading coefficient to the bottom row.

    -7 5 2 0
    -2
    -7

    Multiply the -2 by the -7 to get 14.

    -7 5 2 0
    -2 14
    -7

    Add 5 and 14 to get 19.

    -7 5 2 0
    -2 14
    -7 19

    Continue to alternate between multiplication (by -2) and addition.

    -7 5 2 0
    -2 14 -38 72
    -7 19 -36 72

    The last number is called the remainder. One way to express the answer is with an identity (an equation thatâ€™s true for all \(x\)):

    \[\frac{- 7 x^{3} + 5 x^{2} + 2 x}{x + 2} ~=~ - 7 x^{2} + 19 x - 36 + \frac{72}{x + 2}\]

    From the remainder theorem, we also know that \(p(-2) = 72\). This can be seen by multiplying both sides by x + 2 and pluggling in \(x=-2\).

    \[p(x) ~=~ - 7 x^{3} + 5 x^{2} + 2 x ~=~ (- 7 x^{2} + 19 x - 36)(x + 2) + (72) \]

    \[p(-2) ~=~ - 7 (-2)^{3} + 5 (-2)^{2} + 2 (-2) ~=~ (\ldots)(0) + (72) \]

    \[p(-2) ~=~ 72\]
