  1. Question

    Let function \(f\) be defined as follows: \[f(x) ~=~ - 5 x^{3} - 2 x^{2} + 5 x - 4\] Which of the following expressions is equivalent to \(-f(a)\)?


    1. \(5 a^{3} + 2 a^{2} - 5 a + 4\)
    2. \(- 5 a^{3} + 2 a^{2} + 5 a + 4\)
    3. \(5 a^{3} - 2 a^{2} - 5 a - 4\)
    4. \(- 5 a^{3} - 2 a^{2} + 5 a - 4\)

    Solution

    Substitute \(a\) for \(x\) and multiply the whole expression by \((-1)\).

    \[-f(a) ~=~ 5 a^{3} + 2 a^{2} - 5 a + 4\]
