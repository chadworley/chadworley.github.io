  1. Question

    A small sample was gathered. The measurements are below.

    \(x_i\)
    74
    74
    74
    62

    Please calculate the sample standard deviation. To do this, first calculate the sample mean.

    \[\bar{x} = \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^n x_i}{n}\]

    Calculate the sample variance by dividing the sum of the squared deviations by \(n-1\). This subtlety (of replacing the denominator with \(n-1\) instead of \(n\)) is called Bessel’s correction. This provides an unbiased estimation of the population’s variance when the population’s mean is also unknown. \[\text{sample variance (with Bessel correction)} = s^2 = \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^n (x_i-\bar{x})^2}{n-1} \]

    Determine the sample standard deviation by taking the square root of the sample variance. \[s = \sqrt{s^2} \]


    Solution

    Find the sample mean. \[\bar{x} = \frac{74+74+74+62}{4} = 71 \]

    Find the variance.

    \[s^2 = \frac{(74-71)^2+(74-71)^2+(74-71)^2+(62-71)^2}{3} \]

    \[s^2 = \frac{(3)^2+(3)^2+(3)^2+(-9)^2}{3}\]

    \[s^2 = \frac{9+9+9+81}{3}\]

    \[s^2 = 36 \] Calculate the standard deviation. \[s = \sqrt{36} = 6 \]

    You might find it helpful to organize your work in a table.

    \(x_i\) \((x_i-\bar{x})\) \((x_i-\bar{x})^2\)
    74 68 9
    74 68 9
    74 68 9
    62 56 81
    \(n\) \(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} (x_i-\bar{x})^2\)
    4 108
    \(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} x_i\) \(s^2=\frac{1}{n-1}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} (x_i-\bar{x})^2\)
    284 36
    \(\bar{x}\) \(s\)
    71 6
