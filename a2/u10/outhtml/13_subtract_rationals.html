  1. Question

    Subtracting rational expressions is similar to subtracting fractions: specifically, a common denominator can be helpful when subtracting rational expressions. Find the parameters \(a\), \(b\), \(c\), and \(d\) such that the identity below is equivalent for any value of \(x\).

    \[\frac{3}{x-2}-\frac{6}{x-5} ~\equiv~ \frac{ax+b}{x^2+cx+d}\]

    • \(a=\)
    • \(b=\)
    • \(c=\)
    • \(d=\)


    Solution

    The given expression:

    \[\frac{3}{x-2}-\frac{6}{x-5}\]

    To get a common denominator, multiply the first rational fraction by \(\frac{x-5}{x-5}\) and the second rational fraction by \(\frac{x-2}{x-2}\).

    \[\frac{3\cdot (x-5)}{(x-2)(x-5)}-\frac{6\cdot(x-2)}{(x-5)(x-2)}\]

    Expand both numerators and both denominators.

    \[\frac{3x-15}{x^2-7x+10}-\frac{6x-12}{x^2-7x+10}\]

    Now, since we have common denominators, we can subtract the numerators.

    \[\frac{{-3}x-3}{x^2-{7}x+10}\]

    This form of the expression matches our goal. To enforce the identity,

    \[a=-3\] \[b=-3\] \[c=-7\] \[d=10\]
