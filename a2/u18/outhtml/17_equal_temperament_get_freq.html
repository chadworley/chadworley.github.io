17_equal_temperament_get_freq


  1. Question

    In music, an equal temperament implies the available fundamental frequencies follow a geometric progression. Imagine a keyboard instrument for 41 EDO. That means there are 41 frequencies per octave, and the ratio between neighboring frequencies is \(2^{1/41} \approx 1.0170497\). That instrument acts as a function that maps key number (\(k\)) to frequency (\(f\)). (Notice, I am NOT referring to the key or the key signature; I am referring to the “buttons” of a keyboard as keys.)

    Let’s assume a concert pitch of 440 Hz is assigned to the key number 132, so we can calculate the frequency from any key number using the following exponential function.

    \[f = 440 \cdot 2^{(k-132)/41} \]

    What is the frequency, in Hz, of key number \(k=\) 189? The tolerance is \(\pm 1\) Hz.


    Solution

    \[f = 440 \cdot 2^{(189-132)/41}\]

    \[f = 440 \cdot 2^{57/41}\]

    \[f = 1153.3411631\]

    1153.3411631 Hz
