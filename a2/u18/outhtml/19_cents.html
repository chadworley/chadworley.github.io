  1. Question

    In music, to compare two frequencies (to measure an interval), we can use cents.

    \[I = 1200 \cdot \log_2 \left(\frac{f_2}{f_1}\right)\]

    where \(I\) is the size of the interval in cents, \(f_1\) is the first frequency (typically in hertz), and \(f_2\) is the second frequency.

    A pianist plays \(A_{2}\) and \(D_{3}\). On a standard 88-key piano, these notes correspond to key number \(k_1=25\) and \(k_2=30\), and their frequencies are \(f_1\approx 110\) Hz and \(f_2\approx 146.832384\) Hz.


    Find \(I\), the size of the interval in cents.


    Solution

    Use the formula.

    \[I ~=~ 1200 \cdot \log_2\left(\frac{146.832384}{110}\right) ~=~ 500\]

    If your calculator does not allow logs with base 2, you can use the change-of-base identity.

    \[I ~=~ 1200 \cdot \frac{\log\left(\frac{146.832384}{110}\right)}{\log(2)}~=~ 500\]
