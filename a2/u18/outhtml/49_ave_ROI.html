  1. Question

    An investor puts $670 into an index fund. Over 10 years, the value of the index fund has changed as shown in the table below:

    Years after initial investment (\(x\)) Value of the investment (\(y\))
    0 670.00
    1 729.27
    2 755.43
    3 824.51
    4 853.88
    5 882.61
    6 942.53
    7 1069.09
    8 1088.88
    9 1160.40
    10 1287.12

    The investor wants to fit these data to an exponential growth curve to estimate the expected yearly ROI of the index fund. Use Desmos to fit an exponential model using default settings (\(y_1\sim a\cdot b^{x_1}\), NOT in Log Mode).

    Based on the equation of best fit, what percent increase should we expect per year? (For an answer of 54.3% increase per year, please answer with 54.3.)


    Solution

    Copy/paste data into Desmos.

    Fit the data with an exponential regression.

    The default settings should give \(y=666.132\cdot1.06541^x\). Notice the first parameter is near the initial amount of money invested. The second parameter, \(b\), is important for answering our question. Right now, \(b=1.06541\) is the yearly scale factor, or multiplier. To get percent increase, subtract 1 and then multiply by 100.

    \[\mathrm{expected ~ annual ~ ROI}=6.54\%\]
