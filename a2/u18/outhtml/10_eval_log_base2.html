  1. Question

    • \(\log_{2}(4)=\)

    • \(\log_{2}(1024)=\)

    • \(\log_{2}(16)=\)

    • \(\log_{2}(512)=\)

    • \(\log_{2}(32)=\)



    Solution

    • \(2^{2}=4\) therefore \(\log_{2}(4)=2\)
    • \(2^{10}=1024\) therefore \(\log_{2}(1024)=10\)
    • \(2^{4}=16\) therefore \(\log_{2}(16)=4\)
    • \(2^{9}=512\) therefore \(\log_{2}(512)=9\)
    • \(2^{5}=32\) therefore \(\log_{2}(32)=5\)
