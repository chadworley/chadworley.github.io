  1. Question

    At \(t=0\) a population is \(18.1\) million. The population decays exponentially, following the exponential decay equation below where \(t\) has units of years, \(y\) has units of millions, and \(k=0.32\) has units of years\(^{-1}\).

    \[y = 18.1e^{-0.32t}\]

    What is the half life (in years)? (The tolerance is \(\pm 0.2\) years.)


    Solution

    You can graph the function. Find where the graph reaches half the initial value.


    The half life is 2.1660849 years.

    You could also use algebra to find \(t\) when the population reaches half the original amount.

    \[\frac{18.1}{2}=18.1e^{-0.32t}\]

    To both sides of the equation you’d divide by 18.1, take natural log, and divide by -0.32.
