20_f2_from_cents


  1. Question

    Remember the way to calculate an interval in cents. \[I = 1200\cdot \log_2\left(\frac{f_2}{f_1}\right)\]

    with \(I\) as the (signed) interval in cents, \(f_1\) as the first frequency in hertz, and \(f_2\) as the second frequency in hertz. We can solve the formula for \(f_2\) because logarithms and exponentials are inverses.

    \[f_2 = f_1 \cdot 2^{I/1200}\]

    A musician plays two notes whose pitches are determined by their fundamental frequencies. One note has a fundamental frequency of \(f_1=1174.6590717\) Hz. The second fundamental frequency is 6200 cents under. What is the second fundamental frequency?


    Solution

    Use the formula. Because \(f_2\) is 6200 cents under \(f_1\), then \(I=-6200\).

    \[f_2 = 1174.6590717 \cdot 2^{-6200/1200} \]

    \[f_2=32.7031957\]
