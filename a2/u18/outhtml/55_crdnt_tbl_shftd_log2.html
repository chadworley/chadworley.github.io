  1. Question

    Consider the following points on \(f(x)=\log_2(x)\):

    \(x\) \(y=f(x)\)
    1 0
    2 1
    4 2
    8 3
    16 4

    Another function, \(g\), is created by shifting function \(f\) horizontally and vertically.

    \[g(x)~=~\log_{2}(x-9)-5\]

    Fill out the corresponding points on the \(y=g(x)\) curve.

    \(x\) \(y=g(x)\)

    Also, \(g(x)\) has a at .



    Solution

    We have shifted horizontally by \(9\) and vertically by \(-5\).

    \(x\) \(y=g(x)\)
    10 -5
    11 -4
    13 -3
    17 -2
    25 -1

    Also, \(g(x)\) has a vertical asymptote at \(y=9\).
