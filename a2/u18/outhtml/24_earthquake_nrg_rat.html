  1. Question

    When two separate earthquakes occur, we are often interested in comparing their energies as a ratio (to say how many times more energy the larger one released). Wikipedia provided me with the following formula to do this:

    \[\frac{E_2}{E_1}~=~10^{1.5(M_2-M_1)}\]

    If the smaller earthquake had a magnitude of 7.4 and the larger earthquake had a magnitude of 7.9, then how many times larger was the energy released by the larger earthquake than the smaller earthquake?


    Solution

    Plug the numbers into the equation.

    \[\frac{E_2}{E_1}~=~10^{1.5(7.9-7.4)}\]

    Evaluate.

    \[\frac{E_2}{E_1}~=~5.6234133\]

    Notice, \(E_2~=~(5.6234133)\cdot E_1\), so the larger energy is 5.6234133 times the smaller energy.
