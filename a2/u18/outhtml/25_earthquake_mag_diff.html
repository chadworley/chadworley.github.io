  1. Question

    When two separate earthquakes occur, we are often interested in comparing their energies as a ratio (to say how many times more energy the larger one released). Wikipedia provided me with the following formula to do this:

    \[\frac{E_2}{E_1}~=~10^{1.5(M_2-M_1)}\]

    If the second earthquake’s energy was \(5.01\cdot 10^{8}\) times the first earthquake’s energy, then what was the (absolute) difference in magnitudes?


    Solution

    You’ve been given the ratio of energies. Replace with ratio of energies with the given number.

    \[5.01\cdot 10^{8}~=~10^{1.5(M_2-M_1)}\]

    The inverse of \(f(x)=10^x\) is \(f^{-1}(x)=\log_{10}(x)\). So, to undo the “10 to the”, we apply “log10 of” to both sides.

    \[\log_{10}\left(5.01\cdot 10^{8}\right)~=~\log_{10}\left(10^{1.5(M_2-M_1)}\right)\]

    We know that \(\log_B(B^x)\equiv x\). You can see the relevant identity under “Cancelling exponentials” in wikipedia’s list of logarithmic identities.

    \[\log_{10}\left(5.01\cdot 10^{8}\right)~=~1.5(M_2-M_1)\]

    Divide both sides by 1.5.

    \[\frac{\log_{10}\left(5.01\cdot 10^{8}\right)}{1.5}~=~M_2-M_1\]

    Use a calculator.

    \[M_2-M_1~=~5.7998918\]

    You can round to the tenths place.

    \[M_2-M_1~=~5.8\]

    So, the difference in magnitudes is approximately 5.8.
