  1. Question

    When the ratio between two fundamental frequencies is near 3:2, musicians call the interval a “fifth”, named from playing the first and fifth notes in a classical diatonic scale. Somewhat annoyingly, equal temperament does not allow for a ratio of exactly 3:2. Some equal temperaments get closer than others. Let’s investigate how close 37 EDO can get to an exact 3:2 ratio of frequencies. We will report the “distance” in cents.


    First, determine the interval (in cents) of an exact 3:2 ratio.

    \[I_\text{exact} ~=~ 1200\cdot\log_2\left(\frac{3}{2}\right) ~=~ 701.9550009\]

    Determine the best fret on the 37-EDO guitar to approximate a 3:2 ratio (after playing an open string) by rounding a logarithmic function.

    \[x ~=~ \text{round} \left(37\cdot \log_2\left(\frac{3}{2}\right) \right)\] Then, determine the ratio of frequencies between an open string and the \(x\)th fret on the 37-EDO guitar. The ratio should be near 1.5, but a bit off.

    \[r = 2^{x/37}\]

    Calculate the cents of the interval between the open string and the \(x\)th fret.

    \[I_{\text{fret}} = 1200 \cdot \log_2(r) \]

    Finally, determine the absolute difference.

    \[\text{answer} = \left|I_{\text{fret}} - I_{\text{exact}}\right|\]

    Your answer can be rounded to the nearest cent.


    Solution

    Use the formulas. First find the fret.

    \[x ~=~ \text{round} \left(37\cdot \log_2(1.5) \right) ~=~ 22\]

    To approximate a 3:2 ratio, the musician could play a string open and then the same string on fret number 22. Find the ratio of frequencies.

    \[r ~=~ 2^{22/37} ~=~ 1.5100482\] When the musician plays a string open and then on fret number 22, the ratio of frequencies is 1.5100482. Get the interval between open and fret in cents.

    \[I_{\text{fret}} ~=~ 1200 \cdot \log_2(1.5100482) ~=~ 713.5135135\]

    It should be pointed out that due to logarithmic identities, we could calculate \(I_{\text{fret}}\) in a more straightforward manner.

    \[I_{\text{fret}} ~=~ 1200 \cdot \frac{22}{37} ~=~ 713.5135135\]

    A ratio of 1.5100482 is equivalent to 713.5135135 cents. We can get the final answer by taking the absolute difference of the two intervals.

    \[\Big|701.9550009 - 713.5135135\Big| = 11.5585126\]
