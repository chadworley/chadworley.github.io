    \[20x^2-64x-21~=~0\]

    You could, with enough guess-and-check, factor this expression. However, since this quadratic has a composite (non-prime) leading coefficient and composite constant, I’d recommend using the quadratic formula. Start by identifying the parameters.

    \[a~=~20\] \[b~=~-64\] \[c~=~-21\]

    \[x~=~\frac{64 \pm \sqrt{4096-(-1680)}}{40}\]

    \[x~=~\frac{64 \pm \sqrt{5776}}{40}\]

    \[x~=~\frac{64 \pm 76}{40}\]

    Split the \(\pm\) into \(-\) and \(+\).

    \[\begin{aligned} x~&=~\frac{-12}{40} & \mathrm{or}&& x~&=~\frac{140}{40} \end{aligned}\]

    Simplify the fractions.

    \[\begin{aligned} x~&=~\frac{-3}{10} & \mathrm{or}&& x~&=~\frac{7}{2} \end{aligned}\]

    I’ll point out that if you had guess-and-checked the factored form, you’d get:
