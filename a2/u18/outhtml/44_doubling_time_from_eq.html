  1. Question

    At \(t=0\) a population is \(4.2\) million. The population grows exponentially, following the exponential growth equation below where \(t\) has units of years, \(y\) has units of millions, and \(k=0.23\) has units of years\(^{-1}\).

    \[y = 4.2e^{0.23t}\]

    What is the doubling time (in years)? (The tolerance is \(\pm 0.2\) years.)


    Solution

    You can graph the function. Find where the graph reaches twice the initial value.


    The doubling time is 3.0136834 years.

    You could also use algebra to the time, \(t\), when the population is twice the initial value.

    \[2\cdot 4.2 ~=~ 4.2e^{0.23t}\]
