  1. Question

    Find all solutions to the equation:

    \[\frac{2^{x^2}\cdot2^{18}}{2^{11x}}~=~1\]


    1. -12
    2. -11
    3. -10
    4. -9
    5. -8
    6. -7
    7. -6
    8. -5
    9. -4
    10. -3
    11. -2
    12. -1
    13. 0
    14. 1
    15. 2
    16. 3
    17. 4
    18. 5
    19. 6
    20. 7
    21. 8
    22. 9
    23. 10
    24. 11
    25. 12

    Solution

    On the left side of the equation, there are two useful rules to get a single base.

    \[b^m \cdot b^n ~\equiv~b^{m+n}\] \[\frac{b^m}{b^n} ~\equiv~b^{m-n}\]

    Use those rules as needed to simplify the left side of the equation.

    \[2^{x^2-11x+18}=1\]

    Now, also, use the rule \(b^0\equiv 1\) to get a common base on the right side.

    \[2^{x^2-11x+18}=2^0\]

    Use the rule that if \(b^a = b^c\) then \(a=c\) to claim the exponents are equal.

    \[x^2-11x+18=0\]

    You can use the quadratic formula. However, this quadratic polynomial factors easily. Remember, you are looking for two numbers with a sum of -11 and a product of 18. Those two number are -9 and -2.

    \[(x-9)(x-2)=0\]

    Use the zero-product property to find the solutions.

    \[x=9~~~~\text{or}~~~~x=2\]
