15_solve_exponential


  1. Question

    Consider the exponential equation below.

    \[y~=~4\cdot 5^{2x}\]

    Solve for \(x\).


    1. \(x=\frac{\log_{2}\left(\frac{y}{4}\right)}{5}\)
    2. \(x=\frac{\log_{4}\left(\frac{y}{2}\right)}{5}\)
    3. \(x=\frac{\log_{4}\left(\frac{y}{5}\right)}{2}\)
    4. \(x=\frac{\log_{5}\left(\frac{y}{2}\right)}{4}\)
    5. \(x=\frac{\log_{2}\left(\frac{y}{5}\right)}{4}\)
    6. \(x=\frac{\log_{5}\left(\frac{y}{4}\right)}{2}\)

    Solution

    Start with the given equation.

    \[y~=~4\cdot 5^{2x}\]

    Divide both sides by 4. (Division is the inverse of multiplication.)

    \[\frac{y}{4}~=~\frac{4\cdot 5^{2x}}{4}\]

    Simplify the right side.

    \[\frac{y}{4}~=~5^{2x}\]

    The inverse of exponentiation with base 5 is logarithm with base 5. So, we take the log, base 5, of both sides.

    \[\log_{5}\left(\frac{y}{4}\right)~=~\log_{5}\left(5^{2x}\right)\]

    We know, from our logarithm rules, that \(\log_B(B^z)\equiv z\). In our case, \(B=5\) and \(z=2x\). Simplify the right side.

    \[\log_{5}\left(\frac{y}{4}\right)~=~2x\]

    Divide both sides by 2.

    \[\frac{\log_{5}\left(\frac{y}{4}\right)}{2}~=~\frac{2x}{2}\]

    Simplify the right side.

    \[\frac{\log_{5}\left(\frac{y}{4}\right)}{2}~=~x\]

    Swap the sides to bring \(x\) to the left.

    \[x~=~\frac{\log_{5}\left(\frac{y}{4}\right)}{2}\]
