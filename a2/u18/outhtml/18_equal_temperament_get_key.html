18_equal_temperament_get_key


  1. Question

    In music, an equal temperament implies the available fundamental frequencies follow a geometric progression. Imagine a keyboard instrument for 9 EDO. That means there are 9 frequencies per octave, and the ratio between neighboring frequencies is \(2^{1/9} \approx 1.0800597\). That instrument acts as a function that maps key number (\(k\)) to frequency (\(f\)). (Notice, I am NOT referring to the key or the key signature; I am referring to the “buttons” of a keyboard as keys.)

    Let’s assume a concert pitch of 440 Hz is assigned to the key number 33, so we can calculate the frequency from any key number using the following exponential function.

    \[f = 440 \cdot 2^{(k-33)/9} \]

    The inverse of exponentiation is the logarithm. This allows us to create a formula to calculate the key number from the frequency.

    \[k = 9 \log_2\left(\frac{f}{440}\right)+33\]

    What key number produces a frequency of \(f=237.6131426\) Hz? (Please round to nearest integer.)


    Solution

    \[k = 9 \log_2\left(\frac{237.6131426}{440}\right)+33\]

    \[k=25\]

    If your calculator does not allow logs with base 2, you can use the change-of-base identity.

    \[k = 9 \frac{\log\left(\frac{237.6131426}{440}\right)}{\log(2)}+33= 25\]
