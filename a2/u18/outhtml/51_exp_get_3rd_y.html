  1. Question

    An exponential curve, \(y=ab^x\), goes through points \((0.40,1.42)\) and \((2.50,1.06)\). The same curve goes through \((3.40,v)\). What is the value of \(v\)?

    The tolerance is \(\pm 0.01\).


    Solution

    We know the following:

    \[1.42=ab^{0.4}\] \[1.06=ab^{2.5}\]

    From this system of two equations, we can determine \(a\) and \(b\). It is easiest to divide the two equations to eliminate \(a\).

    \[\frac{1.06}{1.42}=\frac{b^{2.5}}{b^{0.4}}\]

    The quotient of two exponentials with common bases is equivalent to that base raised to the difference of powers.

    \[\frac{1.06}{1.42}=b^{2.5-0.4}\] \[\frac{1.06}{1.42}=b^{2.1}\]

    Raise both sides to the power of \(1/2.1\).

    \[\left(\frac{1.06}{1.42}\right)^{1/2.1}=b\]

    Now, use either of the initial equations to get \(a\).

    \[1.42=a\left(\left(\frac{1.06}{1.42}\right)^{1/2.1}\right)^{0.4}\]

    \[1.42=a\left(\frac{1.06}{1.42}\right)^{0.4/2.1}\]

    \[\frac{1.42}{\left(\frac{1.06}{1.42}\right)^{0.4/2.1}}=a\]

    Approximate decimal representations will be fine.

    \[a\approx 1.5013277\] \[b\approx 0.8700258\]

    So, now use \(x=3.4\) to get \(v\).

    \[v = (1.5013277)(0.8700258)^{3.4}\]

    \[v \approx 0.9351576\]
