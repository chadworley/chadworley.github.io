Question

For exponential functions, a horizontal stretch is equivalent to a change of base. Consider the two functions below, where \(g\) is equivalent to stretching \(f\) horizontally by a factor \(2.8\):


\[\begin{align} f(x) &= e^{x}\\\\ g(x) &= e^{x/2.8} \end{align}\]

Instead of using the horizontal stretch transformation, we could have changed the base.

\[g(x) = B^{x}\]

where \(B\) represents the new base.

  • Find \(B\). The tolerance is \(\pm 0.01\).

Solution

It can be shown that \(B = 1.42924\). For all \(x\):

\[e^{x/2.8} = B^x \]

Division is multiplication by a reciprocal and \(a^{bc}\equiv \left(a^{b}\right)^c\).

\[\left(e^{1/2.8}\right)^x = B^x \]

Since the equation must hold for all values of \(x\),

\[e^{1/2.8} = B \]

Evaluate.

\[B = 1.42924\]
