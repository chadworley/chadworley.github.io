  1. Question

    A mass-spring system oscillates over time. The position (in meters) of the mass is plotted as a function of time (in seconds).


    Choose a trigonometric function (\(g\)), and the parameters (\(A\), \(B\), and \(D\)), to model this oscillatory motion.

    \[y~=~A\cdot g(Bt)+D\]

    • \(g=\)
    • \(A=\)
    • \(B=\)
    • \(D=\)


    Solution

    • The amplitude is 2 meters.
    • The period is 10 seconds.
    • The midline is at 1 meters.

    The only calculation needed is to convert the period (\(P=10\)) into angular frequency (\(B\)) by dividing \(2\pi\) by the period.

    \[B=\frac{2\pi}{P}\] \[B=0.6283185\]

    The amplitude is the absolute value of \(A\). \[|A|~=~2\]

    1. If when \(t=0\) (at \(y\)-axis) the wave is at midline and is increasing, then \(A>0\) and \(g=\sin\).
    2. If when \(t=0\) (at \(y\)-axis) the wave is at midline and is decreasing, then \(A<0\) and \(g=\sin\).
    3. If when \(t=0\) (at \(y\)-axis) the wave is at maximum, then \(A>0\) and \(g=\cos\).
    4. If when \(t=0\) (at \(y\)-axis) the wave is at minimum, then \(A<0\) and \(g=\cos\).

    So, \(A=2\) and \(g=\sin\). Also, \(D=1\).

    So,

    \[y ~=~ 2\cdot\sin(0.63t)+1\]

    • \(g=\sin\)
    • \(A=2\)
    • \(B=0.63\)
    • \(D=1\)
