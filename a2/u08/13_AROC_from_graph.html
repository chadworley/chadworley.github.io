Question

This question is about average rate of change. For function \(f(x)\), its average rate of change between \(x=a\) and \(x=b\) equals a quotient of differences: \[\text{ave rate of change} = \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} \]

A function \(f\) is graphed below.


  • Find the average rate of change between \(x=0\) and \(x=10\).

You can round to the nearest hundredth.

Solution


\[f(0)=7\]

\[f(10)=8\]

\[\text{ave rate of change} = \frac{f(10)-f(0)}{10-0} = \frac{8-7}{10-0} = \frac{1}{10} = 0.1 \]

Notice the orange segment has length of 1 and the violet segment has length of 10. The formula for average rate of change is rise over run.
