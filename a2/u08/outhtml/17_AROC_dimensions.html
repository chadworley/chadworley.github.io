  1. Question

    In the table below, various physical quantities and some common units of measure are presented:

    quantity units of measure
    time seconds (\(\mathrm{s}\))
    distance meters (\(\mathrm{m}\))
    velocity meters per second (\(\mathrm{m/s}\))
    acceleration meters per squared second (\(\mathrm{m/s^2}\))
    jerk meters per cubed second (\(\mathrm{m/s^3}\))
    force Newtons (\(\mathrm{N}\))
    impulse (momentum) Newton-seconds (\(\mathrm{N\cdot s}\))
    energy Newton-meters (\(\mathrm{N\cdot m}\))
    energy Joules (\(\mathrm{J}\))
    energy kilowatt-hours (\(\mathrm{kW\cdot hr}\))
    power Newton-meters per second (\(\mathrm{\frac{N\cdot m}{s}}\))
    power Joules per second (\(\mathrm{J/s}\)) = Watts (\(\mathrm{W}\))
    power kilowatt (\(\mathrm{kW}\))
    electrical charge Coulombs (\(\mathrm{C}\))
    electrical current Coulombs/second (\(\mathrm{C/s}\)) = amperes (\(\mathrm{A}\))

    A device measures energy (in \(\mathrm{N\cdot m}\)) as a function of distance (in \(\mathrm{m}\)). Those measurements are graphed below.


    The two marked points are at coordinates (42.2, 47.4) and (55.4, 63). Determine the average rate of change of the energy over the given interval of distance marked by the two points.

    AROC =

    This average rate of change is a quantity of .



    Solution

    \[\mathrm{AROC}~=~\frac{(63-47.4)(\mathrm{N\cdot m})}{(55.4-42.2)(\mathrm{m})}\]

    \[\mathrm{AROC}~=~\frac{15.6}{13.2}\,\frac{\mathrm{N\cdot m}}{\mathrm{m}}\]

    \[\mathrm{AROC}~=~1.18\,\mathrm{N}\]

    Thus when we find the average rate of change of energy over an interval of distance, the result is a quantity of force.
