  1. Question

    The continuous random variable \(X\) follows the distribution shown by the density curve and spinner below. It has a mean of \(\mu=9.96\) and standard deviation of \(\sigma=1.75\).



    That spinner (\(X\)) will be fairly spun 882 times, and the sample mean (\(\bar{X}\)) of the spins will be recorded. Determine the probability that the random sample mean is less than 9.99. \[P\left(\bar{X}<9.99\right) ~=~ \,? \]

    (Even though single spins are not normally distributed, the random means will be normally distributed. See central limit theorem.)


    Solution

    • (old) video

    We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. The mean of the sampling distribution (\(\mu_{\bar{x}}\)) is equal to the mean of the population (\(\mu\)).

    \[\mu_{\bar{x}} = \mu = 9.96 \]

    The standard error (\(\sigma_{\bar{x}}\)) is found by dividing the population standard deviation (\(\sigma\)) by the square root of the sample size.

    \[\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{1.75}{\sqrt{882}} = 0.0589256\]

    So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 9.96 and a standard deviation of 0.0589256. \[\bar{X} \sim N(9.96,\,0.0589256) \]

    Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{9.99-9.96}{0.0589256} = 0.5091169 \]

    You can round that to \(z=0.5\) or \(z=0.51\). You can then use a z table to find the probability. Any of the following probabilities will get credit.

    \[P(Z < 0.5091169) = 0.6946649 \] \[P(Z < 0.5) = 0.6915 \] \[P(Z < 0.51) = 0.695 \]

    To get the probability, you could also use a spreadsheet. Either will work:

    • =NORMDIST(0.5091169,0,1,TRUE)
    • =NORMDIST(9.99,9.96,0.0589256,TRUE)

    To get the probability, you could also use Desmos. Either will work:

    • Use \(z\) score:
      • Make a standard normal curve with normaldist(0,1).
      • Click the magnifying glass with the + to get the normal curve to show nicely.
      • Check the box labeled Find Cumulative Probability (CDF)
      • Keep \(-\infty\) as the minimum boundary. Use 0.5091169 as the maximum boundary.
    • Use the given boundary:
      • Make a sampling-distributions normal curve with normaldist(9.96,0.0589256).
      • Click the magnifying glass with the + to get the normal curve to show nicely.
      • Check the box labeled Find Cumulative Probability (CDF)
      • Keep \(-\infty\) as the minimum boundary. Use 9.99 as the maximum boundary.
