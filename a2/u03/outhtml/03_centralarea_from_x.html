  1. Question

    Random variable \(B\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 480\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 150\). \[B \sim \text{N}(480,\,150) \] Evaluate \(P\left(\big|B-480\big| < 60\right)\). In other words, what is the probability that \(B\) is within \(\pm60\) units from the mean?


    Solution

    0.3108

    Use online app

    Go to either:

    • chadworley.github.io/a2/tools/normal.html
      • Set \(\mu\) to 480
      • Set \(\sigma\) to 150
      • Choose center interval (radio button)
      • Set \(r\) to 60.
    • www.desmos.com/calculator
      • Type NORMALDIST(480,150)
      • Click Find Cumulative Probability (CDF).
      • Enter 480 as Min. Notice, \(540-60=480\).
      • Enter 600 as Max. Notice, \(540+60=600\).

    video (using app)

    Use z table

    video (using z-table)

    First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(B\) axis by adding integer multiples of 150 to 480. We know to shade the center because values near 480 satisfy the condition \(\big|B-480\big| < 60\). We draw the boundaries at \(480-60=420\) and \(480+60=540\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|B-480\big| = 60\). We can also rephrase the probability.

    \[P\left(\big|B-480\big| < 60\right) ~=~ P(420<B<540)\]


    We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{420-480}{150} \\\\ &= \frac{-60}{150} \\\\ &= -0.4 \end{aligned}\]

    Right boundary:

    \[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{540-480}{150} \\\\ &= \frac{60}{150} \\\\ &= 0.4 \end{aligned}\]

    We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

    \[P\left(\big|B-480\big| < 60\right) ~=~ P(420<B<540) ~=~ P(-0.4<Z<0.4) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|<0.4\right)\]

    So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|<0.4)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{60}{150} = 0.4\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|<d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| < \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

    From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|<0.4) &= 2 \times P(Z<0.4)-1 \\ &= 2 \times 0.6554-1 \\ &= 0.3108 \end{aligned}\]
