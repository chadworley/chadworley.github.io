  1. Question

    Random variable \(K\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 10\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 1\). \[K \sim \text{N}(10,\,1) \] Evaluate \(P\left(9 < K < 10\right)\). In other words, what is the probability that \(K\) is between \(9\) and \(10\)?


    Solution

    Online app

    • chadworley.github.io/a2/tools/normal.html
      • Set \(\mu\) to 10
      • Set \(\sigma\) to 1
      • Choose between interval (radio button)
      • Set \(b_1\) to 9.
      • Set \(b_2\) to 10.
    • www.desmos.com/calculator
      • Type NORMALDIST(10,1)
      • Click Find Cumulative Probability (CDF).
      • Enter 9 as Min.
      • Enter 10 as Max.

    video (using app): the app

    Z table

    video (using z-table)

    First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(K\) axis by adding integer multiples of 1 to 10. We know to shade the between \(9\) and \(10\) because those values satisfy the condition \(9 < K < 10\).


    We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{k_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{9-10}{1} \\\\ &= -1 \end{aligned}\]

    Right boundary:

    \[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{k_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{10-10}{1} \\\\ &= 0 \end{aligned}\]

    We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

    \[P\left(9 < K < 10\right) ~=~ P(-1<Z<0)\]

    So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-1<Z<0)\).

    From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-1<Z<0) &= P(Z<0)-P(Z<-1)\\ &= 0.5-0.1587 \\ &= 0.3413 \end{aligned}\]
