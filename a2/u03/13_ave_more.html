Question

The continuous random variable \(X\) follows the distribution shown by the density curve and spinner below. It has a mean of \(\mu_{X}=48.37\) and standard deviation of \(\sigma_{X}=3.42\).



That spinner (\(X\)) will be fairly spun 285 times, and the sample mean of the spins will be recorded. Determine the probability that the random sample mean is more than 48.54. \[P\left(\bar{X}>48.54\right) ~=~ \,? \]

(Even though single spins are not normally distributed, the random means will be normally distributed. See central limit theorem.)

Solution

video

We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 48.37 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{3.42}{\sqrt{285}} = 0.2025833\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 48.37 and a standard deviation of 0.2025833. \[\bar{X} \sim N(48.37,\,0.2025833) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{48.54-48.37}{0.2025833} = 0.8391609 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.83\) or \(z=0.84\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > 0.8391609) = 0.2006895 \] \[P(Z > 0.83) = 0.2033 \] \[P(Z > 0.84) = 0.2005 \]

To get the probability, you can use a spreadsheet. Either will work:

  • =1-NORMDIST(0.8391609,0,1,TRUE)
  • =1-NORMDIST(48.54,48.37,0.2025833,TRUE)
