  21. Question

    Let \(z_1\) and \(z_2\) be two complex numbers. \[z_1 = -1-9i\] \[z_2 = -4+i\] Find the product. \[z_1 \cdot z_2 = ~?\]

    Type your answer precisely, with no spaces, using digits, “-”, “+”, and “i”. If the real term is nonzero, put it first. If either component is zero, do not include that term. If both components are zero, type “0” without the quotes. Instead of writing “1i” or “-1i” just write “i” or “-i”.


    Solution

    To multiply complex numbers, follow a procedure similar to FOIL, but remember \(i^2=-1\).

    \[(-1-9i)(-4+i)\]

    \[\Big((-1)+(-9i)\Big)\Big((-4)+(1i)\Big)\]

    \[(-1)(-4)~~+~~(-1)(1i)~~+~~(-9i)(-4)~~+~~(-9i)(1i)\] \[4~~+~~(-1)i~~+~~(36)i ~~+~~(-9)i^2\]

    Remember that \(i^2=-1\), so the first and last terms are both real. Let’s group similar terms.

    \[4~~+~~(9)~~+~~(-1)i~~+~~(36)i\] Combine the real terms and combine the imaginary terms.

    \[13+35i\]
