  1. Question

    Bob has a 0.31 chance of winning a game. If Bob wins, he has a 0.75 chance of being happy after the game. If Bob loses, he has a 0.42 chance of being happy after the game.

    After the game, you notice Bob is happy. What is the chance that Bob won his game?


    Solution

    (ANS=0.4451465)

    video

    Use the definition of conditional probability. \[P(\text{win given happy}) = \frac{P(\text{win and happy})}{P(\text{happy})}\] We can first determine all the joint probabilities. \[P(A\text{ and }B) = P(A)\cdot P(B\text{ given } A) \] \[\begin{aligned} P(\text{win and happy}) &= 0.31\times0.75 = 0.2325\\ P(\text{win and sad}) &= 0.31\times(1-0.75) = 0.0775\\ P(\text{lose and happy}) &= (1-0.31)\times0.42 = 0.2898\\ P(\text{lose and sad}) &= (1-0.31)\times(1-0.42) = 0.4002\\ \end{aligned}\]

    Notice there are two mutually exclusive ways Bob could be happy. \[\begin{aligned} P(\text{happy}) &= P\left(\text{[win and happy] OR [lose and happy]}\right) \\ &= P(\text{win and happy}) + P(\text{lose and happy}) \\ &= 0.2325+0.2898 \\ &= 0.5223 \end{aligned}\] So, back to the conditional probability. \[P(\text{win given happy}) = \frac{0.2325}{0.5223} = 0.445\]
