  1. Question

    In symbolic logic, we often use the following symbols for NOT, AND, and OR:

    \[\neg ~=~ \text{not}\] \[\cap ~=~ \text{and} ~=~ \text{intersection}\] \[\cup ~=~ \text{or} ~=~ \text{union}\]

    In this case (and almost always), unary operators (like \(\neg\)) have higher precedence than binary operators (like \(\cup\) and \(\cap\)).

    You are given the following probabilities (regarding events \(A\) and \(B\)).

    • \(P(B)=0.39\)
    • \(P(A\cap \neg B)=0.3\)
    • \(P(A)=0.45\)

    Calculate the following:

    • \(P(A\cap B)=\) students
    • \(P(\neg A\cap B)=\) students
    • \(P(A\cup B)=\) students
    • \(P(\neg A\cap \neg B)=\) students
    • \(P(\neg A)\) students
    • \(P(\neg B)\) students

    Optional metaphorical context

    Imagine getting blindfolded and throwing darts at a board far away on a gusty day. On that board are two overlapping circles: circle A and circle B. If you miss the board completely, the throw does not count, and you try again. If you hit circle A, that counts as event \(A\). If you miss circle A (but hit the board), that counts as \(\neg A\). The probability that you hit circle A will depend on the proportion of the boardâ€™s area taken up by circle A.

    \[P(A) ~=~ \frac{\text{area of circle A}}{\text{area of board}}\] \[P(\neg A) ~=~ \frac{\text{area of board} ~~-~~ \text{area of circle A}}{\text{area of board}}\]



    Solution

    • \(P(A\cap B)=0.15\)
    • \(P(\neg A\cap B)=0.24\)
    • \(P(A\cup B)=0.69\)
    • \(P(\neg A \cap \neg B)=0.31\)
    • \(P(\neg A)=0.55\)
    • \(P(\neg B)=0.61\)

    You might find it helpful to draw a Venn diagram.

    • NOTE: These areas are not drawn to scale.
