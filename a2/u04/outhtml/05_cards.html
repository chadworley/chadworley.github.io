  1. Question

    In a deck of strange cards, there are 475 cards. Each card has an image and a color. The number of repeats of each image-color combination is shown in the table below.

    yellow red orange indigo black Total
    dog 41 14 39 22 25 141
    gem 26 17 43 49 45 180
    rug 32 13 46 47 16 154
    Total 99 44 128 118 86 475
    1. What is the probability a random card is a dog?
    2. What is the probability a random card is orange?
    3. What is the probability a random card is both a gem and yellow?
    4. What is the probability a random card is either a rug or black (or both)?
    5. What is the probability a random card is indigo given it is a dog?
    6. What is the probability a random card is a gem given it is red?
    7. Between a dog and a gem, which is more likely to be a indigo?


    Solution

    video

    The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.

    1. \(P(\text{dog})=\frac{141}{475}=0.3\)
    2. \(P(\text{orange})=\frac{128}{475}=0.27\)
    3. \(P(\text{gem and yellow})=\frac{26}{475}=0.05\)
    4. \(P(\text{rug or black})=\frac{154+86-16}{475}=0.47\)
    5. \(P(\text{indigo given dog})=\frac{22}{141}=0.16\)
    6. \(P(\text{gem given red})=\frac{17}{44}=0.39\)
    7. \(P(\text{indigo given dog}) = 0.16\) and \(P(\text{indigo given gem}) = 0.27\), so a gem is more likely to be indigo than a dog is.
