04_ind_or_mut_exclu


  1. Question

    • Events \(A\) and \(B\) are mutually exclusive if and only if \(P(A \cap B)=0\).
    • Events \(A\) and \(B\) are independent if and only if \(P(A \cap B)=P(A)\cdot P(B)\).

    Consider the following:

    \[P(A)=0.25\]

    \[P(B)=0.16\]

    \[P(A \cup B)=0.41\]

    Are events \(A\) and \(B\) independent, mutually exclusive, or neither?


    1. Independent
    2. Mutually exclusive
    3. Neither

    Solution

    First, find the probability of getting A and B.

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ P(A)+P(B)-P(A \cup B)\]

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ 0.25+0.16-0.41\]

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ 0\]

    Notice \(P(A \cap B)=0\), so events A and B are mutually exclusive.



  2. Question

    • Events \(A\) and \(B\) are mutually exclusive if and only if \(P(A \cap B)=0\).
    • Events \(A\) and \(B\) are independent if and only if \(P(A \cap B)=P(A)\cdot P(B)\).

    Consider the following:

    \[P(A)=0.42\]

    \[P(B)=0.18\]

    \[P(A \cup B)=0.5244\]

    Are events \(A\) and \(B\) independent, mutually exclusive, or neither?


    1. Independent
    2. Mutually exclusive
    3. Neither

    Solution

    First, find the probability of getting A and B.

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ P(A)+P(B)-P(A \cup B)\]

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ 0.42+0.18-0.5244\]

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ 0.0756\]

    Notice \(P(A \cap B)=0.0756\). Also \(P(A)\cdot P(B)=0.42\cdot0.18=0.0756\) so events A and B are independent.

  5. Question

    • Events \(A\) and \(B\) are mutually exclusive if and only if \(P(A \cap B)=0\).
    • Events \(A\) and \(B\) are independent if and only if \(P(A \cap B)=P(A)\cdot P(B)\).

    Consider the following:

    \[P(A)=0.26\]

    \[P(B)=0.42\]

    \[P(A \cup B)=0.6605\]

    Are events \(A\) and \(B\) independent, mutually exclusive, or neither?


    1. Independent
    2. Mutually exclusive
    3. Neither

    Solution

    First, find the probability of getting A and B.

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ P(A)+P(B)-P(A \cup B)\]

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ 0.26+0.42-0.6605\]

    \[P(A \cap B) ~=~ 0.0195\]

    Notice \(P(A \cap B)=0.0195\). However, \(P(A)\cdot P(B)=0.26\cdot0.42=0.1092\). Clearly, \(0.0195\ne0.1092\), so events A and B are NOT independent. Also, because \(P(A \cap B)\ne 0\), events A and B are NOT mutually exclusive.
