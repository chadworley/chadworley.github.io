    9. \(x=-2\)
    10. \(x=-1\)
    11. \(x=0\)
    12. \(x=1\)
    13. \(x=2\)
    14. \(x=3\)
    15. \(x=4\)
    16. \(x=5\)
    17. \(x=6\)
    18. \(x=7\)
    19. \(x=8\)
    20. \(x=9\)
    21. \(x=10\)

    Solution

    Our original equation is a quadratic equal to 0.

    \[x^{2}-{13}x+{36}=0\]

    The quadratic has a leading coefficient of 1 and a constant of 36. Consider all the pairs of linear factors with integer coefficients whose FOIL product gives a quadratic with a leading coefficient of 1 and a constant of 36.

    first factor second factor FOIL product
    \((x+{1})\) \((x+{36})\) \(x^{2}+{37}x+{36}\)
    \((x-{1})\) \((x-{36})\) \(x^{2}-{37}x+{36}\)
    \((x+{2})\) \((x+{18})\) \(x^{2}+{20}x+{36}\)
    \((x-{2})\) \((x-{18})\) \(x^{2}-{20}x+{36}\)
    \((x+{3})\) \((x+{12})\) \(x^{2}+{15}x+{36}\)
    \((x-{3})\) \((x-{12})\) \(x^{2}-{15}x+{36}\)
    \((x+{4})\) \((x+{9})\) \(x^{2}+{13}x+{36}\)
    \((x-{4})\) \((x-{9})\) \(x^{2}-{13}x+{36}\)
    \((x+{6})\) \((x+{6})\) \(x^{2}+{12}x+{36}\)
    \((x-{6})\) \((x-{6})\) \(x^{2}-{12}x+{36}\)

    By guess and check, we find the two integers with sum -13 and product 36 are \(-9\) and \(-4\). Indeed, \((-9)+(-4) = -13\) and \((-9)(-4) = 36\). This allows us to write the original equation in factored form.

    \[(x-9)(x-4)=0\]

    The zero-product rule lets us find the solutions.

    \[x = 9\] \[x = 4\]
