  1. Question

    An event has two types of tickets: each adult ticket costs $8.78 and each child ticket costs $3.28. The event sold 65 tickets for a revenue of $361.70.

    • How many child tickets were sold?

    Solution

    Let’s use \(x\) to represent the number of adult tickets and \(y\) to represent the number of child tickets. We know the total number of tickets sold is 65. \[x+y~=~65\] The revenue from adults equals the product of $8.78 and \(x\). The revenue from children equals the product of $3.28 and \(y\). The total revenue is the sum of the two types of revenue, and it equals $361.70.

    \[8.78x+3.28y~=~361.70\]

    We have a system of two equations and two unknowns. We want to solve for \(y\), which is the number of child tickets. To do this, I’d start by isolating \(x\) in the first equation (the equation from the number of tickets).

    \[x~=~65-y\]

    We can now substitute \(65-y\) for \(x\) in the second equation (the equation from the total revenue).

    \[8.78\cdot(65-y)+3.28y=361.70\]

    Distribute.

    \[570.7-8.78y+3.28y=361.70\]

    Subtract 570.7 from both sides.

    \[-8.78y+3.28y=-209\]

    Combine the linear terms.

    \[-5.5y=-209\]

    Divide both sides by -5.5.

    \[y=38\]
