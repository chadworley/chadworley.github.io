19_circ_2nd_coordinate


  1. Question

    A circle is centered at the origin with a radius equal to 1. This circle is called the unit circle, where “unit” refers to 1.

    A horizontal line passes through point \((0.48,0.77)\).

    That horizontal line intersects the circle at two points: one in Quadrant I and another in Quadrant II.

    Determine the \(x\)-coordinate of the intersection between the line and the circle in Quadrant I.

    Please be accurate within \(\pm 0.001\).


    Solution

    The general form of a circle:

    \[(x-h)^2+(y-k)^2~=~r^2\]

    We were told the center is \((0,0)\) and radius is 1. So, the equation of the unit circle:

    \[(x-0)^2+(y-0)^2=1^2\]

    \[x^2+y^2=1\]

    The general form of a horizontal line:

    \[y=c\]

    In our case, because the line hits point \((0.48,0.77)\) we know the horizontal line should include every point where \(y\) equals 0.77. \[y=0.77\]

    So, to find the other coordinate of the intersection, replace y with 0.77 in the unit-circle equation. Solve for x.

    \[x^2+0.77^2=1\] \[x^2=1-0.77^2\] \[x=\sqrt{1-0.77^2}\] \[x~\approx~0.6380439\]

    Let’s graph the circle, both points, and line.
