  1. Question

    A taxi service charges a fixed base cost of $4.04 and then a mileage cost of $1.16 per mile. The following people wonder whether they can travel a specified distance with the amount of cash they are carrying.

    • Can Arnold go 20.2 miles with $33.09?
    • Can Betty go 5.5 miles with $13.61?
    • Can Chester go 9.5 miles with $12.11?
    • Can Denali go 12.8 miles with $14.63?
    • Can Edward go 2.4 miles with $5.12?


    Solution

    Iâ€™d recommend doing this with Desmos. Start by creating the equation describing the linear equation between distance (\(x\), miles) and total cost (\(y\), dollars).

    \[y~=~4.04+1.16x\]

    A passenger needs to have at least the total in cash to afford the ride. If the passenger has more than enough money, they can still afford the ride. So, if we redefine \(y\) to be any possible amount of pocket money, we can say \(y\) must be as much or more than the cost of the ride.

    \[y~\ge~4.04+1.16x\]

    So, type that inequality into Desmos. Then type in the following points:

    (20.2,33.09)

    (5.5,13.61)

    (9.5,12.11)

    (12.8,14.63)

    (2.4,5.12)

    For each point, if it falls in the feasible region, then it is a solution to the inequality.


    • Yes, Arnold can afford to go 20.2 miles with $33.09 in pocket.
    • Yes, Betty can afford to go 5.5 miles with $13.61 in pocket.
    • No, Chester can NOT afford to go 9.5 miles with $12.11 in pocket.
    • No, Denali can NOT afford to go 12.8 miles with $14.63 in pocket.
    • No, Edward can NOT afford to go 2.4 miles with $5.12 in pocket.
