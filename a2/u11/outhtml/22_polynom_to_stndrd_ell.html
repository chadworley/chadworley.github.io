  1. Question

    The following equation (in polynomial form) represents an ellipse.

    \[4x^2-8x+64y^2-640y+1348=0\]

    With some algebra (completing the square), you can convert the equation into standard form:

    \[\frac{(x-h)^2}{(r_1)^2}+\frac{(y-k)^2}{(r_2)^2}=1\]

    From the given polynomial form, determine the 4 parameters.

    • \(h=\)
    • \(k=\)
    • \(r_1=\)
    • \(r_2=\)


    Solution

    \[4x^2-8x+64y^2-640y+1348=0\] Factor out the quadratic coefficients from each variable’s polynomial. Also move the constant term to the right with addition/subtration.

    \[4(x^2-2x)+64(y^2-10y)=-1348\]

    Complete the square for the \(x\) quadratic. If \(x^2+bx+c\) is a perfect square, then \(c=\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2\). In this case, the linear coefficient is \(-2\). If you divide it by 2 and square the result, you get the necessary constant to complete the square (1). Notice \((x-1)^2\equiv x^2-2x+1\). Lastly, since we are adding 1 inside parentheses with a multiplier of 4, we are technically adding 4 to both sides to make an equivalent equation.

    \[4(x^2-2x+1)+64(y^2-10y)=-1348+4\]

    Complete the square for the \(y\) quadratic. If \(y^2+by+c\) is a perfect square, then \(c=\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2\). In this case, the linear coefficient is \(-10\). If you divide it by 2 and square the result, you get the necessary constant to complete the square (25). Notice \((y-5)^2\equiv y^2-10x+25\). Lastly, since we are adding 25 inside parentheses with a multiplier of 64, we are technically adding 1600 to both sides to make an equivalent equation.

    \[4(x^2-2x+1)+64(y^2-10y+25)=-1348+4+1600\]

    Combine the constants.

    \[4(x^2-2x+1)+64(y^2-10y+25)=256\]

    O yeah, remember how you completed the square? Twice? Let’s rewrite those quadratic expressions in factored form. (This could have been done earlier.)

    \[4(x-1)^2+64(y-5)^2=256\]

    Divide both sides by \(256\).

    \[\frac{4(x-1)^2}{256}+\frac{64(y-5)^2}{256}=1\]

    Simplify the fractions. Notice that \(\frac{4}{256}= \frac{1}{64}\) and \(\frac{64}{256}= \frac{1}{4}\).

    \[\frac{(x-1)^2}{64}+\frac{(y-5)^2}{4}=1\]

    If you find the square roots of both denominators, you will have the standard form of an ellipse. Notice \(\sqrt{64}=8\) and \(\sqrt{4}=2\).

    \[\frac{(x-1)^2}{8^2}+\frac{(y-5)^2}{2^2}=1\]

    Thus,

    \[\begin{align} h &= 1 \\ k &= 5 \\ r_1 &= 8 \\ r_2 &= 2 \end{align}\]
