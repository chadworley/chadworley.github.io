Al-Jabr (Algrebra) for IRT

  • Solve \(x^2+x^2=1^2\).
  • Combine like terms.
  • Also, \(1^2=1\). \[2x^2=1\]
  • Divide both sides by \(2\).
  • \[x^2=\frac{1}{2}\]
  • Take the (positive) square root of both sides.
  • \[x=\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}\]

Al-Jabr (Algrebra) for IRT cont.

  • \[x=\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}\]
  • For reasons, when we simplify, we do not leave fractions inside a radical. Distribute the radical to both factors of the quotient. This is allowed because \(\left(\frac{g}{h}\right)^p\equiv\frac{g^p}{h^p}\) and \(\sqrt{z}\equiv z^{1/2}\).
  • \[x=\frac{\sqrt{1}}{\sqrt{2}}\]
  • We know \(\sqrt{1}=1\).
  • \[x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Al-Jabr (Algrebra) for IRT cont..

  • \[x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\]
  • For reasons, when we simplify, we do not leave radicals in denominators. We need to ârationalize the denominatorâ. To do this, multiply both the numerator and denominator by the radical in the denominator.
  • \[x=\frac{1\cdot \sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}\cdot \sqrt{2}}\]
  • Simplify.
  • \[x=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\]

Algebra for HET

\[\begin{align*} x^2+\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2&=1^2\\ x^2+\frac{1}{4}&=1 \\ x^2&=1-\frac{1}{4} \\ x^2&=\frac{3}{4} \\ x&=\sqrt{\frac{3}{4}} \\ x&=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{4}} \\ x&=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{align*}\]

How get \(\tan(\pi/6)\)?

\[\begin{align*} \tan(\pi/6) ~&=~ \frac{\sin(\pi/6)}{\cos(\pi/6)} \\\\ &=~ \frac{1/2}{\sqrt{3}/2} \\\\ &=~ \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \\\\ &=~ \frac{1\cdot \sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}\cdot\sqrt{3}} \\\\ &=~ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \end{align*}\]

How get \(\tan(\pi/3)\)?

\[\begin{align*} \tan(\pi/3) ~&=~ \frac{\sin(\pi/3)}{\cos(\pi/3)} \\\\ &=~ \frac{\sqrt{3}/2}{1/2} \\\\ &=~ \sqrt{3} \end{align*}\]
