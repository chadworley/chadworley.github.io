  1. Question

    In an \(xy\)-plane, point \(A\) has coordinates \((8.5,-4.6)\). That point is rotated 153.3° counterclockwise around the origin, producing point \(B\). Find the (Cartesian) coordinates of \(B\).

    • (,)


    Solution

    Since we are turning counterclockwise, the turning angle is positive.

    \[\theta_T = 153.3°\]

    Find polar coordinates of point \(A\). Use distance formula (Pythagorean theorem) to get the radius, and use the two-argument arctangent to get the angle.

    \[r_A~=~\sqrt{(8.5)^2+(-4.6)^2}~=~9.6648849\] \[\theta_A~=~\arctan(-4.6,8.5)~=~-28.4212092°\]

    When we rotate around the origin, the radius stays the same.

    \[r_B~=~r_A\] \[r_B~=~9.6648849\]

    We add the turning angle to the original angle to find the terminal angle.

    \[\theta_B~=~\theta_A+\theta_T\] \[\theta_B~=~(-28.4212092°)+(153.3°)\] \[\theta_B~=~124.8787908°\]

    Convert the polar coordinates of point \(B\) back into Cartesian coordinates.

    \[x_B ~=~ r_B \cdot \cos(\theta_B) ~\approx~ -5.5\] \[y_B ~=~ r_B \cdot \sin(\theta_B) ~\approx~ 7.9\]


    I should also mention that there are other ways to determine the result of a rotation.
