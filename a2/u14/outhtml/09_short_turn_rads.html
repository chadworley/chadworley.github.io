  1. Question

    In the video game Asteroids, a player rotates a space ship to aim projectiles at asteroids.

    The ship is located at \((0,0)\) and currently points towards \((-7, 2)\). The ship hopes to rotate to aim at \((9, -8)\) with as little turning as possible. Using radians, and positive (or negative) for counterclockwise (or clockwise), what turning angle should the ship use?

    (The tolerance is \(\pm 0.001\) radians.)


    Solution

    I would first use two-argument arctangent to find a standard angle (rotating from positive \(x\) axis) for each given point. In Desmos, you can just use arctan(2,-7) and arctan(-8,9). Remember, RADIAN MODE.

    \[\theta_1 ~=~ \arctan(2,-7)~=~2.8633\]

    \[\theta_2 ~=~ \arctan(-8,9)~=~-0.7266\]

    Now… unfortunately… we cannot simply assume \(\theta_2-\theta_1\) will give the desired turning angle. It would definitely accomplish the task of turning from point 1 to point 2, but \(\theta_2-\theta_1\) might not be the smallest turn. To make sure we get the smallest turning angle, we can consider 3 coterminal turning angles.

    \[\alpha ~=~ \theta_2-\theta_1\] \[\beta ~=~ \alpha+2\pi\] \[\gamma ~=~ \alpha-2\pi\]

    Using the formulae above, calculate the three possible turning angles that would turn from the first point to the second point.

    \[\alpha ~=~ -3.59\] \[\beta ~=~ 2.693\] \[\gamma ~=~ -9.873\]

    Find the option with the least ABSOLUTE value. Also, remember, in math, positive angles correspond to counterclockwise rotations, and negative angles correspond to clockwise rotations. (This is reversed in navigation with bearings.)


    The ship should turn 2.693 radians counterclockwise, so the turning angle should be 2.693 radians.
