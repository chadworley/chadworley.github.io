  1. Question

    In an \(xy\)-plane, point \(A\) has coordinates \((-4.4,2.7)\). That point is rotated 2.258 radians clockwise around the origin, producing point \(B\). Find the (Cartesian) coordinates of \(B\).

    • (,)


    Solution

    Since we are turning clockwise, the turning angle is negative.

    \[\theta_T = -2.258\]

    Find polar coordinates of point \(A\). Use distance formula (Pythagorean theorem) to get the radius, and use the two-argument arctangent to get the angle.

    \[r_A~=~\sqrt{(-4.4)^2+(2.7)^2}~=~5.1623638\] \[\theta_A~=~\arctan(2.7,-4.4)~=~2.5912067\]

    When we rotate around the origin, the radius stays the same.

    \[r_B~=~r_A\] \[r_B~=~5.1623638\]

    We add the turning angle to the original angle to find the terminal angle.

    \[\theta_B~=~\theta_A+\theta_T\] \[\theta_B~=~(2.5912067)+(-2.258)\] \[\theta_B~=~0.3332067\]

    Convert the polar coordinates of point \(B\) back into Cartesian coordinates.

    \[x_B ~=~ r_B \cdot \cos(\theta_B) ~\approx~ 4.9\] \[y_B ~=~ r_B \cdot \sin(\theta_B) ~\approx~ 1.7\]


    I should also mention that there are other ways to determine the result of a rotation.
