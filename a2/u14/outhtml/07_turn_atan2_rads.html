07_turn_atan2_rads


  1. Question

    In the video game Asteroids, a player rotates a space ship to aim projectiles at asteroids.

    If the ship is located at \((0,0)\) and currently points towards \((1,0)\), how many radians, and in which direction, should the ship rotate to aim at \((-4, -8)\) with as little turning as possible?



    Solution

    This is a perfect setup for using two-argument arctangent.

    In Desmos, this can be done by typing arctan(-8,-4). Notice, the two-argument arctangent takes the \(y\) coordinate first and the \(x\) coordinate second. You can blame the writers of Fortran in 1961.

    Make sure you are in RADIAN MODE.

    A positive angle indicates counterclockwise turning. A negative angle indicates clockwise turning.
