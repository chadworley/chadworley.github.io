Question

A circle with radius \(r=9\) centimeters is cut into 8 congruent sectors. A length of red string is pulled around the circle, and the string is exactly long enough to span the arc of 5 adjacent sectors, as shown in the diagram below.


How long, in centimeters, is the red string? Please answer as a decimal approximation accurate within \(\pm 0.01\) centimeters.

Solution

Find the diameter.

\[d=2r\] \[d=2\cdot9\] \[d=18\]

Find the circumference.

\[C = \pi d\] \[C = \pi \cdot 18\] \[C~\approx~56.5486678\]

The string’s length is \(\frac{5}{8}\) of the circumference.

\[L~=~\frac{5}{8}C\] \[L~=~\frac{5}{8}d\pi\] \[L~=~\frac{5}{8}2\pi r\] \[L~\approx~\frac{5}{8}(56.5486678)\] \[L~\approx~35.3429174\]
