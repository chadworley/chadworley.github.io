Radians

  • In mathematics, the standard way to measure an angle is radians.
    • Not degrees!

\[360^{\circ}~=~2\pi~\text{radians}\] \[180^{\circ}~=~\pi~\text{radians}\] \[1~\text{radian} ~\approx~57.3^{\circ}\]

  • In radians, common angles are often expressed as a product (multiplication) of \(\pi\) and a rational number, or a fraction of \(\pi\).

\[\begin{align} 30^{\circ}~&=~\frac{\pi}{6}~\text{radians} \\\\ 45^{\circ}~&=~\frac{\pi}{4}~\text{radians} \\\\ 60^{\circ}~&=~\frac{\pi}{3}~\text{radians} \\\\ 90^{\circ}~&=~\frac{\pi}{2}~\text{radians} \\\\ 120^{\circ}~&=~\frac{2\pi}{3}~\text{radians} \\\\ 135^{\circ}~&=~\frac{3\pi}{4}~\text{radians} \\\\ 150^{\circ}~&=~\frac{5\pi}{6}~\text{radians} \end{align}\]

  • Technically, we do not even need to say radians as the unit. If an angle is expressed without an explicit unit, we assume the angle is in radians. This is because radians are a dimensionless ratio of an arc length over a radius.

Convert from degrees to radians

  • To convert from degrees to radians, use the fact that \(180^{\circ}=\pi~\text{radians}\) to make a conversion factor.
  • In other words, multiply by \(\frac{\pi~\text{radians}}{180^{\circ}}\).
  • Example, convert 42 to radians:
  • \[42^{\circ}~=~\frac{42^{\circ}}{1}\cdot\frac{\pi~\text{rad}}{180^{\circ}}\]
  • \[42^{\circ}~=~\frac{7\pi}{30} ~\text{rad}\]
  • \[42^{\circ}~\approx~ 0.7330383~\text{rad}\]

Convert from radians to degrees

  • To convert from radians to degrees, multiply by \(\frac{180^{\circ}}{\pi~\text{radians}}\).
  • Example, convert \(\frac{\pi}{9}\) radians to degrees:
  • \[\frac{\pi}{9}\cdot \frac{180^{\circ}}{\pi} ~=~ 20^{\circ}\]

Visual/geometric interpretation

  • Draw a circle of radius \(r\).
  • Draw a central angle, \(\theta\), in standard position.
  • That central angle subtends an arc length \(L\).


  • In radians, there is a simple relationship between these three quantities.

\[\theta = \frac{L}{r}\]

  • Notice, in degrees, this is more complicated \[\theta = \frac{L\cdot 180^{\circ}}{r\cdot\pi}\]
