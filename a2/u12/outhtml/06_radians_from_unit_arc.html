06_radians_from_unit_arc


  1. Question

    The radian measure of an angle is the length of the arc on the unit circle subtended by the angle. In mathematics, radians are the default way to measure an angle.

    Consider a circle with a radius of length \(r=1\) meter. A central angle, in standard position, subtends an arc of length \(1.62\) meters.


    The central angle, measured in degrees, is approximately equal to 93°. However, from the radius and the arc length, you should be able to calculate the angle in radians exactly.

    Find \(\theta\) in radians.


    Solution

    The radian measure of an angle is the length of the arc on the unit circle subtended by the angle.

    \[\theta=1.62\]
