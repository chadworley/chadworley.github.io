  1. Question

    A circle with radius \(r=9\) centimeters is cut into 3 congruent sectors. A length of red string is pulled around the circle, and the string is exactly long enough to span the arc of 2 adjacent sectors, as shown in the diagram below.


    How long, in centimeters, is the red string? Please answer as a decimal approximation accurate within \(\pm 0.01\) centimeters.


    Solution

    Find the diameter.

    \[d=2r\] \[d=2\cdot9\] \[d=18\]

    Find the circumference.

    \[C = \pi d\] \[C = \pi \cdot 18\] \[C~\approx~56.5486678\]

    The stringâ€™s length is \(\frac{2}{3}\) of the circumference.

    \[L~=~\frac{2}{3}C\] \[L~=~\frac{2}{3}d\pi\] \[L~=~\frac{2}{3}2\pi r\] \[L~\approx~\frac{2}{3}(56.5486678)\] \[L~\approx~37.6991118\]
