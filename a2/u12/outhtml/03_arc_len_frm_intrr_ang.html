  1. Question

    A length of red string is pulled around the circle with radius \(r=3\). The red string subtends a central angle with a degree measure of \(\psi\)°, and the red string also subtends an inscribed angle with degree measure of 87°.


    How long, in centimeters, is the red string? Please answer as a decimal approximation accurate within \(\pm 0.01\) centimeters.


    Solution

    The inscribed angle theorem states that an angle \(\theta\) inscribed in a circle is half of the central angle \(2\theta\) that intercepts the same arc on the circle. Surprisingly, this is independent of where the inscribed angle sits on the circle. I find this animation helpful. Please take a moment to internalize this theorem, as it shows up frequently on standardized tests.

    Anyway, for this problem, from the inscribed-angle theorem, we know \(\psi=2\cdot 87=174\). In other words, our central angle is twice the corresponding inscribed angle.

    Find the diameter.

    \[d=2r\] \[d=2\cdot3\] \[d=6\]

    Find the circumference.

    \[C = \pi d\] \[C = \pi \cdot 6\] \[C~\approx~18.8495559\]

    Remember, there are \(360^{\circ}\) in a full circle. The string’s length is \(\frac{174}{360}\) of the circumference.

    \[L~=~\frac{174}{360}C\] \[L~=~\frac{174}{360}d\pi\] \[L~=~\frac{174}{360}2\pi r\] \[L~\approx~\frac{174}{360}(18.8495559)\] \[L~\approx~9.1106187\]
