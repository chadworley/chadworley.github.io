02_arc_length_from_degs


  1. Question

    A length of red string is pulled around the circle with radius \(r=3\). The red string subtends a central angle with a degree measure of 212°, as shown in the diagram below.


    How long, in centimeters, is the red string? Please answer as a decimal approximation accurate within \(\pm 0.01\) centimeters.


    Solution

    Find the diameter.

    \[d=2r\] \[d=2\cdot3\] \[d=6\]

    Find the circumference.

    \[C = \pi d\] \[C = \pi \cdot 6\] \[C~\approx~18.8495559\]

    Remember, there are \(360^{\circ}\) in a full circle. The string’s length is \(\frac{212}{360}\) of the circumference.

    \[L~=~\frac{212}{360}C\] \[L~=~\frac{212}{360}d\pi\] \[L~=~\frac{212}{360}2\pi r\] \[L~\approx~\frac{212}{360}(18.8495559)\] \[L~\approx~11.100294\]
