15_sector_area


  1. Question

    An arc is drawn along the circumference of a circle with a radius of \(r=4\) meters. The arc’s length is \(L=10.68\) meters. What is the corresponding sector’s area (labelled \(A\)) in square meters?


    Please give an exact answer (in \(\mathrm{m^2}\)).


    Solution

    Divide the arc length by the radius to get the angle measure (in radians).

    \[\theta = \frac{10.68}{4}\]

    \[\theta = 2.67\]

    Find the area of the entire circle. Then multiply that area by the fraction of the circle.

    \[A = \pi r^2 \cdot \frac{\theta}{2\pi}\]

    \[A = \pi (4)^2 \cdot \frac{2.67}{2\pi}\]

    \[A = 21.36\]

    If you are feeling clever, you might derive a simpler formula.

    \[\theta = \frac{L}{r}\] \[A ~=~ \pi r^2 \cdot \frac{L}{2\pi r} ~=~ \frac{rL}{2}\]

    So, the area of the sector is 21.36 \(\mathrm{m^2}\).
