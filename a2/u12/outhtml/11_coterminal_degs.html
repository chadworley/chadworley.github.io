  1. Question

    Find an angle measure (in degrees) between 0\(^\circ\) and 360\(^\circ\) that is coterminal to an angle of 661\(^\circ\).


    Solution

    The given angle, 661\(^\circ\), is more than 360\(^\circ\), so subtract 360\(^\circ\) until you get an angle between 0\(^\circ\) and 360\(^\circ\).

    \[661-360=301\]

    Thus, the answer is 301\(^\circ\). We can graph both angles: 661\(^\circ\) and 301\(^\circ\).


    Notice both terminal sides are in the same spot, even though the rotations to get there are different.



  2. Question

    Find an angle measure (in degrees) between 0\(^\circ\) and 360\(^\circ\) that is coterminal to an angle of -987\(^\circ\).


    Solution

    The given angle, -987\(^\circ\), is less than 0\(^\circ\), so add 360\(^\circ\) until you get an angle between 0\(^\circ\) and 360\(^\circ\).

    \[-987+360=-627\] \[-627+360=-267\] \[-267+360=93\]

    Thus, the answer is 93\(^\circ\). We can graph both angles: -987\(^\circ\) and 93\(^\circ\).


    Notice both terminal sides are in the same spot, even though the rotations to get there are different.
