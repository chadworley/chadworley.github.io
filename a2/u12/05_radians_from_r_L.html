Question

Two concentric circles have radii \(r_1=1\) meters and \(r_2=3.77\) meters. A central angle subtends similar arcs on both circles: a blue arc on the smaller circle and a red arc on the larger circle. The red arc length is 4.52 meters, as shown in the diagram below.


What is the length, in meters, of the blue arc? The tolerance is set to \(\pm0.01\) meters.

Solution

Okay, this might seem absurdly easy if you are comfortable with similarity.

\[\frac{1}{?} ~=~ \frac{3.77}{4.52}\]

\[?~=~ \frac{4.52}{3.77} ~=~ 1.198939\]

I am trying to introduce the radian, which is the arc length of a unit circle.

  • Massachusetts learning objective F-TF.A.1:
    • Understand radian measure of an angle as the length of the arc on the unit circle subtended by the angle.

If you look up circular arc on wikipedia, youll see a formula relating the arc length (\(L\)) to radius (\(r\)) and the central angle measured in radians (\(\theta\)). \[L~=~r\theta\]

So, in our case:

\[4.52~=~(3.77)(\theta)\]

\[\theta~=~\frac{4.52}{3.77}~=~ 1.198939\]
