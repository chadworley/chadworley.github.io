  1. Question

    You wish to simulate how long your morning commute takes. You break the commute into two parts:

    • Moving time
      • Approximately normal with \(\mu=10\) minutes and \(\sigma=1.2\) minutes.
      • In a spreadsheet, this is simulated with =norminv(rand(),10,1.2)
    • Waiting time due to 6 intersections.
      • Each intersection has a 0.35 chance to be green. If the light is not green, you will wait up to 3.5 minutes (following a uniform distribution).
      • In a spreadsheet, each intersection is simulated with =if(rand()<0.35, 0, rand()*3.5)

    Simulate 1000 repetitions. In each repetition, get the total time (moving and waiting). Find the 2.5th percentile and the 97.5th percentile to find the boundaries of the middle 95% of totals. Divide this interval’s width by 2 to get the margin of error.

    What is your simulated margin of error?


    Solution

    You should get a margin of error near 6.08 minutes. The tolerance should take anything from 5.55 and 6.61 minutes.

    I’ve shown a histogram of my simulation below.


    • In a spreadsheet, put =norminv(rand(),10,1.2) in cells A1:A1000.
    • Put =if(rand()<0.35, 0, rand()*3.5) in cells B1:G1000.
    • In cell I1, put =sum(A1:G1). Drag formula down to cell I1000.
    • Freeze the simulation by copying everything and pasting values only.
    • Sort sheet by column I.
    • Find the values at I25 and I975. These are the boundaries of the interval of typical totals.
    • Subtract those boundaries and divide the result by 2 to find the margin of error.
