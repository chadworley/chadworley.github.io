  1. Question

    S-IC.A.2: Decide if a specified model is consistent with results from a given data-generating process, e.g., using simulation.

    On an alien planet, adult blue-coated bullfrogs’ masses are normally distributed with a mean equaling \(\mu=360\) grams and a standard deviation equaling \(\sigma=19\) grams. You can simulate measuring a random bullfrog’s mass with =NORMINV(RAND(),360,19) in a spreadsheet.

    You “cultivate” some of these bullfrogs on your spaceship. You find that in a sample of \(n=6\) adult bullfrogs, you get a sample mean \(\bar{x}=346.76\) grams.

    You wonder whether the spaceship conditions have caused a significant change in average mass. To answer this, you repeatedly simulate the null hypothesis. To do this, you simulate getting the mean mass from 6 adults from the alien planet, and repeat that simulation 1000 times. From those 1000 repetitions, you will get 1000 means. Find the 2.5th percentile and the 97.5th percentile to get the interval of typical means. (Pick the closest answer.)

    • Lower boundary of interval of typical means:
    • Upper boundary of interval of typical means:

    Is the result of a sample mean equaling \(\bar{x}=346.76\) grams, with sample size of \(n=6\) bullfrogs, consistent with the simulation’s interval of typical means?

    • Is the result consistent with simulation?


    Solution

    • In cells A1:F1000, put =NORMINV(RAND(),360,19). Notice each row has 6 bullfrog masses.
    • In cell H1 put =AVERAGE(A1:F1). Drag the formula down through cell H1000. You now have 1000 means.
    • In cell I1, put =PERCENTILE(H1:H1000,0.025). This is your lower boundary of the interval of typical means. Find the closest answer.
    • In cell I2, put =PERCENTILE(H1:H1000,0.975). This is your upper boundary of the interval of typical means. Find the closest answer.
    • If the result (\(\bar{x}=346.76\)) is inside the interval, the last answer is YES. Otherwise, the last answer is NO.

    The best answers are: 344.8, 375.1, and “yes”.

  4. Question

    S-IC.B.4: Use data from a sample survey to estimate a population mean or proportion; develop a margin of error through the use of simulation models for random sampling.

    A scientist wants to estimate the average mass of adult lab rats under certain conditions. She randomly selects a sample of \(n=9\) mice from the population, and finds their masses in grams, to the nearest gram.

    491 493 485 473 469 494 454 468 476


    Your goals are:

    • Decide a best estimate for the population mean. (Hint: the sample mean.)
    • Find a 95% confidence interval by resampling 1000 times.
    • Find the margin of error (by dividing the interval width by 2).

    Your answers (pick closest to your answer):

    • What is the best estimate for the population mean? grams.
    • Lower boundary of confidence interval = grams.
    • Upper boundary of confidence interval = grams.
    • Margin of error =


    Solution

    • The best guess for the population mean is the sample mean. \(\bar{x}=478\).
    • The lower boundary = 469
    • The upper boundary = 486
    • The margin of error \(= \frac{486-469}{2} = 8\)

    Use a new spreadsheet.

    • Paste data into A1:I1.
    • To find sample mean, in cell C3, type =AVERAGE(A1:I1).
    • The key formula for resampling will be =INDEX($A$1:$I$1,1,RANDBETWEEN(1,9)).
    • In all cells from M1 to V1, put the formula.
    • Notice there are 9 values, just like the original sample. However there will be more repetition, because these 9 values are made by resampling with replacement.
    • To repeat 1000 times, select range M1:V1 and use ctrl+shift+down and ctrl+enter.
    • Now, we want an average for each row. In K1 write =AVERAGE(M1:V1).
    • Copy the average formula down for all rows. You now have 1000 averages from resampling.
    • Freeze the page with ctrl+a to select everything, ctrl+c to copy, and ctrl+shift+v to paste values only.
    • Sort column K.
    • In an empty cell, type =K25. In another empty cell, type =K975. These are the boundaries of the 95% confidence interval.

    To find the margin of error, find half the difference of the boundaries.

    Aria Dante
    213 207
    214 220
    210 205
    240 224
    214 196
    253 234
    242 228
    180



    To run the test, first calculate the observed absolute difference of sample means.

    • Aria’s sample mean = \(\bar{x}_1 =\)
    • Dante’s sample mean = \(\bar{x}_2 =\)
    • The absolute difference = \(|\bar{x}_2 - \bar{x}_1| =\)

    Permutation Test

    1. Reshuffle those two samples 1000 times. I’d recommend this tool, which wants comma-separated data, so here are the samples in that format:

      213, 214, 210, 240, 214, 253, 242

      207, 220, 205, 224, 196, 234, 228, 180

      Notice this reshuffling is equivalent to putting all the scores on cards, shuffling them all together, and then dealing into two piles (repeated 1000 times). Notice, Aria had 7 scores, so one pile always gets 7 numbers, and the other pile gets 8 numbers. We are simulating the hypothesis that maybe both people’s sample came from equivalent populations with a difference of sample means from natural variation, not unequal skill. We will then see if the observed difference is consistent with the simulated differences.

    2. Paste the 1000 rows into a spreadsheet.

    3. For each row, find the absolute difference of the means. Remember, spreadsheets have =ABS() for absolute value and =AVERAGE() to find a mean.

    1. What proportion of the time was the simulation as big or bigger? (In statistics this is called the \(p\)-value.) (Choose an interval.)
    1. If the \(p\)-value is less than 0.05, then the difference is significant. Otherwise, the difference is not significant. Is the difference significant?
    1. So, then, can we conclude that Aria is significantly better than Dante at archery?

    • \(\bar{x}_2= 211.75\)
    • \(|\bar{x}_2-\bar{x}_1| = 14.821\)
    • Your number of sim diffs less than the obs diff “should” be between 837 and 886. So you would pick interval \((800,900)\).
    • Your number of sim diffs greater than or equal to the obs diff “should” be between 114 and 163. So you would pick interval \((100,200)\).
    • Your \(p\)-value should be between 0.114 and 0.163. So, you would pick interval \((0.1,0.2)\)
