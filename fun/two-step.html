Shared Qs (two-step)


  1. Question

    Solve the equation below. \[61=5 x+6\]


    Solution

    We know that 61 equals the result when we start with \(x\) and then multiply by 5 and then add 6. In order to undo those operations, apply the inverse operations in reverse order: subtract 6 and then divide by 5.

    \[x = \frac{61-6}{5}\]

    So,

    \[x=11\]
