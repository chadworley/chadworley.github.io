Shared Qs (two-step)


  1. Question

    Solve the equation below. \[2=\frac{x-7}{2}\]


    Solution

    We know that 2 equals the result when we start with \(x\) and then subtract 7 and then divide by 2. In order to undo those operations, apply the inverse operations in reverse order: multiply by 2 and then add 7.

    \[x = 2\cdot 2+7\]

    So,

    \[x=11\]
