  1. Question

    Consider the quadratic equation with a standard-form quadratic expression on the left side and a constant on the right side.

    \[x^{2}-{13}x+{34}~=~-2\]

    Find all solutions.


    1. \(x=-12\)
    2. \(x=-11\)
    3. \(x=-10\)
    4. \(x=-9\)
    5. \(x=-8\)
    6. \(x=-7\)
    7. \(x=-6\)
    8. \(x=-5\)
    9. \(x=-4\)
    10. \(x=-3\)
    11. \(x=-2\)
    12. \(x=-1\)
    13. \(x=0\)
    14. \(x=1\)
    15. \(x=2\)
    16. \(x=3\)
    17. \(x=4\)
    18. \(x=5\)
    19. \(x=6\)
    20. \(x=7\)
    21. \(x=8\)
    22. \(x=9\)
    23. \(x=10\)
    24. \(x=11\)
    25. \(x=12\)

    Solution

    Make right side equal to zero by adding 2 to both sides.

    \[x^{2}-{13}x+{36}=0\]

    Factor the left-side expression.

    \[(x-{9}) (x-{4})=0\]

    The zero-product property tells us that if a product equals 0, then at least one of the factors of the product equals 0. In this case, we know the product of \((x-{9})\) and \((x-{4})\) equals zero. This means to satisfy the equation either \(x-{9}\) equals zero or \(x-{4}\) equals zero. Solve each of these using addition or subtraction.

    • First solution: \[x-{9}=0\] \[x=9\]

    • Second solution: \[x-{4}=0\] \[x=4\]

    Thus, the two solutions are \(x=9\) and \(x=4\).
