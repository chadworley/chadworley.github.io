  1. Question

    Consider the quadratic expression shown in standard form:

    \[x^{2}-{13}x+{36}\]

    In factored form, the expression can be represented as the product of two factors. What are those factors?


    1. \(x-{10}\)
    2. \(x-{9}\)
    3. \(x-{8}\)
    4. \(x-{7}\)

    19. \(x+{9}\)
    20. \(x+{10}\)

    Solution

    You want two numbers that have a sum of -13 and a product of 36.

    You can get this by guessing and checking. Eventually you might guess the following factored form:

    \[(x-{9})\cdot(x-{4})\]

    You can check by using polynomial multiplication (in this case you can use the FOIL method).

    * \(x\) \(-9\)
    \(x\) \(x^2\) \({-9}x\)
    \(-4\) \({-4}x\) \(36\)

    \[p(x) ~=~ x^{2}-{9}x-{4}x+{36}\]

    \[p(x) ~=~ x^{2}-{13}x+{36}\]

    To spell it out in more detail, you want two numbers that have a sum of -13 and a product of 36. So, you start thinking of factor pairs of 36 until you find a pair that sums to -13.     Factor pairs of 36 Factored polynomial Expanded polynomial
    1 and 36 \((x+{1})\cdot (x+{36})\) \(x^{2}+{37}x+{36}\)
    -1 and -36 \((x-{1})\cdot (x-{36})\) \(x^{2}-{37}x+{36}\)
    2 and 18 \((x+{2})\cdot (x+{18})\) \(x^{2}+{20}x+{36}\)
    -2 and -18 \((x-{2})\cdot (x-{18})\) \(x^{2}-{20}x+{36}\)
