  1. Question

    Jason drew two overlapping rectangles on a 10x10 grid.


    Let’s call the red rectangle A and the blue rectangle B. Find the following areas:

    • Area covered by A = squares
    • Area covered by B = squares
    • Area covered by the intersection of A and B = squares
    • Area covered by the union of A and B = squares
    • Area covered by ONLY A (and not B) = squares
    • Area covered by ONLY B (and not A) = squares
    • Area not covered (neither A nor B) = squares


    Solution

    • Area covered by A = 48
    • Area covered by B = 49
    • Area covered by the intersection of A and B = 42
    • Area covered by the union of A and B = 55
    • Area covered by ONLY A (and not B) = 6
    • Area covered by ONLY B (and not A) = 7
    • Area not covered (neither A nor B) = 45

    Calculate the following volumes:

    • What is the volume remaining when object A is subtracted from object B? \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • What is the volume of the union? \(\mathrm{m^3}\)


    Solution

    • The volume of the intersection is 18 cubic meters.
    • The volume remaining when object A is subtracted from object B is 4 cubic meters.
    • The volume of the union is 33 cubic meters.
