  1. Question

    To answer this question, open a new SketchUp with Decimal - Meters as your units. In this problem, assume all lengths are in meters and all volumes are in cubic meters.

    • Generate a rectangular prism, with \(w=5.1\), \(d=9.8\), and \(h=9.3\), with a corner on the origin:
      • Draw a rectangle with one corner on the origin. Dimensions: 5.1 by 9.8. Make sure the rectangle will pull up along the blue axis.
      • Pull the rectangle up by 9.3 meters (in direction of blue axis).
      • Triple click the object to highlight everything.
      • Right click and Make Group.
    • Generate a cylinder with radius \(r=4.4\) and height \(h=15.1\) so that the bottom face’s center is at the origin.
      • Draw a circle with radius \(4.4\) with its center at the origin. Make sure the circle will pull up along the blue axis.
      • Pull the circle up by 15.1 meters (in direction of blue axis).
      • Triple click the object to highlight everything.
      • Right click and Make Group.

    We will practice using the Union, Intersection, and Subtract functions in SketchUp. To find the volume of an object, click on it and then open the Entity Info (button is top-right of window). The volume will be shown there.


    • What is the volume of the rectangular prism?
    • \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • What is the volume of the cylinder?
    • \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • What is the volume of the union between the prism and the cylinder?
    • \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • What is the volume of the intersection between the prism and the cylinder?
    • \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • What is the volume of the subtraction of the cylinder from prism? (click cylinder then prism)
    • \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • What is the volume of the subtraction of the prism from cylinder? (click prism then cylinder)
    • \(\mathrm{m^3}\)


    Solution

    • The volume of the rectangular prism: 465 \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • The volume of the cylinder: 908 \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • The volume of the union: 1233 \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • The volume of the intersection: 140 \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • The volume of the subtraction of cylinder from prism: 325 \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
    • The volume of the subtraction of prism from cylinder: 768 \(\mathrm{m^3}\)
