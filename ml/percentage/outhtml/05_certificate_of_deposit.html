  1. Question

    Consider a certificate of deposit (CD) with 2.61% APY and a maturity term of 8 years. An investor invests $14000 in this CD. When the CD matures (after 8 years), how much money can be withdrawn?


    Solution

    Each year the value increases by 2.61%, and this compounding occurs 8 times.

    Find the scale factor. \[1+0.0261~=~ 1.0261\]

    Multiply the principal ($14000) by the scale factor 8 times. Remember, repeated multiplication can be represented with an exponent.

    \[14000\cdot 1.0261^{8}~=~ 17204.64\]

    So, after 8 years, this CD is worth $17204.64. You need to remember to withdraw in a short grace period, otherwise the money will typically roll into a new CD, and you’ll need to wait another 8 years to withdraw without penalties.
