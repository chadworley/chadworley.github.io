  1. Question

    At \(t=0\) minutes, a cup of coffee is 77 Kelvin hotter than the surrounding air. Each minute, this amount decreases by 7.73%. After 11 minutes, how many Kelvin above surrounding-air temperature is the cup of coffee?


    Solution

    Find the scale factor.

    \[1-0.0773~=~0.9227\]

    Multiply the initial amount by the scale factor 11 times.

    \[77\cdot 0.9227^{11}~=~31.7801873\]

    So, after 11 minutes, the coffee is only 32 Kelvin hotter than the surrounding air.
