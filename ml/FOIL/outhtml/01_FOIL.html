  1. Question

    The equivalence below is true for any value of \(x\).

    \[(x-6)(x-3) \equiv x^2+Bx+C\]

    Find \(B\) and \(C\).

    • \(B=\)
    • \(C=\)


    Solution

    FOIL!

    \[(x-6)(x-3)~\equiv~x^2+(-3)x+(-6)x+(18)\]

    Combine like terms and simplify.

    \[(x-6)(x-3)~\equiv~x^2-9x+18\]

    So,

    \[B=-9\] \[C=18\]
