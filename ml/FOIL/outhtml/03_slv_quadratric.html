    Solve \(x^{2} + 13 x + 36=0\) by factoring.


    1. -10
    2. -9
    3. -8

    13. 2

    17. 6
    18. 7
    19. 8
    20. 9
    21. 10

    Solution

    By guess and check, we find the two integers with sum 13 and product 36 are \(4\) and \(9\).

    The sum: \[(4)+(9) = 13\]

    The product: \[(4)(9) = 36\] This allows us to write the equation in factored form.

    \[\left(x + 4\right) \left(x + 9\right) = 0 \]

    The zero-product rule lets us find the solutions.

    \[x = -4\] \[x = -9\]
