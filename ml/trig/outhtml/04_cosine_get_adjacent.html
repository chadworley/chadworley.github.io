04_cosine_get_adjacent


  1. Question

    A right triangle has an angle of 0.67 radians. The hypotenuse has a length of 44 meters. How long, in meters, is the leg adjacent the angle?


    Please round your answer to the nearest meter.


    Solution

    First, identify the correct trigonometric function. Remember, SOHCAHTOA.

    \[\sin(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\cos(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{adjacent}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\tan(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{adjacent}}\]

    In this case, we are given the angle and the hypotenuse, and we are asked for the adjacent leg. The trigonometric ratio that relates these two sides is cosine. Let \(x\) represent the requested length.

    \[\cos(0.67) = \frac{x}{44}\] Multiply both sides by 44 to solve for \(x\).

    \[44 \cdot \cos(0.67) = x\] To evaluate \(x\), type the following into a modern calculator: round(44*cos(0.67)). Make sure to be in radian mode, not degree mode.

    \[x \approx 34\]
