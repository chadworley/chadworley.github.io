06_cosine_get_angle


  1. Question

    A right triangle has an angle measure \(\theta\). The adjacent leg’s length is 34 meters, and the hypotenuse’s length is 44 meters. What is the angle measure \(\theta\) in radians?


    Please round your answer to the nearest hundredth of a radian.


    Solution

    First, identify the correct trigonometric function. Remember, SOHCAHTOA.

    \[\sin(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\cos(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{adjacent}}{\mathrm{hypotenuse}}\]

    \[\tan(\theta)=\frac{\mathrm{opposite}}{\mathrm{adjacent}}\]

    In this case, we are given the adjacent and the hypotenuse, and we are asked for the angle measure. The trigonometric ratio that relates these two sides is cosine. Use \(\theta\) to represent the requested angle measure.

    \[\cos(\theta) = \frac{34}{44}\]

    To evaluate \(\theta\), we need to use the inverse of cosine. This inverse function has a few names: arccos, acos, or \(\cos^{-1}\) are the most commonly used.

    \[\theta = \arccos\left(\frac{34}{44}\right)\]

    \[\theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{34}{44}\right)\]

    Depending on your calculator, you might type something like round(arccos(34/44),2)

    \[\theta \approx 0.69\]
