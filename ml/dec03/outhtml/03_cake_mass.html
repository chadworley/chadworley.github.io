  1. Question

    A three-tiered cake is constructed with three cylinders. Each cylinder has a height of 16 centimeters. The radii are 9 centimeters, 19 centimeters, and 29 centimeters.

    plot of chunk unnamed-chunk-1

    The volume (\(V\)) of each cylinder can be calulated with the following formula, where \(r\) is radius and \(h\) is height.

    \[V~=~\pi r^2 h\]

    You can get the total volume by adding up the volumes of all three cylinders.

    After baked, the density of the cake is 0.84 grams per cubic centimeter (this is without the frosting). You can get the total mass by multiplying the volume by the density.

    What is the mass of the cake (in grams)?


    Solution

    Get the volume of each cylinder.

    \[V_1 ~=~ \pi (9)^2 (16) ~=~ 4071.5 ~\mathrm{cm^3}\] \[V_2 ~=~ \pi (19)^2 (16) ~=~ 18146 ~\mathrm{cm^3}\] \[V_3 ~=~ \pi (29)^2 (16) ~=~ 42273 ~\mathrm{cm^3}\]

    Get the total volume.

    \[V ~=~ 4071.5+18146+42273 ~=~ 64500 ~\mathrm{cm^3}\]

    Multiply the volume by the density to get the mass.

    \[64500 ~\mathrm{cm^3} \cdot \frac{0.84~\mathrm{g}}{1~\mathrm{cm^3}} ~=~ 54180 ~\mathrm{g}\]
