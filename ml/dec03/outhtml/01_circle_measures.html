  1. Question

    A 2D circle is the collection of points that are equally far from its center point. That distance from the center to the edge is called the radius (\(r\)). The distance across the circle, through the center, is called the diameter (\(d\)). The distance going all the way around the edge is called the circumference (\(c\)). Lastly, the area (\(A\)) can be calculated from the radius.


    Here are the formulas people memorize:

    \[d = 2r\] \[c = \pi d\]

    \[A=\pi r^2\]

    Fill out the table below.

    \(r\) \(d\) \(c\) \(A\)
    circle 1 4.06
    circle 2 10.7
    circle 3 40.2
    circle 4 245


    Solution

    Here is the solution:

    \(r\) \(d\) \(c\) \(A\)
    circle 1 4.06 8.12 25.5 51.8
    circle 2 5.34 10.7 33.6 89.6
    circle 3 6.4 12.8 40.2 129
    circle 4 8.83 17.7 55.5 245

    Use the formulas. You might need to do a tiny bit of algebra. For example, in the last row, to get the radius from the area of 245, use \(A=\pi r^2\)â€¦

    \[245~=~\pi r^2\]

    Divide both sides by \(\pi\).

    \[\frac{245}{\pi}~=~r^2\]

    Take the square root of both sides.

    \[\sqrt{\frac{245}{\pi}}~=~r\]

    Evaluate. \(r\approx8.83\)
