  11. Question

    Find the mean of the following list: \[42, 48, 44, 49, 41\]

    (Do not round your answer.)


    Solution

    Divide the total by the count.

    \[\frac{42+48+44+49+41}{5} = 44.8\]

    Ill point out that you could recognize that all numbers have in the 40s. To simplify the arithmetic, you could instead just find the average of the ones digits and add 40: \[40+\frac{2+8+4+9+1}{5} ~=~ 44.8\]

  31. Question

    Depending on the type of distribution, we can make a strong claim regarding the mean and median.

    • Symmetric (bell, uniform, bimodal)
      • \(\text{mean} \approx \text{median}\)
    • Skew-right
      • \(\text{mean} > \text{median}\)
    • Skew-left
      • \(\text{mean} < \text{median}\)

    A sample was gathered and visualized with a histogram.


    What claim can you make regarding the mean and median?


    1. \(\text{mean} \approx \text{median}\)
    2. \(\text{mean} > \text{median}\)
    3. \(\text{mean} < \text{median}\)

    Solution

    video

    The distribution is symmetric, so \(\text{mean} \approx \text{median}\).

    3. Which sample has the largest median?
    4. Which sample has the smallest median?
    5. Which sample has the largest IQR?
    6. Which sample has the smallest IQR?


    Solution

    video

    1. Z. You find the sample with a whisker that goes furthest to the right.
    2. V. You find the sample with a whisker that goes furthest to the left.

    4. V. You find the sample with the median (thick line in middle of box) furthest to the left.
    5. Z. You find the sample with the widest box.
    6. Y. You find the sample with the narrowest box.
