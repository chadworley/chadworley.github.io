  21. Question

    Expand the following polynomial.

    \[\left(x - 4\right)^{2}\]

    Answer: \(x^2\)



    Solution

    The answer is \(x^{2} - 8 x + 16\).

    There are multiple methods for expanding a product of two binomials. We begin by rewriting the product. \[(x - 4)(x - 4)\]

    Method 1: Distribution

    The distributive property promises us that for all \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\): \[a(b+c) ~\equiv~ ab+ac\]

    Since we are expanding \((x - 4)(x - 4)\), we can say \(a=(x - 4)\) and \(b=x\) and \(c=-4\), leading us to \[(x - 4)(x)~+~(x - 4)(-4)\] Multiplication is commutative, meaning the order does not matter. It promises that \(de \equiv ed\) for all \(d\) and \(e\). We can rearrange the products in order to reuse the distributive property.

    \[(x)(x - 4)~+~(-4)(x - 4)\]

    For each term, \((x)(x - 4)\) and \((-4)(x - 4)\), reuse the distributive property.

    \[x^2 ~+~ (-4)x ~+~ (-4)x ~+~ (16) \] Notice the two like terms: \(-4x\) and \(-4x\). Combine the like terms to get the final answer.

    \[x^{2} - 8 x + 16\]

    Method 2: FOIL

    The acronym FOIL stands for products of Firsts, Outsides, Insides, Lasts. For any \((a+b)(c+d)\):

    • Firsts: \(ac\)
    • Outsides: \(ad\)
    • Insides: \(bc\)
    • Lasts: \(bd\)

    In our example, \((x - 4)(x - 4)\), we find the products:

    • Firsts: \(x^2\)
    • Outsides: \(-4x\)
    • Insides: \(-4x\)
    • Lasts: \(16\)

    When we add together those products, we get:

    \[x^2 ~+~ (-4)x ~+~ (-4)x ~+~ (16) \] Notice the two like terms: \(-4x\) and \(-4x\). Combine the like terms to get the final answer.

    \[x^{2} - 8 x + 16\]

    Method 3: Box

    The factored form has two factors: \((x - 4)\) and \((x - 4)\). The two terms in the first factor are \(x\) and \(-4\). The two terms in the second factor are \(x\) and \(-4\). We put all four terms on the outside of the box. Then we find the products.

    \(x\) \(-4\)
    \(x\) \(x^2\) \(-4x\)
    \(-4\) \(-4x\) \(16\)

    We write a sum of those 4 products.

    \[x^2 ~+~ (-4)x ~+~ (-4)x ~+~ (16) \] Notice the two like terms: \(-4x\) and \(-4x\). Combine the like terms to get the final answer.

    \[x^{2} - 8 x + 16\]

    Method 4: sum and product

    Notice, we started with factored form: \[\left(x - 4\right)^{2}\]

    The sum of -4 and -4 is -8. \[(-4)+(-4)=-8\] The product of -4 and -4 is 16. \[(-4)(-4) = 16\]

    The sum and product show up consistently in the expanded form:

    \[x^{2} - 8 x + 16\]
