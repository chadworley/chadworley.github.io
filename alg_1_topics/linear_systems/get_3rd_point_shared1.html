  1. Question

    A line goes through two points: (1, -2) and (4, 2).

    On the same line, if \(x=-6\), then what is \(y\)? In other words, a third point on the line is (-6, \(y_3\)), and your goal is to find \(y_3\).

    The tolerance is \(\pm 0.01\).


    Solution

    Find the slope.

    \[m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\] \[m = \frac{(2)-(-2)}{(4)-(1)}\]

    \[m=1.3333333\]

    Find the \(y\)-intercept of the line (\(b\)) by using slope-intercept form and the first point.

    \[y = mx+b\]

    \[-2 = (1.3333333)(1)+b \]

    \[-2 = 1.3333333+b \]

    Subtract 1.3333333 from both sides.

    \[-3.3333333 = b\]

    So the \(y\)-intercept equals \(b=-3.3333333\).

    Now, plug in the third \(x\) value.

    \[y = mx+b\]

    \[y_3 = (1.3333333)(-6)+(-3.3333333)\]

    \[y_3 = -11.3333333\]
