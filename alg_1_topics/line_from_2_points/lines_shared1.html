Shared Qs (lines)


  1. Question

    A line goes through two points: \((-2, -5)\) and \((2, 3)\). Find the equation of the line in slope-intercept form.

    \(y =\) \(x\)



    Solution

    Slope intercept form is \(y=mx+b\), where \(m\) is slope and \(b\) is y-intercept.

    First, calculate the slope. \[m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\]

    \[m = \frac{(3)-(-5)}{(2)-(-2)} = \frac{8}{4}\]

    \[m=2\]

    Plug in the second point \((2, 3)\) into the slope intercept form, leaving \(b\) unknown.

    \[3 = \left(2\right)(2)+b\]

    Solve for \(b\).

    \[b = 3-\left(2\right)(2)\]

    \[b=-1\]

    So the line’s equation, in slope-intercept form, is:

    \[y = 2 x -1\]

  21. Question

    A line goes through two points: (3, 5) and (6, -5).

    On the same line, if \(x=8\), then what is \(y\)? In other words, a third point on the line is (8, \(y_3\)), and your goal is to find \(y_3\).

    The tolerance is \(\pm 0.01\).


    Solution

    Find the slope.

    \[m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\] \[m = \frac{(-5)-(5)}{(6)-(3)}\]

    \[m=-3.3333333\]

    Find the \(y\)-intercept of the line (\(b\)) by using slope-intercept form and the first point.

    \[y = mx+b\]

    \[5 = (-3.3333333)(3)+b \]

    \[5 = -10+b \]

    Subtract -10 from both sides.

    \[15 = b\]

    So the \(y\)-intercept equals \(b=15\).

    Now, plug in the third \(x\) value.

    \[y = mx+b\]

    \[y_3 = (-3.3333333)(8)+(15)\]

    \[y_3 = -11.6666667\]
