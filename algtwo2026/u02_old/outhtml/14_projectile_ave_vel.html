  1. Question

    On an alien planet, a projectile is shot upward. The gravity is -4.2 m/s\(^2\) and the initial upward velocity (at \(t=0\)) is 43.1 m/s. The effects of air resistance are minimal, so that the height in meters, \(h\), is a quadratic function in time, \(t\), in seconds.

    \[h(t)~=~-2.1t^2+43.1t\]

    What is the average velocity, in meters per second, of the projectile between \(t=11.8\) seconds and \(t=18.6\) seconds?


    Solution

    \[\bar{v}~=~\frac{\Delta h}{\Delta t}~=~ \frac{h(18.6)\,-\,h(11.8)}{18.6-11.8}\]

    \[\bar{v}~=~ \frac{75.144\,-\,216.176}{6.8}\]

    \[\bar{v}~=~ -20.74\,\mathrm{m/s}\]

    Personally, I would use Desmos:

    1. Define \(h(t)~=~-2.1t^2+43.1t\)
    2. Evaluate \(\frac{h(18.6)\,-\,h(11.8)}{18.6-11.8}\).
      • Answer = -20.74
