Distributing a negative to multiple terms

\[f(x)~=~-\left(x^7-x^6-x^5+x^4+x^3+x^2-x+1\right)\]

Problem 1

Let function \(f\) be defined with the following polynomial.

\[f(x)~=~3x^7+5x^6+x^5-x^4-4x^3+7x^2+6x+1\]

Write a polynomial expression, without any parentheses, that is equivalent to \(f(-a)\).

\[f(-a)~=~ ?\]

  • Substitute \((-a)\) for each instance of \(x\). \[f(-a)~=~ 3(-a)^7+5(-a)^6+(-a)^5-(-a)^4-4(-a)^3+7(-a)^2+6(-a)+1\]
  • Use rules of negatives raised to integer powers.
    • A negative raised to an even power is positive.
    • A negative raised to an odd power is negative.
  • So, whether each term has a \(+\) or \(-\) in front depends on whether the original term had a \(+\) or \(-\) and whether the power was even or odd. \[f(-a)~=~ -3a^7+5a^6-a^5-a^4+4a^3+7a^2-6a+1\]
  • I should say, we did not need to use \(a\). We could have used \(x\). \[f(-x)~=~ -3x^7+5x^6-x^5-x^4+4x^3+7x^2-6x+1\]
  • We can graph curve \(y=3x^7+5x^6+x^5-x^4-4x^3+7x^2+6x+1\) and curve \(y=-3x^7+5x^6-x^5-x^4+4x^3+7x^2-6x+1\). What rigid transformation will relate the two curves?
  • The two curves will be horizontal reflections over the \(y\) axis.

\[f(x)~=~6x^3+4x^2-x+8\]

  • Replace each instance of \(x\) with \(-a\). Then, multiply the whole thing by \(-1\) (by putting a negative symbol in front and parentheses around the whole polynomial). \[-f(-a)~=~-\left(6(-a)^3+4(-a)^2-(-a)+8\right)\]
  • Use rules about negatives raised to powers to simplify. \[-f(-a)~=~-\left(-6a^3+4a^2+a+8\right)\]
  • Distribute the negative to every term. \[-f(-a)~=~6a^3-4a^2-a+8\]

Shortcut rules

  • Each term of a polynomial is either ODD powered or EVEN powered.
  • To find \(f(-x)\) from polynomial \(f(x)\), switch sign on ODD powered terms.
  • To find \(-f(-x)\) from polynomial \(f(x)\), switch sign on EVEN powered terms.
  • To find \(-f(x)\) from polynomial \(f(x)\), switch sign on ALL terms.
  • The linear term (like \(-5x\) or \(+7x\)) is an ODD powered term, because \(x^1\equiv x\).
  • The constant term (like \(-5\) or \(+7\)) is an EVEN powered term, because \(x^0 \equiv 1\) if we ignore the special case of \(0^0\), which is undefined.

Define an EVEN function

  • A function is even if and only if \(f(x)=f(-x)\) for all values of \(x\) in the domain of the function.
  • An example of an even polynomial is \(f(x)=5x^8+4x^6-3x^4+9x^2-7\).
    • Notice it only has EVEN powered terms.
  • In an even function opposite inputs give equal outputs.

Define an ODD function

  • A function is odd if and only if \(f(x)=-f(-x)\) for all values of \(x\) in the domain of the function.
  • An example of an odd polynomial is \(f(x)=5x^9+4x^7-3x^5+9x^3-7x\).
    • Notice it only has ODD powered terms.
  • In an odd function opposite inputs give opposite outputs.

Is it possibleâ¦

Can a function be neither even nor odd?

  • Indeed, most functions are neither even nor odd. For example \(y=2x+3\) is neither even nor odd because it is the sum of an odd term (the linear term, \(2x\)) and an even term (the constant, \(3\)).
  • You could prove this by recognizing \(2(-x)+3\) is not equivalent to \(2x+3\), and also \(-(2(-x)+3)\) is not equivalent to \(2x+3\).
