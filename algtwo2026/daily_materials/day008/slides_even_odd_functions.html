Each term of a polynomial is either ODD powered or EVEN powered.

To find \(f(-x)\) from polynomial \(f(x)\), switch sign on ODD powered terms.

To find \(-f(-x)\) from polynomial \(f(x)\), switch sign on EVEN powered terms.

To find \(-f(x)\) from polynomial \(f(x)\), switch sign on ALL terms.

The linear term (like \(-5x\) or \(+6x\)) is an ODD powered term, because \(x^1\equiv x\). (And 1 is ODD.)

The constant term (like \(-5\) or \(+6\)) is an EVEN powered term, because \(x^0 \equiv 1\). (And 0 is EVEN.)

Graphical examples

  • Let two functions be defined with polynomials: \[f(x)=x^5+x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1\] \[g(x)=-x^5+x^4-x^3+x^2-x+1\] What type of reflection relates these two functions?

  • Since the ODD powered terms have their signs changed, it seems \(g(x)~=~f(-x)\), so the two curves \(y=f(x)\) and \(y=g(x)\) are horizontal reflections over the \(y\) axis.

  • Let two functions be defined with polynomials: \[f(x)=x^5+x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1\] \[h(x)=-x^5-x^4-x^3-x^2-x-1\] What type of reflection relates these two functions?

  • Since ALL terms have their signs changed, it seems \(h(x)~=~-f(x)\), so the two curves \(y=f(x)\) and \(y=h(x)\) are vertical reflections over the \(x\) axis.

  • Let two functions be defined with polynomials: \[f(x)=x^5+x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1\] \[j(x)=x^5-x^4+x^3-x^2+x-1\] What type of reflection relates these two functions?

  • Since the EVEN powered terms have their signs changed, it seems \(j(x)~=~-f(-x)\), so the two curves \(y=f(x)\) and \(y=j(x)\) are 180Â° rotations around the origin.

Define an EVEN function

  • A function is even if and only if \(f(x)=f(-x)\) for all values of \(x\) in the domain of the function.
  • An example of an even polynomial is \(f(x)=5x^8+4x^6-3x^4+9x^2-7\).
    • Notice it only has EVEN powered terms.
  • In an even function opposite inputs give equal outputs.

Define an ODD function

  • A function is odd if and only if \(f(x)=-f(-x)\) for all values of \(x\) in the domain of the function.
  • An example of an odd polynomial is \(f(x)=5x^9+4x^7-3x^5+9x^3-7x\).
    • Notice it only has ODD powered terms.
  • In an odd function opposite inputs give opposite outputs.

Can a function be neither even nor odd?

  • Indeed, most functions are neither even nor odd. For example \(y=2x+3\) is neither even nor odd because it is the sum of an odd term (the linear term, \(2x\)) and an even term (the constant, \(3\)).
  • You could prove this by recognizing \(2(-x)+3\) is not equivalent to \(2x+3\), and also \(-(2(-x)+3)\) is not equivalent to \(2x+3\).
