  1. Question

    In real life, the average rate of change over a small time interval is used to approximate the instantaneous rate of change (like with a bicycle’s speedometer). The step size, \(h\), is the width of the interval, so we find the average rate of change of \(f(x)\) between \(x=a\) and \(x=a+h\):

    \[\text{AROC} = \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}\]

    Let function \(f\) be defined as follows: \[f(x) = - 5 x^{2} - 2 x + 5\] Evaluate the average rate of change of \(f\) at \(x=-6\) with a step size \(h=0.1\).

    You can round (or truncate) your final answer at the hundredths place.


    Solution

    \[f(-6)=-163\] \[f(-6+0.1)=-157.25\]

    \[\text{AROC} = \frac{(-157.25)-(-163)}{0.1} = 57.5 \]
