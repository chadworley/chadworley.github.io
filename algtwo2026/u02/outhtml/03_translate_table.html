  1. Question

    Let \(f(x)\) be defined by the table below:

    \(x\) \(f(x)\)
    2 6
    3 4
    5 3
    8 7

    So, for example, \(f(2)=6\).

    Let \(g(x)\) be defined with the equation \(g(x)=f(x+3)-1\). What are the 4 corresponding points on \(g\)? (Use same order.)

    \(x\) \(g(x)\)


    Solution

    \(x\) \(g(x)\)
    -1 5
    0 3
    2 2
    5 6
    • Notice \(g(-1)~=~f(-1 +3)-1~=~f(2)-1~=~6 -1 ~=~ 5\)
    • Notice \(g(0)~=~f(0 +3)-1~=~f(3)-1~=~4 -1 ~=~ 3\)
    • Notice \(g(2)~=~f(2 +3)-1~=~f(5)-1~=~3 -1 ~=~ 2\)
    • Notice \(g(5)~=~f(5 +3)-1~=~f(8)-1~=~7 -1 ~=~ 6\)

    The “shortcut” is to recognize that the definition \(g(x)=f(x+3)-1\) implies a horizontal shift of left by 3 and a vertical shift of down by 1. The horizontal shift is an additive/subtractive change to the inputs. The vertical shift is an additive/subtractive change to the outputs.
