  1. Question

    This question is about average rate of change. For function \(f(x)\), its average rate of change between \(x=a\) and \(x=b\) equals a quotient of differences: \[\text{ave rate of change} = \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} \]

    A function \(f\) is graphed below.


    • Find the average rate of change between \(x=4\) and \(x=7\).

    You can round to the nearest hundredth.


    Solution


    \[f(4)=2\]

    \[f(7)=6\]

    \[\text{ave rate of change} = \frac{f(7)-f(4)}{7-4} = \frac{6-2}{7-4} = \frac{4}{3} = 1.33 \]

    Notice the orange segment has length of 4 and the violet segment has length of 3. The formula for average rate of change is rise over run.
