  1. Question

    Let \(f\) be a function defined below for \(x\ge 0\):

    \[f(x)~=~\sqrt{48\cdot x}\,-\,x\]

    Notice \(f\) has a global extremum at point \((12, 12)\).

    A second function, \(g\), is created by randomly selecting \(a\) and \(b\) in the definitional equation below.

    \[g(x)~=~a\cdot f(bx)\]


    We can see that \(g\) is a stretched (and possibly reflected) version of \(f\). Finish the equality below.

    • \(g(x)~=~\) \(\cdot f(\) \(\cdot x)\)


    Solution

    Notice the extremum of \(g\) is at \((-18, -18)\).

    • The horizontal scale factor equals \(\frac{-18}{12} ~=~ \frac{-3}{2}\)
    • The vertical scale factor equals \(\frac{-18}{12} ~=~ \frac{-3}{2}\)

    The horizontal multiplicative factor (\(b\)) is the reciprocal of the horizontal scale factor.

    \[b~=~ \left(\frac{-3}{2}\right)^{-1} ~=~ \frac{-2}{3}\]

    The vertical multiplicative factor (\(a\)) is equal to the vertical scale factor.

    \[a~=~\frac{-3}{2}\]

    Thus,

    \[g(x) ~=~ \frac{-3}{2} \cdot f\left(\frac{-2}{3} \cdot x\right)\]
