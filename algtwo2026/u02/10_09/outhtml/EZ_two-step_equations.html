  1. Question

    Solve the equation below. \[56=(x+5)\cdot 8\]


    Solution

    Rewrite the given equation. \[56=(x+5)\cdot 8\] Divide both sides of the equation by 8.

    \[7=x+5\]

    Subtract 5 from both sides of the equation.

    \[2=x\]

    Switch sides (which is allowed because equality is commutative).

    \[x=2\]

    You can check your possible answer by substituting 2 for \(x\) in the original equation.

    \[56\stackrel{?}{=}((2)+5)\cdot 8\]

    \[56 \stackrel{?}{=} 7\cdot 8\]

    \[56 \stackrel{\checkmark}{=} 56\]

    So, indeed we see that \(x=2\) is a solution to the equation.
