The square-root function

  • The square-root function is typically indicated with a radical symbol. \[\text{square-root}(x) ~=~ \sqrt{x}\]

  • Notice the overline indicates the argument of the function. \[\sqrt{\mathrm{argument}}\]

  • The square-root function is an imperfect inverse of the squaring function. For example… \[\sqrt{3^2}=3\] \[\sqrt{4^2}=4\] \[\sqrt{(-4)^2}\ne -4\]

  • Here are some key input-output pairs: \[\sqrt{0}=0\] \[\sqrt{1}=1\] \[\sqrt{4}=2\] \[\sqrt{9}=3\] \[\sqrt{16}=4\] \[\sqrt{25}=5\]

  • Practice: evaluate the expression. \[2\cdot \sqrt{\frac{10}{2}+11}+4\]

Adding or Subtracting Inside and Out

Use these facts: \[\sqrt{0}=0\] \[\sqrt{1}=1\] \[\sqrt{4}=2\] \[\sqrt{9}=3\] \[\sqrt{16}=4\] \[\sqrt{25}=5\]
