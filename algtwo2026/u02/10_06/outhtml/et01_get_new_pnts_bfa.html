  1. Question

    Let \(f\) be a function with three known input-output pairs, shown as solutions to the equation \(b=f[a]\).

    \(a\) \(b\)
    32 44
    72 16
    88 43

    Find the corresponding solutions to the following equation: \[y~=~2\cdot f{\left[8\left(x+3\right)\right]}-6\]

    \(x\) \(y\)


    Solution

    Restate the given equation.

    \[y~=~2\cdot f{\left[8\left(x+3\right)\right]}-6\]

    Set \(f\)’s argument equal to \(a\).

    \[8\left(x+3\right)~=~a\]

    Solve the two-step equation.

    \[x~=~\frac{a}{8}-3\]

    Because we set \(8\left(x+3\right)\) equal to \(a\), we can replace \(8\left(x+3\right)\) with \(a\) in the main equation.

    \[y=2\cdot f{\left[a\right]}-6\]

    We know \(f[a]=b\), so replace \(f[a]\) with \(b\).

    \[y~=~2\cdot b-6\]

    We now have two formulas: \(x~=~\frac{a}{8}-3\) and \(y~=~2\cdot b-6\). Use those formulas!

    \(a\) \(b\) \(x\) \(y\)
    \[32\] \[44\] \[\frac{32}{8}-3\] \[2\cdot 44-6\]
    \[72\] \[16\] \[\frac{72}{8}-3\] \[2\cdot 16-6\]
    \[88\] \[43\] \[\frac{88}{8}-3\] \[2\cdot 43-6\]

    Evaluate all numerical expressions to get the answers.

    \(x\) \(y\)
    1 82
    6 26
    8 80
