  1. Question

    Consider the two functions \(f\) and \(g\), where \(g\) is defined as a transformation of \(f\), as shown below as an equation true for any value of \(x\):

    \[g[x]~=~2\cdot f{\left[8\left(x+3\right)\right]}-6\]

    We know the following points on the \(y=f[x]\) curve:

    \(x\) \(f[x]\)
    32 44
    40 28
    72 16
    88 43

    Find the corresponding points on the \(y=g[x]\) curve:

    \(x\) \(g[x]\)


    Solution

    Use the shortcut for \(x\), which tells us we can make a formula by inverting the argument expression. The argument expression says to add 3 and then multiply by 8, so the inverse must divide by 8 and then subtract 3.

    \[x_{\text{new}}~=~\frac{x_{\text{old}}}{8}-3\]

    Use the shortcut for \(y\), which tells us we can make a formula by just looking at the operations outside of the function.

    \[y_{\text{new}}~=~2\cdot y_{\text{old}}-6\]

    Use those formulas!

    \(x_{\text{old}}\) \(y_{\text{old}}\) \(x_{\text{new}}\) \(y_{\text{new}}\)
    \[32\] \[44\] \[\frac{32}{8}-3\] \[2\cdot 44-6\]
    \[40\] \[28\] \[\frac{40}{8}-3\] \[2\cdot 28-6\]
    \[72\] \[16\] \[\frac{72}{8}-3\] \[2\cdot 16-6\]
    \[88\] \[43\] \[\frac{88}{8}-3\] \[2\cdot 43-6\]

    And evaluate all the numerical expressions to get the answer. Notice we are only interested in \(x_{\text{new}}\) and \(y_{\text{new}}\).

    \(x\) \(g[x]\)
    1 82
    2 50
    6 26
    8 80
