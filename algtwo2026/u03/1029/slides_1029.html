Sacred Example!

You are given a quadratic expression in factored form: \[(6x+5)(7x-2)\]

Expand the expression into standard form using the BOX method.

Expand with box method

Given factored form:

\[(6x+5)(7x-2)\]

Use box.

ANSWER: \[42x^2+23x-10\]

Simpler question both binomials have linear coefficients of one

You are given a quadratic expression in factored form: \[(x+6)(x+7)\]

  • What do you get when you expand into standard form?
  • You get \(x^2+13x+42\)
  • Notice \(13\) is the sum of \(6\) plus \(7\).
  • Notice \(42\) is the product of \(6\) times \(7\).
  • This is not a coincidence

Another simple question

You are given a quadratic expression in factored form: \[(x-5)(x+9)\]

  • What do you get when you expand into standard form?
  • You get \(x^2+4x-45\)
  • Notice \(4\) is the sum of \(-5\) plus \(9\).
  • Notice \(-45\) is the product of \(-5\) times \(9\).
  • This is not a coincidence
