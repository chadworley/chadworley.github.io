Box method for multiplying two two-digit numbers

Consider the product \(52\cdot 43\).

  • We will evaluate with the box method.
  • First, break each factor into its tens and ones. \[52~=~ 50+2\] \[43~=~ 40+3\]
  • We can make a 2x2 box, putting the first factors terms as column labels and the second factors terms as row labels.
  • Fill each cell with the product of its column label and its row label.
  • Add up the results. \[2000+150+80+6\] \[2150+86\] \[2236\]

Combining like terms

  • Terms are expressions that are added (or subtracted) to something.
  • Factors are expressions that are multiplied (or divided) by something.
  • Terms can be composed of factors (and vice versa).
  • We will start with 3 types of terms:
  • Quadratic terms (a number times \(x^2\))
  • Linear terms (a number times \(x\))
  • Constant terms (just a number)
  • A common step when simplifying expressions is to combine like terms. For example imagine we are simplifying the following expression.\[4x+5+8x^2+7+9x-3x^2\]
  • We identify the quadratic terms are \(8x^2\) and \(-3x^2\).
  • We identify the linear terms are \(4x\) and \(9x\).
  • We identify the constants are \(5\) and \(7\).
  • Thus, when we combine like terms we get (and put in standard order of quadratic, linear, and then constant)
  • We get \[5x^2+13x+12\]
  • Notice combine means add the coefficients and coefficient is the numerical part of the term.

Combine-like-terms example 1

Consider the function below.

\[f(x)~=~ 6x^2 +8x+4+3x^2-x-2\]

When simplified, that function can be written in standard form:

\[f(x)~=~ ax^2+bx+c\]

where \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are integers. Find \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\).

  • Combine the quadratic terms.\[6x^2+3x^2~\equiv~ 9x^2\]
  • Combine the linear terms. Notice \(-x\) is equivalent to \(-1x\), but we do not write coefficients of 1 or -1. \[8x-x~\equiv~ 7x\]
  • Combine the constant terms.\[4-2~\equiv~ 2\]
  • Write the function. \[f(x)~=~ 9x^2+7x+2\]
  • Identify the parameters.\[a=9\] \[b=7\] \[c=2\]

Combine-like-terms example 2

Consider the function below.

\[f(x)~=~ 2+8+4x-5x-3+9x^2-x-8x^2+6x\]

When simplified, that function can be written in standard form:

\[f(x)~=~ ax^2+bx+c\]

where \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are integers. Find \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\).

  • Combine the quadratic terms.\[9x^2-8x^2+6x^2~\equiv~ 7x^2\]
  • Combine the linear terms. \[4x-5x-x~\equiv~ -2x\]
  • Combine the constant terms.\[2+8-3~\equiv~ 7\]
  • Write the function. \[f(x)~=~ 7x^2-2x+7\]
  • Identify the parameters.\[a=7\] \[b=-2\] \[c=7\]

Some simple examples of equivalent expressions

  • Simplify \(3x+4x\)
  • \(7x\)
  • Simplify \(x+4x\)
  • \(5x\)
  • Simplify \(3x^2+4x^2\)
  • \(7x^2\)
  • Simplify \(3x^2+4x\)
  • \(3x^2+4x\) Notice we can only combine like terms.
  • Simplify \((2)\cdot (x)\)
  • \(2x\)
  • Simplify \((x)\cdot(x)\)
  • \(x^2\)
  • Simplify \((3x)\cdot (5x)\)
  • \(15x^2\)
  • Distribute \(2\cdot (3x+5)\)
  • \(6x+10\)
  • Distribute \(x\cdot (3x+5)\)
  • \(3x^2+5x\)

Distributing a sum times a sum

  • What do I get when I distribute \((x+2)\cdot (3x+5)\)?
  • First, distribute \((x+2)\) to each term in \((3x+5)\).
    \[(x+2)\cdot 3x ~~~+~~~ (x+2)\cdot 5\]
  • Distribute \(3x\) to each term in its \((x+2)\) multiplier.
    \[3x^2+6x ~~~+~~~ (x+2)\cdot 5\]
  • Distribute \(5\) to each term in its \((x+2)\) multiplier.
    \[3x^2+6x ~~~+~~~ 5x+10\]
  • Notice we have two like terms.
  • Combine the like terms.
  • You get: \[3x^2+11x+10\]
  • When we get rid of parentheses and combine like terms, we call the process expansion. You can be asked to expand the expression.

Multiply linear binomials with box method

Expand the expression shown below.

\[(2x+3)\cdot (4x+5)\]

  • We can make a 2x2 box, putting the first factors terms as row labels and the second factors terms as column labels.
  • In each cell, write the product of the column label and row label
  • Write the terms. \[8x^2+10x+12x+15\]
  • Combine the two linear terms to get the answer: \[8x^2+22x+15\]
  • Notice, this is the same result we would get by following the method on the previous slide of repeatedly distributing.
