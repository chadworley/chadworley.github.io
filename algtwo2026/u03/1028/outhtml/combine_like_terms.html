  1. Question

    Consider the function below.

    \[f(x)~=~{-7}x+{2}x^{2}-{9}x^{2}+{4}+{5}x^{2}-{4}x+{6}x-{6}-1\]

    By combining like terms, we can create a simplified quadratic function in standard form.

    \[f(x)~=~ax^2+bx+c\]

    Find the values of \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\).

    • \(a=\)
    • \(b=\)
    • \(c=\)


    Solution

    Combine the quadratic terms. The quadratic coefficients are {2 , -9 , 5}, and their sum is -2.

    \[{2}x^{2}-{9}x^{2}+{5}x^{2}~\equiv~ -2x^2\]

    Combine the linear terms. The linear coefficients are {-7 , -4 , 6}, and their sum is -5.

    \[{-7}x-{4}x+{6}x~\equiv~ -5x\]

    Combine the constant terms. The constant coefficients are {4 , -6 , -1}, and their sum is -3.

    \[{4}-{6}-1~\equiv~ -3\]

    Thus, we can write the function in standard form.

    \[f(x)~=~{-2}x^{2}-{5}x-{3}\]

    We can now identify the values of \(a\) and \(b\) and \(c\).

    \[a=-2\] \[b=-5\] \[c=-3\]
