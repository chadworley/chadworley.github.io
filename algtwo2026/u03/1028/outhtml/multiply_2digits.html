  1. Question

    Let’s consider an alternative way to multiply 25 and 93.

    First, let’s break each number into its tens and ones.

    \[25 ~~=~~ 20+5\] \[93 ~~=~~ 90+3\]

    Now, find all 4 indicated products. Also, find the totals.

    * \(20\) \(5\) total
    \(90\)
    \(3\)
    total


    Solution

    \[25\cdot 93 = 2325\]

    * \(20\) \(5\) total
    \(90\) 1800 450 2250
    \(3\) 60 15 75
    total 1860 465 2325

    At some point I learned about the multiplication by lines method. You’ll notice there are 2 lines above 5 lines, both with positive slope. There are also 9 lines above 3 lines, both with negative slope. The number of intersections in the four corners are found with multiplication: \(2\cdot9=18\) and \(5\cdot9=45\) and \(2\cdot3=6\) and \(5\cdot3=15\).


    \[1800 + 450 + 60 + 15 ~~=~~ 2325\]
