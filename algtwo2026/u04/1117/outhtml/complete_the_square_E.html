  1. Question

    The not-drawn-to-scale figure below is composed of a blue square and a red rectangle. Each of the square’s lengths measures \(x\) feet. The rectangle is \(x\) feet tall and \(28\) feet wide.


    The total combined area, found by adding together the area of the square and the area of the rectangle, is \(429\) square feet.

    What value of \(x\) satisfies the conditions stated above?


    Solution

    Write the sum of areas in algebraic notation.

    \[x^2+28x~=~429\]

    To visualize “completing the square”, cut the rectangle in half, so divide 28 by two.

    \[\frac{28}{2}~=~14\]

    Place those two halves to make an L-shaped figure.


    Add some extra area to the picture to make a large square. In this case, we need to add a square with edge lengths of 14, adding 196 square feet to the area.


    \[x^2+28x+196~=~429+196\]

    \[x^2+28x+196~=~625\]

    But, notice, if you look at the last picture, we have another way to represent the total area. The newly formed large square has side lengths of \(x+14\). So we can represent the total area of the large square as \((x+14)^2\).

    \[(x+14)^2~=~625\]

    Undo the squaring on both sides. Use a calculator to find that \(\sqrt{625}=25\).

    \[x+14~=~\pm 25\]

    And, lastly, subtract 14 from both sides of the equation.

    \[x~=~-14 \pm 25\]

    We are only interesting in the positive value.

    \[x~=~-14 + 25\]

    \[x~=~11\]
