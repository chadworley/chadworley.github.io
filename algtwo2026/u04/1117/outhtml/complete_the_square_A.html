  1. Question

    A large square comprises a yellow square, two congruent blue rectangles, and a red square.


    The yellow square has an area of 121 little squares. The two blue rectangles have a combined area of 308 little squares.

    What is the area, in little squares, of the red square?


    Solution

    Find the edge length of the yellow square by using the square-root operator.

    \[x=\sqrt{121}\]

    \[x=11\]

    Find the area of each blue rectangle.

    \[B=\frac{308}{2}\] \[B=154\]

    Each blue rectangle has one length of 11 and another of length \(y\).

    \[xy~=~154\] \[11y~=~154\] \[y~=~14\]

    The red square has side lengths equal to \(y=14\). To find its area, square the side length.

    \[R=y^2\] \[R=14^2\] \[R=196\]

    The area of the red square is 196 little squares.
