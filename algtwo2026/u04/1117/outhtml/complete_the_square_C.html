  1. Question

    A wrap-around L-shaped patio will measure 14 feet along each wall. The patio will stick out \(x\) feet from the walls, and the total area will be 429 square feet. Note: you can assume all angles are right angles.


    What value of \(x\) satisfies the conditions stated above?


    Solution

    We can write an equation by saying the sum of \(x^2\) and \(14x\) and \(14x\) equals 429. These expressions represent the areas of a small square and two rectangles.


    \[x^2+14x+14x~=~429\]

    Combine like terms.

    \[x^2+28x~=~429\]

    There is a fun way to solve this. We will “complete the square”. In this situation, we imagine adding some extra area to the picture to make a large square. In this case, we need to add a square with edge lengths of 14, adding 196 square feet to the area.


    \[x^2+28x+196~=~429+196\]

    \[x^2+28x+196~=~625\]

    But, notice, if you look at the last picture, we have another way to represent the total area. The newly formed large square has side lengths of \(x+14\). So we can represent the total area of the large square as \((x+14)^2\).

    \[(x+14)^2~=~625\]

    Undo the squaring on both sides. Use a calculator to find that \(\sqrt{625}=25\).

    \[x+14~=~\pm 25\]

    And, lastly, subtract 14 from both sides of the equation.

    \[x~=~-14 \pm 25\]

    We are only interesting in the positive value.

    \[x~=~-14 + 25\]

    \[x~=~11\]
