  1. Question

    A large square comprises a yellow square, two congruent blue rectangles, and a red square.


    The yellow square has an area of \(x^2\) square units. The two blue rectangles have a combined area of \(28x\) square units.

    What is the area, in square units, of the red square?


    Solution

    Find the area of each blue rectangle.

    \[B=\frac{28x}{2}\] \[B=14x\]

    Each blue rectangle has one length of \(x\) and another of length \(14\).


    The red square has side lengths equal to \(y=14\). To find its area, square the side length.

    \[R=y^2\] \[R=14^2\] \[R=196\]

    The area of the red square is 196 square units.
