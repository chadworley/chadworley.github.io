  1. Question

    Find all solutions to the equation below:

    \[x^2~=~28\]


    1. \(x=\pm2\sqrt{2}\)
    2. \(x=\pm2\sqrt{3}\)
    3. \(x=\pm2\sqrt{5}\)
    4. \(x=\pm2\sqrt{7}\)
    5. \(x=\pm3\sqrt{2}\)
    6. \(x=\pm3\sqrt{3}\)
    7. \(x=\pm3\sqrt{5}\)
    8. \(x=\pm3\sqrt{7}\)
    9. \(x=\pm5\sqrt{2}\)
    10. \(x=\pm5\sqrt{3}\)
    11. \(x=\pm5\sqrt{5}\)
    12. \(x=\pm5\sqrt{7}\)
    13. \(x=\pm7\sqrt{2}\)
    14. \(x=\pm7\sqrt{3}\)
    15. \(x=\pm7\sqrt{5}\)
    16. \(x=\pm7\sqrt{7}\)

    Solution

    \[x^2~=~28\]

    \[x~=~\pm\sqrt{28}\]

    Find the prime factorization of 28. You can do this with a factor tree. You should find that \(28=2^{2}\cdot7^{1}\).

    \[x~=~\pm\sqrt{2^{2}\cdot7^{1}}\]

    Simplify the square root.

    \[x~=~\pm 2 \sqrt{7}\]
