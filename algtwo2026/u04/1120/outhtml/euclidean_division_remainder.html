  1. Question

    Acquaint yourself with the terminology and process of Euclidean division.

    If the dividend is 19 and the divisor is 6, then what is the remainder?


    Solution

    If 19 beads are grouped into piles of 6 beads, there will be 3 complete piles and 1 beads left over. In the terminology to Euclidean division:

    • The dividend = 19
    • The divisor = 6
    • The quotient = 3
    • The remainder = 1


    It might also help to express this with math notation:

    \[\frac{19}{6}~=~3+\frac{1}{6}\]

    You might also find this article on modular arithmetic interesting.
