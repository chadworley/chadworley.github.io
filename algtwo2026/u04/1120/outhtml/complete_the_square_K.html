    Solution

    The given equation:

    \[x^2-11x~=~242\]

    In order to make the left side of the equation a perfect square, we can add \(\left(\frac{-11}{2}\right)^2=\frac{121}{4}\) to both sides.

    \[x^2-11x+\frac{121}{4}~=~242+\frac{121}{4}\]

    On the right side of the equation, we need common denominators.

    \[x^2-11x+\frac{121}{4}~=~\frac{968}{4}+\frac{121}{4}\]

    \[x^2-11x+\frac{121}{4}~=~\frac{968+121}{4}\]

    \[x^2-11x+\frac{121}{4}~=~\frac{1089}{4}\]

    Factor the left side of the equation.

    \[\left(x-\frac{11}{2}\right)^2~=~\frac{1089}{4}\]

    Because the squaring function is not one-to-one, when we “undo” it in algebra, we need to consider the possibility of multiple solutions. In this case, squaring either \(\frac{33}{2}\) or \(\frac{-33}{2}\) produces \(\frac{1089}{4}\).

    \[x-\frac{11}{2}~=~\pm\frac{33}{2}\]

    \[x~=~\frac{11}{2}\pm\frac{33}{2}\]

    \[x~=~\frac{11\pm 33}{2}\]

    \[x~=~22~~~~~~~~\text{or}~~~~~~~~x~=~-11\]
