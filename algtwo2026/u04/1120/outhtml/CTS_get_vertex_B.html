  1. Question

    Quadratic polynomial \(p\) is defined below in standard form. \[p(x)~=~{2}x^{2}+{20}x+{58}\]

    By completing the square, you can rewrite the quadratic polynomial in vertex form:

    \[p(x) = a(x-h)^2+k\]

    where \((h,k)\) is the vertex of the parabola. Determine the coordinates of the vertex of quadratic polynomial \(p\).

    • \(h=\)
    • \(k=\)


    Solution

    We are given \(p(x)~=~{2}x^{2}+{20}x+{58}\). From the first two terms, factor out 2.

    \[p(x)~=~2\left(x^{2}+{10}x\right)+{58}\]

    Determine the constant needed to complete the square (of the expression inside parentheses) by dividing the linear coefficient by 2 and then squaring the result. \(\left(\frac{10}{2}\right)^2 ~=~ \left(5\right)^2 ~=~ 25\). Add and subtract this amount inside the parentheses.

    \[p(x)~=~2\left(x^{2}+{10}x+{25}-{25}\right)+{58}\]

    Notice the first three terms inside the parentheses are a perfect square. Rewrite them in factored form.

    \[p(x)~=~2\left(\left(x+{5}\right)^2-25\right)+{58}\]

    Distribute the 2.

    \[p(x)~=~2\left(x+{5}\right)^2-50+{58}\]

    Combine the constants.

    \[p(x)~=~2\left(x+{5}\right)^2 +{8}\]

    And so:

    \[h = -5\] \[k = 8\]
