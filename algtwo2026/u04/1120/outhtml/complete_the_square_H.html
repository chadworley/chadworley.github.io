    Solution

    The given equation:

    \[x^2-12x~=~45\]

    In order to make the left side of the equation a perfect square, we can add \(\left(\frac{-12}{2}\right)^2=36\) to both sides (where -12 is the linear coefficient).

    \[x^2-12x+36~=~45+36\]

    \[x^2-12x+36~=~81\]

    We can rewrite the left side in factored form. Notice that because the left side is a perfect square, we get a squared binomial.

    \[(x-6)^2~=~81\]

    Because the squaring function is not one-to-one, when we “undo” it in algebra, we need to consider the possibility of multiple solutions. In this case both 9 and -9 produce 81 when squared.

    \[x-6~=~\pm 9\]

    And, lastly, subtract -6 from both sides of the equation.

    \[x~=~6\pm9\]

    \[x~=~15~~~~~~~~\text{or}~~~~~~~~x~=~-3\]
