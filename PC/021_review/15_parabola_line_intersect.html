Question

The graph of a quadratic function is shown below.


When the graph of \(-x+y=-3\) is drawn on the same axes, one solution to this system is

Answerlist

  • (-2, -5)
  • (-4, -1)
  • (2, -1)
  • (1, -4)

Solution

When a linear equation is given in standard form, I typically graph by finding the intercepts: \((3, 0)\) and \((0, -3)\). Notice,

\[-(3)+(0)=-3\]

\[-(0)+(-3)=-3\]

To connect the two points on the line, we recognize it has a slope of 1. We draw the line, and look at the two points of intersection. The correct answer is (2, -1). The other intersection point is (-1, -4), but that was not an option.
