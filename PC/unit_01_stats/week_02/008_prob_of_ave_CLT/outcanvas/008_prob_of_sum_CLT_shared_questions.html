  1. Question

    Rolls from 8-sided dice have a population mean of \(\mu=4.5\) and population variance of \(\sigma^2 = 5.25\).

    If someone adds up \(n=60\) rolls (60d8), what is the probability of getting a total closer than 5.5 pips from the expected total?

    Note: I have made a continuity correction for you by using half-integer boundaries.


    Solution

    Calculate the expected total. \[\text{ET}=n\mu=270 \]

    Calculate the standard error (of the total).

    \[\text{SE} = \sigma\sqrt{n} \] \[\text{SE} = (2.2912878)(\sqrt{60})\]

    \[\text{SE} = 17.7482393\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\sum x-\text{ET}}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{5.5}{17.7482393}\] \[z = 0.3098899\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the central probability. \[P(|Z|<0.3099)=0.243 \]

    Solution
