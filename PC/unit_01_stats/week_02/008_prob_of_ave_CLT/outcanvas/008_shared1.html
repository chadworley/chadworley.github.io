  1. Question

    A population of lizards has a mean weight of \(\mu=23.75\) grams with a standard deviation of \(\sigma=19.63\) grams.

    If a sample of size \(n=35\) is taken, what is the probability the sample average is less than 25.08 grams?


    Solution

    Calculate the standard error (of the mean).

    \[\text{SE} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \] \[\text{SE} = \frac{19.63}{\sqrt{35}} = 3.3180756\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{1.33}{3.3180756}\] \[z = 0.4008347\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the leftward probability. \[P(Z<0.4008)=0.656 \]

  5. Question

    Rolls from 8-sided dice have a population mean of \(\mu=4.5\) and population variance of \(\sigma^2 = 5.25\).

    If someone adds up \(n=47\) rolls (47d8), what is the probability of getting a total further than 11 pips from the expected total?

    Note: I have made a continuity correction for you by using half-integer boundaries.


    Solution

    Calculate the expected total. \[\text{ET}=n\mu=211.5 \]

    Calculate the standard error (of the total).

    \[\text{SE} = \sigma\sqrt{n} \] \[\text{SE} = (2.2912878)(\sqrt{47})\]

    \[\text{SE} = 15.7082781\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\sum x-\text{ET}}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{11}{15.7082781}\] \[z = 0.7002677\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the two-tail probability. \[P(|Z|>0.7003)=0.484 \]
