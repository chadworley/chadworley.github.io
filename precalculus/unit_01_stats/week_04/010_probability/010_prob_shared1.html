  1. Question

    In a deck of strange cards, there are 477 cards. Each card has an image and a color.


    1. What is the probability a random card is a flower?
    2. What is the probability a random card is violet?
    3. What is the probability a random card is both a flower and violet?
    4. What is the probability a random card is either a wheel or violet (or both)?
    5. What is the probability a random card is violet given it is a wheel?
    6. What is the probability a random card is a wheel given it is blue?
    7. Between a flower and a quilt, which is more likely to be a indigo?


    Solution

    video

    The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.

    1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower}]=\frac{157}{477}=0.33\)
    2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{violet}]=\frac{85}{477}=0.18\)
    3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower and violet}]=\frac{24}{477}=0.05\)
    4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{wheel or violet}]=\frac{170+85-32}{477}=0.47\)
    5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{violet given wheel}]=\frac{32}{170}=0.19\)
    6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{wheel given blue}]=\frac{36}{92}=0.39\)
    7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{indigo given flower}] = 0.29\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{indigo given quilt}] = 0.13\), so a flower is more likely to be indigo than a quilt is.

    Solution

    (ANS=0.6771654)

    video

    Use the definition of conditional probability. \[P(\text{win given happy}) = \frac{P(\text{win and happy})}{P(\text{happy})}\] We can first determine all the joint probabilities. \[P(A\text{ and }B) = P(A)\cdot P(B\text{ given } A) \] \[\begin{aligned} P(\text{win and happy}) &= 0.5\times0.86 = 0.43\\ P(\text{win and sad}) &= 0.5\times(1-0.86) = 0.07\\ P(\text{lose and happy}) &= (1-0.5)\times0.41 = 0.205\\ P(\text{lose and sad}) &= (1-0.5)\times(1-0.41) = 0.295\\ \end{aligned}\]

    Notice there are two disjoint ways Bob could be happy. \[\begin{aligned} P(\text{happy}) &= P\left(\text{[win and happy] OR [lose and happy]}\right) \\ &= P(\text{win and happy}) + P(\text{lose and happy}) \\ &= 0.43+0.205 \\ &= 0.635 \end{aligned}\] So, back to the conditional probability. \[P(\text{win given happy}) = \frac{0.43}{0.635} = 0.677\]
