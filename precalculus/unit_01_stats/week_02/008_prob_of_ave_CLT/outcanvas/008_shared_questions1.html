  1. Question

    A population of lizards has a mean weight of \(\mu=96.58\) grams with a standard deviation of \(\sigma=18.41\) grams.

    If a sample of size \(n=25\) is taken, what is the probability the sample average is less than 99.89 grams?


    Solution

    Calculate the standard error (of the mean).

    \[\text{SE} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \] \[\text{SE} = \frac{18.41}{\sqrt{25}} = 3.682\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{3.31}{3.682}\] \[z = 0.898968\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the leftward probability. \[P(Z<0.899)=0.816 \]


  2. Question

    A population of lizards has a mean weight of \(\mu=23.87\) grams with a standard deviation of \(\sigma=11.65\) grams.

    If a sample of size \(n=39\) is taken, what is the probability the sample average is further than 3.54 grams from \(\mu\)?


    Solution

    Calculate the standard error (of the mean).

    \[\text{SE} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \] \[\text{SE} = \frac{11.65}{\sqrt{39}} = 1.865493\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{3.54}{1.865493}\] \[z = 1.8976217\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the two-tail probability. \[P(|Z|>1.8976)=0.058 \]


  3. Question

    Solution

    Calculate the standard error (of the mean).

    \[\text{SE} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \] \[\text{SE} = \frac{14.23}{\sqrt{32}} = 2.5155324\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{2.77}{2.5155324}\] \[z = 1.1011586\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the central probability. \[P(|Z|<1.1012)=0.729 \]
