  1. Question

    A population of lizards has a mean weight of \(\mu=65.22\) grams with a standard deviation of \(\sigma=11.47\) grams.

    If a sample of size \(n=56\) is taken, what is the probability the sample average is closer than 0.31 grams from \(\mu\)?


    Solution

    Calculate the standard error (of the mean).

    \[\text{SE} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \] \[\text{SE} = \frac{11.47}{\sqrt{56}} = 1.5327432\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{0.31}{1.5327432}\] \[z = 0.2022518\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the central probability. \[P(|Z|<0.2023)=0.16 \]

  5. Question

    Rolls from 4-sided dice have a population mean of \(\mu=2.5\) and population variance of \(\sigma^2 = 1.25\).

    If someone adds up \(n=35\) rolls (35d4), what is the probability of getting a total more than 85.5 pips?

    Note: I have made a continuity correction for you by using half-integer boundaries.


    Solution

    Calculate the expected total. \[\text{ET}=n\mu=87.5 \]

    Calculate the standard error (of the total).

    \[\text{SE} = \sigma\sqrt{n} \] \[\text{SE} = (1.118034)(\sqrt{35})\]

    \[\text{SE} = 6.6143783\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\sum x-\text{ET}}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{-2}{6.6143783}\] \[z = -0.3023716\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the rightward probability. \[P(Z>-0.3024)=0.619 \]
