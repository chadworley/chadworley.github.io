    A population of lizards has a mean weight of \(\mu=67.44\) grams with a standard deviation of \(\sigma=17.26\) grams.

    If a sample of size \(n=71\) is taken, what is the probability the sample average is less than 67.03 grams?


    Solution

    Calculate the standard error (of the mean).

    \[\text{SE} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \] \[\text{SE} = \frac{17.26}{\sqrt{71}} = 2.0483851\]

    Calculate the \(z\) score.

    \[z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\text{SE}}\]

    \[z = \frac{-0.41}{2.0483851}\] \[z = -0.2001577\]

    Use the standard normal probability calculator to evaluate the leftward probability. \[P(Z<-0.2002)=0.421 \]
