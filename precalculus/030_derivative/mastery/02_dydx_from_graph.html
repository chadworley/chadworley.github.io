Question

The graph of \(y=f(x)\) is shown below. Also, at \(x=3\), a tangent line is drawn.


The tangent line can be used to determine the instantaneous rate of change of \(f(x)\) at \(x=3\).

Please estimate \(f'(3)\) within the nearest hundredth. This could also be notated as \(\left.\frac{df(x)}{dx}\right|_{x=3}\) or as \(\frac{df}{dx}(3)\). For more, see wikipedias article on notation for differentiation.

Solution

Notice the tangent line intersects \(f(x)\) at point \((3,2)\). From there, follow the red line until you get to another point with integer coordinates.


The slope of the tangent line is found with rise over run.

\[m ~=~ \frac{3}{2}\]

\[m ~=~ 1.5\]

So, the equation of the tangent line in point-slope form is \(y=1.5(x-(3))+(2)\). More importantly for this question, the slope of the tangent line equals the instantaneous rate of change of the function, which equals the derivative at \(x=3\). In other words, \[f'(3) ~=~ 1.5\]

\[\left.\frac{df(x)}{dx}\right|_{x=3} ~=~ 1.5\]

\[\frac{df}{dx}(3) ~=~ 1.5\]
