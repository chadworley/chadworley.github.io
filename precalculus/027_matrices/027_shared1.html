  1. Question

    In Calculus, when integrating rational expressions, it is often helpful to perform partial-fraction decomposition. The simplest version of partial-fraction decomposition occurs when there are two distinct linear factors.

    The procedure can be exemplified by trying to determine constants \(A\) and \(B\) to satisfy the equation below for all values of \(x\) (with restrictions \(x\ne -7\) and \(x\ne 2\)).

    \[\frac{3x-33}{(x+7)(x-2)} = \frac{A}{x+7}+\frac{B}{x-2}\]

    • \(A=\)
    • \(B=\)


    Solution

    We are trying to find \(A\) and \(B\), to make both sides of the equation equivalent expressions.

    \[\frac{3x-33}{(x+7)(x-2)} ~=~ \frac{A}{x+7}+\frac{B}{x-2}\]

    Multiply both sides by \((x+7)(x-2)\).

    \[\frac{3x-33}{(x+7)(x-2)} \cdot (x+7)(x-2) ~=~ \left(\frac{A}{x+7}+\frac{B}{x-2}\right) \cdot (x+7)(x-2)\]

    Simplify.

    \[3x-33 = A(x-2)+B(x+7)\]

    Expand the right side of the equation. Gather the linear coefficient and the constant.

    \[3x-33 = (A+B)x+(-2A+7B)\] The equation must be true FOR ALL values of \(x\). This means the linear coefficients must be equal, and the constants must be equal.

    \[\begin{align} 3 &= A + B \\ -33 &= -2A+7B\\ \end{align}\]

    Use elimination. Multiply the first equation by 2.

    \[\begin{align} 6 &= 2A+2B \\ \oplus~~~~ -33 &= -2A+7B\\ \hline -27 &= 9B \end{align}\]

    Divide both sides by 9.

    \[-3 = B\]

    Plug that value into \(3 = A + B\).

    \[3 = A-3\]

    Add 3 to both sides.

    \[6 = A\]

  5. Question

    In Calculus, when integrating rational expressions, it is often helpful to perform partial-fraction decomposition. One version of partial-fraction decomposition occurs when there is a squared linear factor.

    The procedure can be exemplified by trying to determine constants \(A\) and \(B\) to satisfy the equation below for all values of \(x\) (with restriction \(x\ne 3\)). It should be said that knowing/remembering how to set up the equation below is the hardest part of partial-fraction decomposition.

    \[\frac{5x-17}{(x-3)^2} ~=~ \frac{A}{(x-3)}+\frac{B}{(x-3)^2}\]

    • \(A=\)
    • \(B=\)


    Solution

    \[\frac{5x-17}{(x-3)^2} ~=~ \frac{A}{(x-3)}+\frac{B}{(x-3)^2}\]

    Multiply both sides by \((x-3)^2\).

    \[5x-17 ~=~ A(x-3)+B\]

    Expand. Recognize the linear coefficient and the constant.

    \[5x-17 ~=~ Ax+(-3A+B)\]

    The corresponding coefficients must be equal for the equation to be true for all \(x\).

    \[\begin{align} 5 ~&=~ A \\ -17 ~&=~ -3A+B \end{align}\]

    We already have \(A\). Substitute to get \(B\). \[-17 ~=~ -3(5)+B\]

    Multiply -3 and 5.

    \[-17 ~=~ -15+B\]

    Add \(15\) to both sides.

    \[-2 ~=~ B\]

    • \(x=\)
    • \(y=\)
    • \(z=\)


    Solution

    From OpenStax:

    Given a linear system of three equations, solve for three unknowns.

    • Pick any pair of equations and eliminate one variable.
    • Pick another pair of equations and eliminate the same variable.
    • You have created a system of two equations in two unknowns. Solve the resulting two-by-two system.

  17. Question

    In Calculus, when integrating rational expressions, it is often helpful to perform partial-fraction decomposition.

    The procedure can be exemplified by trying to determine constants \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) to satisfy the equation below for all values of \(x\).

    \[\frac{-13x^2-14x+3}{(x+3)(x-5)(x+2)} = \frac{A}{x+3}+\frac{B}{x-5}+\frac{C}{x+2}\]

    • \(A=\)
    • \(B=\)
    • \(C=\)


    Solution

    We start with the given equivalence: \[\frac{-13x^2-14x+3}{(x+3)(x-5)(x+2)} = \frac{A}{x+3}+\frac{B}{x-5}+\frac{C}{x+2}\]

    Multiply both sides by \((x+3)(x-5)(x+2)\).

    \[-13x^2-14x+3 ~~=~~ A(x-5)(x+2)+B(x+3)(x+2)+C(x+3)(x-5)\]

    Expand (by FOIL and distributing).

    \[-13x^2-14x+3 ~~=~~ Ax^2-3Ax-10A+Bx^2+5Bx+6B+Cx^2-2Cx-15C\]

    Gather similar terms (with same degree of \(x\)) to identify the coefficients.

    \[(-13)x^2+(-14)x+(3) ~~=~~ (A+B+C)x^2+(-3A+5B-2C)x+(-10A+6B-15C)\] Corresponding coefficients must be equal for the above equivalence to hold for all \(x\).

    \[\begin{align} -13&=A+B+C\\ -14&=-3A+5B-2C\\ 3&=-10A+6B-15C \end{align}\]

    Solve the 3x3 system. You can use elimination, or you can use SageMath.

    var("A B C")
eq1 = -13==A+B+C
eq2 = -14==-3*A+5*B-2*C
eq3 = 3==-10*A+6*B-15*C
solve([eq1,eq2,eq3],[A,B,C])

    \[\begin{align} A&=-9\\ B&=-7\\ C&=3 \end{align}\]

    And, really, SageMath can do the entire partial-fraction decomposition by using sympy’s apart function.

    var("x")
nmrtr = -13*x^2-14*x+3
dnmntr = (x+3)*(x-5)*(x+2)
exp = nmrtr/dnmntr
from sympy import apart
apart(exp)

    \[\begin{align} A&=-7\\ B&=-3\\ C&=5 \end{align}\]

    And, really, SageMath can do the entire partial-fraction decomposition by using sympy’s apart function.

    var("x")
nmrtr = -5*x^2-14*x+12
dnmntr = (x+6)*(x+3)*(x+2)
exp = nmrtr/dnmntr
from sympy import apart
apart(exp)


  20. Question

    In Calculus, when integrating rational expressions, it is often helpful to perform partial-fraction decomposition.

    The procedure can be exemplified by trying to determine constants \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) to satisfy the equation below for all values of \(x\).

    \[\frac{-7x^2-9x+18}{(x-2)(x-3)(x+5)} = \frac{A}{x-2}+\frac{B}{x-3}+\frac{C}{x+5}\]

    • \(A=\)
    • \(B=\)
    • \(C=\)


    Solution

    We start with the given equivalence: \[\frac{-7x^2-9x+18}{(x-2)(x-3)(x+5)} = \frac{A}{x-2}+\frac{B}{x-3}+\frac{C}{x+5}\]

    Multiply both sides by \((x-2)(x-3)(x+5)\).

    \[-7x^2-9x+18 ~~=~~ A(x-3)(x+5)+B(x-2)(x+5)+C(x-2)(x-3)\]

    Expand (by FOIL and distributing).

    \[-7x^2-9x+18 ~~=~~ Ax^2+2Ax-15A+Bx^2+3Bx-10B+Cx^2-5Cx+6C\]

    Gather similar terms (with same degree of \(x\)) to identify the coefficients.

    \[(-7)x^2+(-9)x+(18) ~~=~~ (A+B+C)x^2+(2A+3B-5C)x+(-15A-10B+6C)\] Corresponding coefficients must be equal for the above equivalence to hold for all \(x\).

    \[\begin{align} -7&=A+B+C\\ -9&=2A+3B-5C\\ 18&=-15A-10B+6C \end{align}\]

    Solve the 3x3 system. You can use elimination, or you can use SageMath.

    var("A B C")
eq1 = -7==A+B+C
eq2 = -9==2*A+3*B-5*C
eq3 = 18==-15*A-10*B+6*C
solve([eq1,eq2,eq3],[A,B,C])

    \[\begin{align} A&=4\\ B&=-9\\ C&=-2 \end{align}\]

    And, really, SageMath can do the entire partial-fraction decomposition by using sympy’s apart function.

    var("x")
nmrtr = -7*x^2-9*x+18
dnmntr = (x-2)*(x-3)*(x+5)
exp = nmrtr/dnmntr
from sympy import apart
apart(exp)
