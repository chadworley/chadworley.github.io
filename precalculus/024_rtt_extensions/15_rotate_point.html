Question

A red triangle has coordinates \(A(-0.04, 7.03)\), \(B(0.14, 9.1)\), and \(C(2.65, 5.6)\). The triangle is rotated 3.45 radians clockwise around the origin to produce a blue triangle \(A'B'C'\).


Find the \(x\) coordinate of point B. The tolerance is \(\pm0.01\).

Solution

Determine the polar coordinates of the original point \(B(0.14, 9.1)\).

\[r_{B} = \sqrt{(0.14)^2+(9.1)^2}\]

\[r_{B} = 9.1010769\]

Point \(B\) is in quadrant \(\mathrm{I}\). In quadrant \(\mathrm{I}\) or \(\mathrm{IV}\), the arctan function will provide a correct angle. For quadrant \(\mathrm{II}\) or \(\mathrm{III}\), add \(\pi\) to get a correct angle.

\[\theta_{B} = \arctan\left(\frac{9.1}{0.14}\right)\]

\[\theta_{B} = 1.5554129\]

The new point, \(B'\), will have the same distance from the origin, but its angle will be changed by subtracting 3.45 radians.

\[r_{B'} = 9.1010769\]

\[\theta_{B'} ~=~ \theta_{B}-3.45 ~=~ -1.8945871\]

Calculate the Cartesian (rectangular) coordinates of point B.

\[x_{B'} ~=~ 9.1010769 \cdot \cos(-1.8945871) ~=~ -2.8956223\] \[y_{B'} ~=~ 9.1010769 \cdot \sin(-1.8945871) ~=~ -8.6281499\]

The answer, rounded to the nearest hundredth, is -2.9.
