Question

A red triangle has coordinates \(A(-2.73, -5.57)\), \(B(-3.47, -6.36)\), and \(C(-2.9, -8.76)\). The triangle is rotated 2.79 radians counterclockwise around the origin to produce a blue triangle \(A'B'C'\).


Find the \(x\) coordinate of point B’. The tolerance is \(\pm0.01\).

Solution

Determine the polar coordinates of the original point \(B(-3.47, -6.36)\).

\[r_{B} = \sqrt{(-3.47)^2+(-6.36)^2}\]

\[r_{B} = 7.2450328\]

Point \(B\) is in quadrant \(\mathrm{III}\). In quadrant \(\mathrm{I}\) or \(\mathrm{IV}\), the arctan function will provide a correct angle. For quadrant \(\mathrm{II}\) or \(\mathrm{III}\), add \(\pi\) to get a correct angle. (Actually, Desmos allows \(y\) and \(x\) to be given as separate arguments to get a correct angle in any quadrant: arctan(-6.36,-3.47).)

\[\theta_{B} = \arctan\left(\frac{-6.36}{-3.47}\right)+\pi\]

\[\theta_{B} = 4.2129321\]

The new point, \(B'\), will have the same distance from the origin, but its angle will be changed by adding 2.79 radians.

\[r_{B'} = 7.2450328\]

\[\theta_{B'} ~=~ \theta_{B}+2.79 ~=~ 7.0029321\]

Calculate the Cartesian (rectangular) coordinates of point B’.

\[x_{B'} ~=~ 7.2450328 \cdot \cos(7.0029321) ~=~ 5.4480666\] \[y_{B'} ~=~ 7.2450328 \cdot \sin(7.0029321) ~=~ 4.7758842\]

The answer, rounded to the nearest hundredth, is 5.45.
