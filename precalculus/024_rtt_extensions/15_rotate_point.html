Question

A red triangle has coordinates \(A(2.16, -5.19)\), \(B(0.43, -6.41)\), and \(C(1.87, -4.05)\). The triangle is rotated 3.51 radians counterclockwise around the origin to produce a blue triangle \(A'B'C'\).


Find the \(y\) coordinate of point A’. The tolerance is \(\pm0.01\).

Solution

Determine the polar coordinates of the original point \(A(2.16, -5.19)\).

\[r_{A} = \sqrt{(2.16)^2+(-5.19)^2}\]

\[r_{A} = 5.6215389\]

Point \(A\) is in quadrant \(\mathrm{IV}\). In quadrant \(\mathrm{I}\) or \(\mathrm{IV}\), the arctan function will provide a correct angle. For quadrant \(\mathrm{II}\) or \(\mathrm{III}\), add \(\pi\) to get a correct angle. (Actually, Desmos allows \(y\) and \(x\) to be given as separate arguments to get a correct angle in any quadrant arctan(-5.19,2.16).)

\[\theta_{A} = \arctan\left(\frac{-5.19}{2.16}\right)\]

\[\theta_{A} = -1.1764157\]

The new point, \(A'\), will have the same distance from the origin, but its angle will be changed by adding 3.51 radians.

\[r_{A'} = 5.6215389\]

\[\theta_{A'} ~=~ \theta_{A}+3.51 ~=~ 2.3335843\]

Calculate the Cartesian (rectangular) coordinates of point A’.

\[x_{A'} ~=~ 5.6215389 \cdot \cos(2.3335843) ~=~ -3.8841437\] \[y_{A'} ~=~ 5.6215389 \cdot \sin(2.3335843) ~=~ 4.0638808\]

The answer, rounded to the nearest hundredth, is 4.06.
