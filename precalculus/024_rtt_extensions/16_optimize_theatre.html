Question

A moviegoer enters a theater with a 28.8-foot screen with a bottom edge 5.8 feet above eye level of chairs on flat ground. How far (horizontally) should the moviegoer sit from the screen to maximize their viewing angle (\(\theta\))?


(Above figure not drawn to scale.)

Determine the value of \(x\) (in feet) that makes \(\theta\) as large as possible. The tolerance is \(\pm0.01\) feet.

Solution

Start by labeling another angle and finding the total height (\(5.8+28.8\)) is 34.6 feet.


Write a system of equations.

\[\tan(\phi) ~=~ \frac{5.8}{x}\]

\[\tan(\phi+\theta) ~=~ \frac{34.6}{x}\]

Take the arctan of both sides of both equations.

\[\phi ~=~ \arctan\left(\frac{5.8}{x}\right)\]

\[\phi+\theta ~=~ \arctan\left(\frac{34.6}{x}\right)\]

Eliminate \(\phi\) by substituting.

\[\arctan\left(\frac{5.8}{x}\right)+\theta ~=~ \arctan\left(\frac{34.6}{x}\right)\]

Get \(\theta\) as a function of \(x\).

\[\theta ~=~ \arctan\left(\frac{34.6}{x}\right)-\arctan\left(\frac{5.8}{x}\right)\]

Plot the function (in Desmos or other graphing utility). Desmos will prefer if you use function notation.

\[\theta(x) ~=~ \arctan\left(\frac{34.6}{x}\right)-\arctan\left(\frac{5.8}{x}\right)\]

Find the maximum of the function.


So, choosing to sit \(x=14.1661569\) feet away will maximize the viewing angle.
