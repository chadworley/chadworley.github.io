  1. Question

    A geometric sequence has a first term 0.7 and a common ratio 1.1. Find the sum of the first 21 terms.

    The tolerance is \(\pm 0.01\).

    (We call the sum of a geometric sequence a geometric series.)


    Solution

    It might be helpful to reference geometric series. Identify the parameters.

    \[a = 0.7\]

    \[r = 1.1\]

    Define the explicit formula to find the \(n\)th term.

    \[a_n = (0.7)(1.1)^{(n-1)}\]

    The sum of the first 21 terms could be donated with \(S_{21}\) or with the upper-case Sigma summation operator.

    \[S_{21} = \sum_{i=1}^{21}(0.7)(1.1)^{(i-1)} \]

    A constant factor (multiplier) can be brought in front of a summation operator.

    \[S_{21} = 0.7\sum_{i=1}^{21}(1.1)^{(i-1)} \]

    Just to be clear, we are attempting to find the following sum:

    \[S_{21} = (0.7)\left(1+1.1+1.1^2+1.1^3+\ldots+1.1^{17}+1.1^{18}+1.1^{19}+1.1^{20}\right) \]

    If you are cool, you might multiply both sides by 1.1, and distribute to all the terms in the summation.

    \[1.1\cdot S_{21} = (0.7)\left(1.1+1.1^2+1.1^3+1.1^4+\ldots+1.1^{18}+1.1^{19}+1.1^{20}+1.1^{21}\right) \]

    Subtract the previous two equations and simplify by eliminating the opposite terms.

    \[S_{21} - 1.1\cdot S_{21} = (0.7)(1-1.1^{21})\]

    Factor out \(S_{21}\) from the terms on the left side of the equation.

    \[S_{21}(1-1.1)= (0.7)(1-1.1^{21})\]

    Divide both sides by \((1-1.1)\).

    \[S_{21}= \frac{(0.7)(1-1.1^{21})}{(1-1.1)}\]

    This process suggests the geometric series formula.

    \[S_n = \frac{a(1-r^n)}{1-r} \]

    \[S_{21}= 44.8017496\]

  11. Question

    An infinite geometric series converges if and only if \(|r|<1\). A convergent series will approach a finite value as more terms are added; a divergent series will approach infinity. Is the following geometric series convergent?

    \[60~-~48~+~38.4~-~\ldots\]


    1. Yes, the series converges.
    2. No, the series diverges.

    Solution

    Determine the common ratio by dividing the second term by the first term.

    \[r = \frac{-48}{60} = -0.8\]

    Notice, this is the same as the ratio of the third and second terms.

    \[r ~=~ \frac{38.4}{-48} ~=~ -0.8\]

    Determine the absolute value of the common ratio.

    \[|r| ~=~ |-0.8| ~=~ 0.8\]

    Compare the absolute common ratio to 1. If it is less than 1, the series converges; otherwise, the series diverges.

    \[|-0.8| < 1\]

    So, the series converges.
