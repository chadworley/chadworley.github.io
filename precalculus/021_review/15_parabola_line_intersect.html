Question

The graph of a quadratic function is shown below.


When the graph of \(x-y=-1\) is drawn on the same axes, one solution to this system is

Answerlist

  • (-3, -4)
  • (-6, -5)
  • (-7, -6)
  • (-5, -6)

Solution

When a linear equation is given in standard form, I typically graph by finding the intercepts: \((-1, 0)\) and \((0, 1)\). Notice,

\[(-1)-(0)=-1\]

\[(0)-(1)=-1\]

To connect the two points on the line, we recognize it has a slope of 1. We draw the line, and look at the two points of intersection. The correct answer is (-7, -6). The other intersection point is (-4, -3), but that was not an option.
