  1. Question

    Let functions \(f\) and \(g\) be defined below: \[f(x) = x^3+x^2\] \[g(x)=\frac{1}{x}-x\]

    Match the following functions to a composition:

    \[\begin{align} a(x) &= \left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right)^3+\left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right)^2 \\\\ b(x) &= \frac{1}{\left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right)}-\left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right) \\\\ c(x) &= \frac{1}{\left(x^3+x^2\right)}-\left(x^3+x^2\right) \\\\ d(x) &= \left(x^3+x^2\right)^3+\left(x^3+x^2\right)^2 \end{align}\]

    Your answers here:

    • Match from above: \(f(g(x)) =\)
    • Match from above: \(g(f(x)) =\)
    • Match from above: \(f(f(x)) =\)
    • Match from above: \(g(g(x)) =\)


    Solution

    \[\begin{align} f(g(x)) &= \left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right)^3+\left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right)^2 = a(x)\\\\ g(f(x)) &= \frac{1}{\left(x^3+x^2\right)}-\left(x^3+x^2\right) = c(x)\\\\ f(f(x)) &= \left(x^3+x^2\right)^3+\left(x^3+x^2\right)^2 = d(x)\\\\ g(g(x)) &= \frac{1}{\left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right)}-\left(\frac{1}{x}-x\right) = b(x) \end{align}\]
