Question

\[4x^2+40x-9y^2-108y-260=0\]

With some algebra (completing the square), you can convert the equation into standard form:

\[\frac{(x-h)^2}{a^2}-\frac{(y-k)^2}{b^2}=1\]

From the given polynomial form, determine the 4 parameters.

  • \(h=\) ##ANSWER1##
  • \(k=\) ##ANSWER2##
  • \(r_1=\) ##ANSWER3##
  • \(r_2=\) ##ANSWER4##

Solution

\[4x^2+40x-9y^2-108y-260=0\] Factor out the quadratic coefficients from each variables polynomial. Also move the constant term to the right with addition/subtration.

\[4(x^2+10x)-9(y^2+12y)=260\]

Complete the square for the \(x\) quadratic. If \(x^2+bx+c\) is a perfect square, then \(c=\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2\). In this case, the linear coefficient is \(10\). If you divide it by 2 and square the result, you get the necessary constant to complete the square (25). Notice \((x+5)^2\equiv x^2+10x+25\). Lastly, since we are adding 25 inside parentheses with a multiplier of 4, we are technically adding 100 to both sides to make an equivalent equation.

\[4(x^2+10x+25)-9(y^2+12y)=260+100\]

Complete the square for the \(y\) quadratic. If \(y^2+by+c\) is a perfect square, then \(c=\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2\). In this case, the linear coefficient is \(12\). If you divide it by 2 and square the result, you get the necessary constant to complete the square (36). Notice \((y+6)^2\equiv y^2+12x+36\). Lastly, since we are adding 36 inside parentheses with a multiplier of -9, we are technically adding -324 to both sides to make an equivalent equation.

\[4(x^2+10x+25)-9(y^2+12y+36)=260+100-324\]

Combine the constants.

\[4(x^2+10x+25)-9(y^2+12y+36)=36\]

O yeah, remember how you completed the square? Twice? Lets rewrite those quadratic expressions in factored form. (This could have been done earlier.)

\[4(x+5)^2-9(y+6)^2=36\]

Divide both sides by \(36\).

\[\frac{4(x+5)^2}{36}-\frac{9(y+6)^2}{36}=1\]

Simplify the fractions. Notice that \(\frac{4}{36}= \frac{1}{9}\) and \(\frac{9}{36}= \frac{1}{4}\).

\[\frac{(x+5)^2}{9}-\frac{(y+6)^2}{4}=1\]

If you find the square roots of both denominators, you will have the standard form of a horizontal hyperbola. Notice \(\sqrt{9}=3\) and \(\sqrt{4}=2\).

\[\frac{(x+5)^2}{3^2}-\frac{(y+6)^2}{2^2}=1\]

Thus,

\[\begin{align} h &= -5 \\ k &= -6 \\ a &= 3 \\ b &= 2 \end{align}\]
