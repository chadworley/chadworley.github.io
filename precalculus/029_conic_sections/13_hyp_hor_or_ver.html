Solution

The graphs should be pretty easy. )( is horizontal and \(\asymp\) is vertical.

The equations can be a bit tricky, because the order of the fractions is randomized. Pay attention to which fraction (containing \(x\) or \(y\)) has the negative sign.

Personally, I think about \(x\) and \(y\) values that make the numerators 0. Then I think if there could be any solutions. The key idea is that there are no real \(n\) and \(d\) such that \(-\frac{n^2}{d^2}=1\). If there are \(x\) values that have no solution (no corresponding \(y\) to make the equation true), then there are vertical lines that do not hit the hyperbola, meaning the hyperbola must be horizontal. If there are \(y\) values that have no solution, then there are horizontal lines that do not hit the hyperbola, meaning the hyperbola must be vertical. I know that is convoluted, but Ive yet to find a more robust way to store this in my long-term memory.

  • Representation \(A\) is of a vertical hyperbola.
  • Representation \(B\) is of a horizontal hyperbola.
  • Representation \(C\) is of a horizontal hyperbola.
  • Representation \(D\) is of a vertical hyperbola.
  • Representation \(E\) is of a vertical hyperbola.
  • Representation \(F\) is of a horizontal hyperbola.
